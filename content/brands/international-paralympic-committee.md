### 2017

**Logo Information:**

- The Isolation area for emblems: Isolation area for integrated designs is provided, with the template in EPS format.
- Organising Committee for the Olympic and Paralympic Games (OCOG) emblems must incorporate the Paralympic symbol and distinct elements identifying the event. The area covered by the Paralympic symbol must be one-third of the total area.
- National Paralympic Committee (NPC) emblems must incorporate the Paralympic symbol, the official organizational name, and any native-language representation. Unique national symbols or distinctive elements are required in designs.
- Minimum height requirement for emblems used by Regional Paralympic Committees, such as the Americas, European, or African Paralympic Committees: 10.5mm.
- World Para Sport logos include combined logos with the wordmark and pictogram within a pod. Monochrome versions and minimum usage size specified as height of 32mm.
- World Para Sport wordmarks require a minimum isolation area of at least one grid unit around the wordmark and a minimum size height of 7.5mm or 20px.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary font is Trade Gothic Next LT Pro for all printed materials (Regular and Bold weights). For digital use such as PowerPoint and emails, Calibri is used.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photos should focus on the athlete as the central figure, showcasing their impairment confidently and equally representing genders, nationalities, and impairment types.
- Athletes in images should appear triumphant, dynamic, and international, appealing to courage, progressiveness, and diversity.
- Editorial misuse includes cropping out impairments and displaying athletes in an unflattering light.
- Artistic misuse includes mirroring photos such that text or symbols appear reversed, and scaling photos disproportionately.

**Brand Voice:**

- Tone of voice is high impact, inspirational, yet assertive and understanding. It is clear, straightforward, and supportive in communication.
- The motto 'Spirit in Motion' captures the Paralympic Movement's attempt to inspire the world with athlete performances by exceeding limits.

**Notes:**

- Approvals from the IPC are required for the use of emblems, logos, and symbols. Specific rules for usage include the IPC Handbook and the Technical Manual on Brand, Identity & Look of the Games.
- All photo materials draw credit from Getty Images, IPC, IWAS, and other sources listed in the guidelines.
