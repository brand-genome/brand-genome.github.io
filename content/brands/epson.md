## Brand Guidelines

### 2010

**Color Information:**

- Gradients: #f8e200 to #f19a0a to #f9cc15.
- Gradients: #3c93d4 to #53bae5 to #aedbf5.
- Gradients: #00b0ad to #00d1cf to #7ed27a.
- Gradients: #628eb3 to #98b6d2 to #e1eef6.
- Gradients: #d56f0a to #f48b15 to #f6c046.
- Gradients: #380a0a to #8f2d2c to #af5453.
- Background gradients and analogous color usage for creating a colorful atmosphere: #285364 to #63a3c7 to #74aed3.
- Gradient colors for banners: #1f3948 to #3d768a to #76b4c3.

**Typography Information:**

- Headline/Feature font: Helvetica Neue Thin, Light, Roman, Bold (recommended lower weights Thin and Light). Tracking set to -25 in Adobe applications to maintain contemporary feel.
- System font: Verdana, a humanist sans-serif typeface, used widely for system text.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Customized imagery experience based on Epson user segments (e.g., Home Office: emotional, nostalgic, fun).
- Brand imagery to be dynamic, engaging, and aligned with Epson brand attributes like creativity, attention-grabbing design. Bland stock photos to be avoided.
- Good imagery selection guidelines: emotionally provocative, fresh designs, and inspirational for other designers.
- Photography guidelines for home use segments: evocative, contains significant emotional and compositional elements, avoiding shallow 'cutesy' styles.
- Business professionals: Styling and dynamism heavily emphasized in professional displays; visual elements must feel ambitious and impactful.

**Brand Voice:**

- Copy tone: priority given to visuals over copy. Content must emphasize scannability and clarity, avoiding boastfulness or hard-sell approaches.
- Promotional banners: messaging varies between functional clarity and aspirational storytelling, avoiding overuse of personality without communicating offers clearly.

**Notes:**

- Document evolves and emphasizes collaborative user-oriented sections ensuring design guidelines dynamically align with evolving markets (referring various Epson corporate brands over multiple markets).

### 2013

**Logo Information:**

- The Epson logo should be locked to the bottom-right corner in banners and either placed in a blue parallelogram or on a dark, uncluttered part of the image. The logo must not transition in before or after the parallelogram.
- Logo placement for carousel banners is not required on the Epson website.

**Color Information:**

- The colors used within the document have not been evaluated by Pantone, Inc., and do not guarantee an accurate match to Pantone standards.

**Typography Information:**

- Headlines are Helvetica Neue Bold 75, all uppercase, and should be either white or 90% black depending on the color of the background image.
- Sub-headlines are Helvetica Neue Bold 75, sentence case, and should be either white or 90% black depending on the color of the background image.
- Body text is separated by sub-headlines in blue header boxes with square corners, with key lines helping to separate body copy.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Banners showcase a consistent graphic system to deal with varying amounts of text and images. Layouts include carousel teaser banners, footer banners, and EDM teaser banners with unique placements such as bottom-left clickable buttons.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Button placement for banners, where possible, should sit in the bottom-left corner 20px off the bottom. Buttons can be placed over blue parallelograms or photographic images but must not be cut in half. Banner design avoids rounded corners, and all panels ensure 90° angled corners.

**Brand Voice:**

- The tone of voice should be accessible, concise, and consistent. Utilize precise punctuation and grammar while avoiding overused adjectives, lazy business language, and inconsistency.
- Engage with the audience by including personal references such as 'we' and 'you' and avoid third-person language like 'it' or 'they'.
- The tone must reflect Epson's balance of technology capability and consumer insight, with sufficient focus on both.

**Notes:**

- Banners for EDMs, side, and footer teaser banners showcase consistent imagery and typographical rules, such as Helvetica Neue Bold 75. Button design maintains strict placement and ensures all text panels are at 90° angles.
- Further guidance and website design updates may be included in future online guidelines.
