## Brand Guidelines

### 2021

**Logo Information:**

- Creighton University primary logo must be used in external communications, including print, web, electronic materials, promotional items, and signage. The logo is acceptable only in Creighton Blue (Pantone 286), black, or white. Alteration, redrawing, distortion, or rebuilding of the logo is prohibited.
- Left-aligned and centered logo orientations are available for colleges and administrative units with optional addition of secondary identification. Athletic logos cannot be sub-branded, and the treated exception applies to Heider College of Business.
- Tertiary logos include unit distinctions appearing below college/school names. Secondary and tertiary logo use is optional; however, rebuilding, distorting, or creating alternate marks is prohibited.
- Clear space around Creighton University's logo must equal the X-height of 'UNIVERSITY.' Minimum size requirement is 1-inch width, ensuring design legibility.
- Incorrect usage examples include stretching/skewing logos, changing colors, placing the logo on backgrounds with insufficient contrast, and adding foreign design elements. Approved logo files are downloadable via logo.creighton.edu.

**Color Information:**

- Creighton's logo is acceptable exclusively in the following colors: Creighton Blue (Pantone 286), black, or white.
- Clear space rules detail incorporating backgrounds to maintain sufficient contrast with the Creighton logo.

**Typography Information:**

- Creighton University uses the font families Proxima Nova, Conduit (all capitals), and Mrs Eaves XL Serif. Proxima Nova is preferred for body text; any can be used for headers.
- Proxima Nova font includes styles Thin, Light, Regular, Semibold, Bold, Extrabold, and Black with italic variants for each weight.
- Conduit font is restricted to all-capitals typography use to ensure consistent visual emphasis.
- Mrs Eaves XL Serif font preserves classic serif components, used particularly for aesthetic legibility and highlights.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Creighton's imagery should reflect environmental, architectural, learning environment, and portrait styles aligned to Jesuit Mission values.
- Environmental photos emphasize natural surroundings while maintaining Creighton's brand identity.
- Architectural photos capture Creighton's campus layout and structural elements, supporting educational settings.
- Learning environment imagery consists of candid classroom/group scenes emphasizing collaboration and education.
- Portrait photography rules encourage aligned composition and reflective mood, focusing on Creighton's diverse individual representation.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Minimum spacing around Creighton University's logo must match the full X-height specifications, ensuring no nearby elements within this 'safe zone.'
- Brochure layouts follow unintrusive design with left-alignment for text blocks and consistent spacing across columns.

**Brand Voice:**

- Creighton amplifies its Jesuit heritage, focusing on values such as forming future leaders, social diversity, and meaningful contributions.
- Social media content must align with brand identity while maintaining approachable tone. Tagged hashtags (#ChooseCreighton, #BluejayLife) are encouraged.

**Notes:**

- Creighton's University Communications and Marketing offers continued guidance on branding queries through accessible contact information.
