## Brand Guidelines

### 2012

**Logo Information:**

- Clear space around the logotype is defined as the height of the letter 'U' in UVT's acronym. The logo cannot be used without this defined clear space.
- Minimum size for the logo is 25mm or 71px in width.
- The logo should always be used in its complete or reduced format without separating its elements. Distortion or alteration of the logo is prohibited.
- Color versions: Officially used in a red and blue-gray color. Logo must always remain on a white background or appear in 'reversed' (white) on colored backgrounds. Institutional colors dominate in logo variations.
- For use in promotional materials, the logotype can be engraved on materials like metal, leather, or wood, following visibility constraints.
- The logo on videos and institutional films must be placed in the bottom-right alignment on a safe margin (equal to 1/20 of screen height).
- On digital media, the logo is required to maintain at least its minimal size visibility and clear surrounding space in banners and similar structures.
- Incorrect Uses: Avoid logo placement on low-contrast/non-white backgrounds, resizing below its minimum size, or placing it on inappropriate regions like bottom-left of communication surfaces, unless on back cover.
- The logo must be centered or right-justified on promotional items, ensuring a clear and consistent display. If space is limited, the logo's reduced version can be used.

**Color Information:**

- The primary brand colors are red and blue-gray. These colors dominate institutional designs. The red should convey energy and the blue-gray a sense of calm and technology-oriented focus.
- Color variations are limited to the defined primary red and blue-gray palette. Additional or secondary colors must align with the brand's visual identity.
- Gradients and transparency effects (e.g., 10% white overlay) are acceptable design elements when used in alignment with branding rules.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary font for digital and print content is Arial, used for body text, captions, and subheadings.
- Font 'Zero Threes' is used for specific headings or thematic highlights, especially in digital banners and video titles.
- For signage, the Arabic font Hacen Tunisia Light and the French font Forgotten Futurist Regular are used.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Imagery should reflect a student or academic environment, with a futuristic and technological focus.
- For the newsletter header, use a monochrome photo in a red background frame. The image should convey a student ambiance.
- Institutional videos should feature specific motifs such as students, educators, and red points symbolizing universities in a network.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Margins around layouts are defined in multiples of 'P' which corresponds to the height of the letter 'U' in the UVT acronym. This applies across various contexts (e.g., newsletters, banners, signage).
- Totem signage dimensions are 40 cm x 180 cm with a 5 cm margin, using rounded edges with a radius equivalent to the height of the letter 'U'.
- The layout of banners follows proportional guidelines with logo size matching the width between margins (horizontal) or height (vertical).

**Brand Voice:**

- The brand emphasizes clarity, academic professionalism, and approachability. Messaging should reflect a balance of innovation and accessibility.
- Key phrases like 'Étudiez en toute liberté!' emphasize the freedom and accessibility of education through digital learning.

**Notes:**

- The guidelines cover a wide range of applications including promotions, signage, digital media, and institutional branding. Each application has specific rules for logo placement, spacing, and color usage.
- Examples provided throughout the document illustrate specific use cases for banners, newsletters, posters, promotional items, and video overlays.
