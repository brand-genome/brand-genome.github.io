### 2012

**Logo Information:**

- Invented by John S. Pemberton in 1886, Coca-Cola soft drinks initially featured the iconic Spencerian script logo. The retro logo was designed in black to fit specific old-fashioned branding campaigns.
- The contour bottle must always be showcased in its entirety and never cropped. Approved versions are available on the Design Machine website.

**Color Information:**

- Color Palette:
- Coke Red: PMS=No equivalent, CMYK=C:4 M:100 Y:95 K:0, RGB=R:244 G:0 B:9, HEX=F40000.
- Black: PMS=Black, CMYK=C:0 M:0 Y:0 K:100, RGB=R:0 G:0 B:0, HEX=000000.
- White: PMS=Opaque White, CMYK=C:0 M:0 Y:0 K:0, RGB=R:255 G:255 B:255, HEX=FFFFFF.

**Imagery Photography:**

- The contour bottle, inspired by a cocoa bean pod, is iconic and identified by silhouette or touch. It serves as a cultural icon and is featured in art by Andy Warhol and others.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Dynamic Ribbon can be used for spanning layouts and containing brand elements. It serves as a device for continuity across executions.

**Brand Voice:**

- Brand tone communicates directly and simply with statements such as 'Drink,' 'Enjoy,' 'Always,' reflecting optimism and happiness. Personality traits include being authentic, connected, optimistic, and spontaneous.
- The tagline 'COKE IS HAPPINESS' reinforces the brand's identity as spreading optimism and positivity globally.

**Notes:**

- Coca-Cola celebrated 125 years of sharing happiness through global events, collaborations, and community-driven initiatives.
