## Brand Guidelines

### 2014

**Logo Information:**

- The STEP Logo Device is used as an avatar across social media channels. Logo examples include specific variations derived for regions and branches.
- Incorrect usage example: The STEP Logo Device may not be altered or removed when creating visuals for STEP branding.

**Color Information:**

- Usage of color palette is specified for various applications such as e-newsletters, promotional material, and web banners. It mentions strong color usage for bold messaging.
- Secondary colors are emphasized for pulling out important content against imagery, as noted in brochure spread designs.

**Typography Information:**

- Editorial and messaging are written to reflect professionalism, simplicity and person-to-person communication, aligning text tone and format. No specific font families or weights are explicitly stated.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Imagery for the STEP branding includes location shots relevant to regions or branches as well as bespoke illustrations showcasing worldwide presence. Imagery may feature dynamic backgrounds dependent on audience and purpose.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Detailed grid system implementation is noted for brochure spreads, stationery designs, and the creation of the STEP shape using software tools like InDesign.
- Precise margin, padding, and spacing measurements are detailed for design elements including letters, compliments slips, and business cards (e.g., 10mm and 5mm measurements for stationery items).

**Brand Voice:**

- STEP's tone of voice is conversational, simple, and globally minded. It avoids formal or long words and explains technical terms for clarity. Tone varies slightly across promotional or editorial channels, but retains consistency to the STEP brand.

**Notes:**

- STEP branding integrates a distinctive 'STEP shape' created with InDesign tools, using isometric circles and Pathfinder tools to curve elements. This shape serves as a prominent branding device across media formats.
- Social media strategy emphasizes using the STEP logo device for avatars and region-specific branding while maintaining imagery consistent with context. STEP's worldwide presence is highlighted through illustrations.
