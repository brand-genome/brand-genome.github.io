## Brand Guidelines

### 2018

**Logo Information:**

- The 5-line 'M' is the primary signifier representing Manchester. It is also available in a 3-line version for smaller spaces, particularly digital use.
- 'M + Manchester' is utilized internationally to provide context where the 'M' is less recognized.
- Minimum size requirements: The 5-line 'M' must be at least 25mm wide in print or 100px wide digitally. The 3-line 'M' can be used at a minimum width of 15mm in print or 55px in digital applications.
- Clear space rules: Maintain a space around the logo that is free from other visual elements or text.
- Placement rules: Positioning of the logo can be on the left, right, or centered, but must adhere to clear space and dimension guidelines.
- Logo placement guidance includes sensitive and improper placement examples to ensure proper alignment.
- The signifier must be placed on plain backgrounds to maintain clarity and legibility. It can appear on suitable images if the signifier remains legible.

**Color Information:**

- Primary brand colors include Pantone 227 CMYK (7 | 100 | 7 | 21), RGB (170 | 0 | 97), HEX (#aa0061); Pantone 1905 CMYK (0 | 47 | 7 | 0), RGB (245 | 155 | 187), HEX (#f59bbb); Pantone 569 CMYK (90 | 9 | 58 | 17), RGB (0 | 129 | 109), HEX (#00816d). Darker colors like purple, forest green, and burgundy are used for more formal presentations. Accent colors derived from imagery should be used sparingly and not dominate the design.

**Typography Information:**

- Details about typography are not explicitly listed in the extracted guidelines.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Imagery should match Manchester's vibrant and eclectic identity, supporting the tone and overall brand aesthetics.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Maintain proper alignment and spacing standards for logo placement, adhering to prescribed rules for clear area around the signifier.

**Brand Voice:**

- Manchester's tone of voice reflects opportunity, confidence, inclusivity, and optimism. It avoids cynicism, arrogance, pretentiousness, and uses conversational and succinct language to connect with audiences.
- Words that are avoided include: Interesting, Fantastic, Amazing, Cool, Madchester, and Stunning. Preferred words include: Vibrant, Surprising, Unique, Thriving, and Progressive.

**Notes:**

- Usage of the 'M' signifier requires prior approval from the brand guardian, Richard Grange, and compliance with the outlined guidelines.
