## Brand Guidelines

### 2019

**Logo Information:**

- The Zero Density logo is the official corporate logo. It is used in company-wide applications such as stationery, building signage, and as sign-off accompanying copyright and legal information. The position, size, color, and spacing of the logo elements are pre-determined and may not be altered.
- Minimum height for Zero Density logo: 8 mm in print and 35 pixels on screen.
- Minimum clear space around the logo: 7.5 mm measured from the 'y' at the end of the logo. To create greater impact, more space should be allowed.
- Do not allow photos, typography, or other graphic elements to enter the clear space area.
- Incorrect logo usage includes altering the shape, proportion, color, or orientation of logos; changing background colors to unapproved ones; changing typefaces within the logo; and changing the orientation of the logo.
- Examples of the prohibited usage of the logo, such as rotating or animating the signature, not maintaining appropriate clear space (7.5 mm around), or using the logo alone without the channel signature.

**Color Information:**

- RGB and HEX color guidelines for digital use: Pantone Orange 021C, RGB(254, 80, 0), HEX(#FE5000), CMYK(0, 65, 100, 0).

**Typography Information:**

- The corporate font used in Zero Density marketing materials is Sofia Pro Set. It should only be used in marketing materials and presentations provided by Zero Density. It is prohibited in third-party marketing materials.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Minimum clear space around the signature of the logo is 7.5 mm. Creating greater space is allowed for more visual impact.

**Brand Voice:**

- Guidelines for written communication of Zero Density trademarks: Always spell and capitalize names as shown. Do not shorten, abbreviate, translate, or modify names; for example, 'Zero' must not be replaced with '0', and trademarks like 'Reality Engine' must not be altered or translated into other languages.
- Channel affiliates and certified individuals must never allow the Zero Density logo to stand alone in their communications. Appropriate channel signature guidelines apply to advertisements, websites, booth signs, stationery, and other communications.
- Zero Density certification signatures are personal. Certified individuals cannot use them on general company communications (e.g., advertisements, brochures, or online promotions). Company references to employee competence are allowed in text format only.

**Notes:**

- Zero Density requires that their logos not be used in any manner that falsely suggests association or damages goodwill, value, or reputation.
- Trademark use advice: Do not render trademarks phonetically, transliterate them, or alter trademark portions such as replacing 'Zero Density' with 'Zero Densité'.
