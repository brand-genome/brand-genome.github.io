**Imagery Photography:**

- Avoid images or copy that glorifies or endorses game addiction; visuals can be inspired by scenes of violence but should not depict actual violent acts. Ensure correct licensing and permissions are received for any use of third-party game titles, characters, or equipment. Avoid picturing competitor products.
- Patterns should be used as subtle overlays over photos and not at 100% opacity over photos. Avoid placing patterns on faces and ensure text legibility when overlaying patterns.

**Brand Voice:**

- Speak to customers like a knowledgeable friend to build trust. Use an edgy, casual tone with gamer-centric humor.

**Notes:**

- Two-color logo guidelines for branded merchandise: one-color white, gray, black, or Crucial Dark Blue logo is best.
- Branded items should avoid unrelated items like weapons; items suitable for branding include mousepads, screen cleaners, USB drives, or other computer-related gifts.
- Event merchandise colors should align with the brand's primary color palette. Neutrals like gray, white, or very dark blue are a first choice; second choices include black or a match of Crucial Blue. Colors outside the primary or accent palette should not be used.
- Approval processes for branded materials are required from regional design leads. Approvals can be obtained in person, via email/chat (24 hours), or through formal review (1-3 days).
- Ensure proper use of trademarks and copyrights to strengthen the brand perception. Crucial®, Ballistix®, and Micron® are registered trademarks of Micron Technology, Inc.
