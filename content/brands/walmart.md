- Other spark colors require brand approval.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary typeface is Myriad Pro. All communication materials should exclusively use this font in varying weights (Light, Regular, Bold, Italic) to create an appropriate information hierarchy. Arial may be used for non-graphic contexts.
- Instructions for using Myriad Pro effectively: use type sizes and weights to emphasize hierarchy, avoid all-uppercase text, and strictly use approved colors.
- Myriad Pro Light: Humanistic feel, formal voice, suitable for headers and fashion-related text.
- Myriad Pro Regular: Suitable for all media, large text with solid background or beneath images.
- Myriad Pro Bold: Used for impactful headlines and emphasis.
- Myriad Pro Italic: Use sparingly for book/movie/music titles, adds subtle emphasis, provides a feminine feel in some applications.
- Compliance rules: Do not adjust kerning or distort fonts. Add effects such as shadows very carefully and sparingly.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Proportional rules for positioning the spark above people or objects: measurements provided for full-body and close-up images.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Detailed measurement guidelines for spark size proportional to text height and number of lines.

**Brand Voice:**

- Typography and layout should not feel overwhelming; use lowercase and uppercase combinations for warmth.

**Notes:**

### 2014

**Logo Information:**

- Clear space around the Walmart tagline and symbol includes measurements: vertical space is defined by the height of the 'r' in Walmart; horizontal space is defined by the width of the 'r'. Clear space for the symbol equals half the height of one spoke.
- Tagline should not be used smaller than 7pt. If smaller usage is required, the tagline must be dropped.
- Logo versions for services: Standard stacked, standard stacked with service title, and horizontal orientations shown.
- Logo versions for department names include standard stacked and horizontal alignments.
- Department name guidelines specify line length should not exceed 8 times the height (x) of the Walmart logo. In case of longer names, character count should be limited to 15.
- Logo color guidelines state to use Medium Blue (PANTONE® 285 C). Alternative color backgrounds can also use PANTONE® 287 C and PANTONE® 284 C in restricted cases.
- One-color logos should use Medium Blue (PANTONE® 285 C). For backgrounds matching or brighter than PANTONE® 285 C, reverse out the logo to white.
- Black-and-white logos: Use black on white backgrounds; on black backgrounds, reverse out to white.

**Color Information:**

- Primary color palette includes: Medium Blue (PANTONE® 285), Light Blue (PANTONE® 284), Dark Blue (PANTONE® 287), Yellow (PANTONE® 1235), Orange (PANTONE® 166), Fresh Product Dark Green (PANTONE® 364), Fresh Product Light Green (PANTONE® 368). Each color is provided with CMYK, RGB, and HEX values.
- Highlight colors should be used sparingly in combination with core colors to add depth.
- Color balance: Use pie chart-based ratio to ensure proper use of core and highlight colors across Walmart-branded materials.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary typeface: Myriad Pro, selected for its approachable and straightforward qualities. Preferred casing includes uppercase and lowercase styling.
- Font weights explained: Myriad Pro Light for feminine tone, Regular for general usage, Bold for headlines and subheads, and Italic for titles or alternative script applications.
- Typeface guidelines: Use approved Walmart typefaces, align text flush left and ragged right, limit uppercase usage, avoid special effects, keep kerning standard, and do not substitute typefaces unless using Arial for corporate communication.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Recommended imagery should feature natural (not overly posed or stylized), high-quality photos showing positive emotional benefits, activity- or product-focused content. Should reinforce brand traits: caring, authentic, innovative, straightforward, and optimistic.
- Specific photography categories include: Apparel, Product, Consumables, Home, Electronics, Food, Grocery.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Tagline and logo layouts require adherence to specific clear space and proportions (e.g., 'R' height/width).
- Department names must follow a strict length and spacing proportional rule relative to the logo height.

**Brand Voice:**

- Tone of voice characteristics include being Caring, Authentic, Innovative, Straightforward, and Optimistic.
- Each tone trait includes specific writing guides: Caring (empathetic, helpful), Authentic (friendly, conversational), Innovative (enthusiastic, technology-aware), Straightforward (brief, honest), and Optimistic (positive, benefit-focused).
- The '9 Key Ingredients' for proper writing voice include avoiding shouting, conversational tone, short sentences, gender-neutral language, contractions, and active call-to-action phrasing.

**Notes:**

- Email signatures must use Arial font and match the provided format. Includes name, title, contact details, and store information.
- Approved PowerPoint templates include preferred white-text-on-blue-background and blue-text-on-white-background styles. Templates available at walmartbrandcenter.com.
- Few mentions exist regarding additional departmental or service artwork available at walmartbrandcenter.com.
- Brand enforcement emphasis: Walmart employees are encouraged to follow guidelines and reach out for clarifications to BRANDCEN86@wal-mart.com.
