## Brand Guidelines

### 2020

**Imagery Photography:**

- Illustration styles include orthographic and isometric approaches. Orthographic is flat and straightforward, while isometric is more complex and expensive. Consider scope and usage duration for choosing a style.
- Representative photography should be fun, clear, and authentic. Avoid overly staged or 'on the nose' stock photos. Images should focus on solutions, concepts, and industry contexts rather than literal depiction.
- Abstract photography can be used for concepts that are not reducible to representative imagery. Opt for macro close-ups, ultra-expansive views, color washes, and other abstract visuals.
- Choose imagery by geography carefully, reflecting a global audience. Consider cultural differences in imagery interpretations and avoid offensive content.
- Photography in use is an evolving component of Blue Prism's design system, setting tone rather than templates for creation.

**Brand Voice:**

- Blue Prism's friendlier and simpler voice combines tone, brand attributes, and guides content creation. Messaging should emphasize clear communication about products and customers without being repurposed from this document. The latest brand messaging and editorial guidelines are found on Blue Prism's SharePoint at: blueprism.sharepoint.com/sites/BrandDesign/SitePages/Brand-Messaging-and-Editorial-Guidelines.aspx.
