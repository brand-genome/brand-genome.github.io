## Brand Guidelines

### 2020

**Logo Information:**

- This hand-crafted sans serif brand mark brings 'If It Barks' into the modern era. The bold typeface is powerful and attention-grabbing while the rounded edges make it playful and approachable. Small or large, this mark makes an impression. The primary logo color is Mastricht Blue. Use the alternate white or black version of the logo when the primary blue is not able to be used.
- The logo should always have space to breathe so it doesn’t appear cramped. The bright blue box surrounding the logo represents the space that should remain clear of other elements. It is recommended that the shaded area be kept clear as well though not necessarily required.
- The submark and wordmark should not be used together unless the paw is being utilized in a watermarked capacity. For patterns and repetitive use, the outline version will be utilized. As a single icon, the bold version will be showcased.
- The primary logo should be equivalent to or larger than 50px wide to maintain legibility. *The absolute minimum is 35px wide and must be 100% legible – for web use only. Use the suggested minimum or larger for print. The submark should retain a minimum stroke of 0.25px for both print and web.
- Use the primary logo in Mastricht Blue whenever possible. Be mindful of wonky color combinations and clashes whether it’s placed on-screen, apparel, or printed materials. Use the alternate white or black version of the logo when the primary logo is not able to be used. The wordmark should be used most prominently when branding, swag, photography overlay, etc.
- Do not alter the visual appearance of the artwork. Violations include stretching or skewing the brandmark, altering its colors, rotating it, adding a shadow, or changing the font.

**Color Information:**

- The primary palette includes the following colors: Mastricht Blue - Pantone 296 C (HEX: #0f2135, RGB: 15, 33, 53, CMYK: 90, 75, 55, 68), Oxford Blue - Pantone 2965 C (HEX: #00263e, RGB: 0, 38, 61, CMYK: 100, 78, 48, 54), Rackley Blue - Pantone 5415 C (HEX: #6481A4, RGB: 100, 129, 164, CMYK: 49, 21, 10, 30), and Dark Puce - Pantone 7645 C (HEX: #522B39, RGB: 82, 43, 57, CMYK: 53, 80, 54, 50).
- A secondary palette is for web use only and does not include CMYK or Pantone equivalents. Mastricht Blue (HEX: #0f2135, RGB: 15, 33, 53), Rackley Blue (HEX: #6481A4, RGB: 100, 129, 164), Dark Jet (HEX: #333333, RGB: 51, 51, 51), Pewter Blue (HEX: #94a6bb, RGB: 148, 166, 187), Payne’s Grey (HEX: #526d8f, RGB: 82, 109, 143), and Platinum (HEX: #e6eaed, RGB: 230, 234, 237).
- Color is an integral part of the identity toolkit, creating consistency and a luxury vibe for the brand with a blue theme providing a happy, modern, and friendly aesthetic.

**Typography Information:**

- Gilroy typography family is used. Gilroy Bold is preferred for headings and subheadings due to its fun yet distinct luxury brand look. Bold and Regular variations should be used for web to keep load times minimal while other variations can be used for print.
- Gilroy Bold should be used as primary typography for all brand materials and customer-facing touchpoints, ensuring a cohesive and consistent look and feel.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Pup-centered photography direction emphasizes comfortable and natural appearances with a focus on minimalist and luxury settings.
- Avoid busy backgrounds, tight leashes, dogs demonstrating fear, intimidating stares, or overly posed images.
- Poses should show personalities without being overly goofy; collars and leashes should be visible with emphasis on dogs' demeanor in tranquil, simple backgrounds.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Clear space is critical for the logo to appear distinct and uncluttered. There should be enough space surrounding the logo so it doesn’t appear cramped.

**Brand Voice:**

- Casual, Friendly, Familiar, Premium, Simple are primary tone of the brand.

**Notes:**

- The guidelines create a cohesive identity system ensuring all customer touchpoints align visually and structurally.
- The brand manual was created in 2020 under the title 'If It Barks'.
