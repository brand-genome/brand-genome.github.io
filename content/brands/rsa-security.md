## Brand Guidelines

### 2018

**Logo Information:**

- The RSA logo is essential for the brand equity of the organization. It should be used correctly on all marketing materials, including trade show graphics, internal and external presentations, packaging, promotional products, and more. The logo comprises three stylized letters that make up RSA. Examples and guidelines for primary and secondary logo usage are on the referenced page.
- Tagline lock-ups must not be altered, such as by changing colors or word sizes. The first appearance of the trademark symbol should lock with the tagline. Variations of the lock-up are available upon request.
- RSA product names are locked with special logo versions for use in PowerPoint presentations, signage, or videos. Contact the brand for more information.
- The RSA logo, as a graphic element, can be utilized as a background on white or colored pages.
- Logo placement guidelines include specific examples where the RSA logo's center aligns with headers and layouts for print, banners, and web.

**Color Information:**

- The primary color for RSA is red with specific secondary accent colors (PMS 1815, PMS 7545, PMS 583) and neutral colors (PMS 427, White, Black, Neutral Black). Designers are encouraged to use red predominantly and neutrals sparingly to maintain consistency. Accent colors should perform support roles like buttons or charts sparingly.
- The logo's secondary usage guideline mentions using a red logo only against white backgrounds. On insufficient contrast backgrounds, white logos are used to ensure readability.
- Clear space and minimum size requirement for logo use are defined: Minimum Digital Dimensions = 60px wide, Minimum Print Dimensions = 0.5 in wide.
- Strict instructions highlight not to stylize the logo against black or colored backgrounds that lack contrast, ensuring proper visibility of the logo. Do not modify color settings.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Black-and-white photography with saturated color focus on the subject is encouraged, mainly for campaigns, social media, or other appropriate situations. A pixel dot texture overlay can be applied to these photos to convey a digital or abstract theme when warranted.
- Photography treatment guidelines include bold duotone imagery with red or other color palettes applied. This is primarily used with abstract photos that lack a clear subject. Designers are encouraged to contact the brand if additional clarification is needed.
- Different photography styles for various mediums (social media, web, print, events) are mentioned. Designers should emphasize RSA's corporate red color across these formats.
- Instructions for corporate email signature emphasize it must remain HTML-based for consistency. Amendments to its format or content are not advised without proper authorization.

**Spacing Layout:**

- The layout extensively uses primary red coupled with neutral tones for integral designs. Sparingly use accent secondary colors in CTAs, charts, or diagrams.
- Header and footer layout arrangements note vector assets and proportion adjustments for online banners and print examples.

**Brand Voice:**

- Color balance guidelines promote RSA red or related imagery prominently in all media to anchor the corporate presence consistently. A balance of bold and duotone graphic themes in layouts is recommended.
- Design preferences emphasize prominent representation of red, maintaining the corporate brand's theme irrespective of whether used in banners, backdrops, or other media.
