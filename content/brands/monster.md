## Brand Guidelines

### 2011

**Logo Information:**

- Online smallest size: 7 pixels measured to x-height of the letter m in 'monster'. Print smallest preferred size: 1.5 picas (0.25") measured to x-height, and minimum print size: 0.75 picas measured to x-height. Wordmark and logo guidelines included.
- Monster logo type must always be used as the prominent identifier for the brand. 'Monster Eye' logo is no longer used.
- Black & White and grayscale logo lockups are provided. Final artwork must not replace description copy, and colors may change only according to color usage guidelines.

**Color Information:**

- Primary brand palette: PMS 267 C Purple and PMS 383 C Green are dominant for consistency. PMS Green variations require press checks for color matching. PMS 151 C Orange must always be treated as an accent color.
- Secondary palette is available for rare branding needs, but requires prior approval by the Monster brand team.
- Online palette for B2C communications: Green (#96B11C), Purple (#660099). Online palette for B2B communications: Green (#96B11C), Blue (#0060B2). Accent color: Orange (#FF9900), mainly for rollovers/buttons.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary font for headlines: Franklin Gothic Book. Text faces include Franklin Gothic Medium. Headlines may incorporate all weights. Franklin Gothic Book is advised against for MS Office documents, use Verdana if unavailable.
- Helvetica may be used when Verdana is unavailable, or in advertising on case-by-case approval. Monster marker font must never be used in advertising or branding mediums.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photographs must communicate authenticity and exuberance (unposed). Preferred imagery: four-color images of people in their natural work environments, energetic and diverse demographics.
- Photography should never use black & white imagery, posed/contrived work environments, or silhouetted visuals.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Spacing or grid guidelines not explicitly extracted in text, but layout likely follows proportions tied to typography sizes (suggested excerpts point indirectly to alignment principles).

**Brand Voice:**

- Copy tone guidelines: Optimistic, provocative, motivating. Focus on enabling user goals. Avoid marketing-heavy language; prefer conversational style. Contractions encouraged ('that's' vs 'that is').

**Notes:**

- Design must adhere to standards outlined within sections, including maintaining original artwork fidelity and referencing PMS/RGB color codes for consistency. Always refer back to updated sections in Helios platform.
