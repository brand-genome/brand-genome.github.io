## Brand Guidelines

### 2013

**Logo Information:**

- The logo structure follows a fixed geometry ensuring compositional consistency. All elements, including the distance between the icon, text, and spacing, are integral to the logo and cannot be modified.
- Clear space indicates the minimum area that must remain clear around the logo, ensuring no interference from text, images, or other elements. Proportions are part of the logo and must be respected.
- Minimum size requirements specify that the logo should not be reduced below 3 cm, as readability might be impacted.
- Examples of incorrect usage are shown concerning proportions, colors, and placement of logo elements.

**Color Information:**

- The distinctive logo color is Burgundy Red (Pantone 7435 C). Alternate color systems are provided, including CMYK (15, 100, 21, 51), RGB (127, 0, 66), and HEX (#7F0042). And for digital uses, RGB or HEX profiles are recommended. Users are advised to perform print tests to ensure color fidelity.
- Color variants are defined based on use cases, such as monochrome black-and-white for fax, grayscale for internal documentation, and full color for external publications. Variants include positive and negative forms of black and white, grayscale, and color, as well as 30% gray on colored backgrounds.

**Typography Information:**

- The institutional font is Frutiger in Roman and Black weights. In cases where Frutiger is unavailable, Arial, a sans serif font, may be substituted.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Clear space rules dictate that the minimum area around the logo should be maintained to avoid any interference from text, images, or margins.

**Notes:**

- The brand guidelines provide clear instructions for visual identity use, including specifics for letterhead, envelopes, and business cards (page 16), PowerPoint templates (page 18), and event posters (page 19).
