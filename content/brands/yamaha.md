**Logo Information:**

- The Yamaha Logomark consists of the Tuning Fork Mark and the Yamaha Logo (wordmark 'Yamaha' in a specific typeface). The preferred Logomark is the 3-D style logo in Yamaha Red, with alternative forms available (2-D and stacked). The Logomark includes a clear space rule, which is one-half the diameter of the Tuning Fork Mark.
- Alternative logomarks include: (a) Yamaha Red 2-D, (b) Black 2-D, and (c) Reverse-out 2-D (white on a Yamaha Red background). Additionally, a 3-D stacked Yamaha Red logomark is available for use in specific square or vertical layout constraints.
- Improper examples: Yamaha logs used by other Yamaha companies (such as Yamaha Music) cannot be used for Yamaha Motor Corporation. Notable differences include separate design features in the Tuning Fork Mark and Yamaha Wordmark.
- Placement rules for the preferred 3D Yamaha Logomark: The Logomark must appear in specific positions within a document—top right, top left, bottom right, centered in the back middle, or back bottom.
- The Yamaha Genuine Parts & Accessories Logo (GP&A) is a derivative with the Yamaha Logomark in Yamaha Red, and additional elements in black. The preferred version is the 3-D GP&A Logomark, with alternative 2-D forms also available.

**Color Information:**

- Yamaha Red: CMYK - 100% magenta, 100% yellow. Pantone PMS1797. RGB - R: 255, G: 0, B: 0. HEX: #FF0000.
- Yamaha Logomarks follow specific background usage rules: (a) white background, using Yamaha Red. (b) Black background with white reverse-out Logomark. (c) Silver background (PMS 877 or Pantone Cool Gray 5). (d) Yamaha Red or other red shades.

**Typography Information:**

- The Yamaha Logo is the word 'YAMAHA' in a designated letter style and spacing. It is only used in conjunction with the Tuning Fork Mark to form the Yamaha Logomark, except for on-product badging.

**Spacing Layout:**

- The Yamaha Logomark must have a clear space of at least 50% of the diameter of the Tuning Fork Mark around it. This area must be free of other graphical elements or text that may diminish the Logomark’s importance or create the appearance of another logo.
