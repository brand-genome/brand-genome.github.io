## Brand Guidelines

### 2018

**Logo Information:**

- Wordmark (text-only Fourth Wave branding); must not be used in isolation without main EDF branding.

**Color Information:**

- Primary colors include Pantone 7469 (Blue Gray), Process Cyan, Pantone 381 (EDF Yellow-green), Pantone 563 (Teal), and Cool Gray 11 with respective HEX, RGB, and CMYK values outlined.
- 4W primary palette to be used in addition to full EDF color palette. Includes bold, rich colors.

**Typography Information:**

- Swiss 721 typeface is the primary font. Variants include Roman, Bold, Bold Italic, Light, Light Italic, Heavy, Heavy Italic. EDF branded Swiss 721 must be used.
- Typography should include bold contrasts, large vs. small type sizes, and contrasting weights.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Singular, graphic photography with bold colors (e.g., expansive gradients, low POV angles for heroic composition). Rich natural colors combined with captivating vistas provide an aspirational, forward-looking feel.
- More graphic and singular focus for photos: Bold compositions with attention to lighting, color, textures, and movement. Includes themes like intersections, expansive gradients, and contrasts.
- Critter imagery to focus on unique vantage points, patterns, textures, and rich compositions to create an engaging and energetic feel.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Clean open layouts using white space; slight deviations from grid to achieve openness. Includes bold horizontal rules of contrasting weights to structure content.
- Attention to sky (e.g., expansive and gradient colors) to reflect a sense of optimism, possibilities, and forward-thinking.

**Brand Voice:**

- Tone is optimistic, inviting, aspirational, and active. Messaging highlights intersections of innovation, people, and action, with a focus on global impact.

**Notes:**

- Wordmark must meet Fourth Wave branding qualifications and requires specific approval before use.
- EDF Fourth Wave guidelines are secondary to the main EDF Brand Style Guide and are designed to push the EDF brand into a bolder, more innovative space.
- Sample ad campaign concepts use bold contrasts in typography, compelling portraiture, and attention to white space for an effective and clean aesthetic.
