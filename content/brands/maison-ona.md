## Brand Guidelines

### 2013

**Logo Information:**

- The logo is made of two musical symbols: niente (from silence) and arpeggio, together meaning 'proliferation from nothing.'
- Logo usage formats include different kerning adjustments for the name, such as 2x the normal letter width. Examples: ON A (Standard), O N A (Spaced)

**Color Information:**

- Primary colors used in branding include: ONA Blue (Bengali - RGB: 183, 221, 223, HEX: #B7DDDF, CMYK unspecified), ONA Blue (Sevres - RGB: 44, 76, 112, HEX: #2C4C70, CMYK unspecified).

**Typography Information:**

- Font families used include Futura Medium for identity and titles, and Adobe Garamond Pro for text and scores. Italics, Bold, and Bold Italics are also specified.
- Custom fonts were created for use in engraving software, informed by traditional music engraving techniques.
- Engraver font based on Sonora by Christian Texier and percussion-specific fonts are also designed for branding purposes.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Score formats are defined as PO (27 x 35 cm) and PS (24 x 32 cm), based on contemporary music requirements and specific needs like percussionists reading from greater distances.

**Brand Voice:**

- Narrative tone emphasizes artisanal production and close collaboration with composers, showcasing the uniqueness and beauty of each score as a carefully designed object.

**Notes:**

- Printed scores include varied quality papers (e.g., Olin natural white matte for readability across lighting conditions, Tyvek for wrap durability, etc.), hand-bound covers, embossed seals, and custom engraver fonts for artistic quality.
- Maison ONA's naming references cultural expansion, adapting the word 'wave' (Spanish) and 'sound' ('on,' Japanese).
