## Brand Guidelines

### 2014

**Logo Information:**

- The button logo family is inspired by a vintage pin. It is intended to symbolize enthusiastic participation and is used for printed marketing materials, television, web, and email communications. It can be scaled on its own, provided the wordmark is included in the viewing area.
- The classic logo family should be used for disaster situations, corporate materials, exterior building signage, or when the button logo is not appropriate.
- The flat disc logo family is suitable for dark backgrounds or when printing restrictions preclude using the button logo. The white circle behind the cross must be present, making it unsuitable for white backgrounds.
- Required 2x clear space indicated by blue lines, where 1x equals the height and width of one arm of the cross.
- Minimum print size: 0.275 inches (~7 mm). Digital minimum size: 26 pixels.

**Color Information:**

- Primary color is 'Red Cross Red' (PMS 485) with the breakdown: CMYK 0/100/100/0, RGB 237/27/46, HEX ED1B2E.
- Secondary colors include Light Blue (PMS 290), Khaki (PMS 4535), Light Gray (Cool Gray 3), Midnight Blue (PMS 7692), and others.
- Colors are detailed by their respective Pantone, CMYK, RGB, and HEX values.

**Typography Information:**

- Akzidenz-Grotesk Standard family is utilized. Regular and Bold for heads and subheads, Regular for body copy, Bold or Italic for emphasis.
- Sentence case preferred; Title case not recommended.
- Digital alternative to Akzidenz is Arial.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Spacing requirements of logo elements include 2x clear space as defined in guidelines. 1x is equated to the height/width of one arm of the cross.

**Brand Voice:**

- The brand tone is uplifting, empowering, inviting, and personal. It avoids focusing on devastation or disaster but rather leaves people with a feeling of hope and possibility.
- Messaging should encourage everyone to be part of the network and make a difference during times of need, treating them with respect and care.

**Notes:**

- Logo files available at redcross.org/brand.
