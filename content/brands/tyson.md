## Brand Guidelines

### 2017

**Logo Information:**

- The Tyson Foods logo is the primary symbol for the company, representing its family-oriented identity, strong roots, and future-focused direction. The logo utilizes the 'T' monogram inside a weathervane as a crest, tying together the vision and legacy of Tyson Foods.
- Clear space or safe zone rules: For the logo lockup, the space above and below the dash is equal to half the height of the 'T' in 'Tyson'.
- Examples of vertical and horizontal logo lockups for Tyson Foods divisions and programs.
- File format rules for creating logo lockups: Assets must be built from the official template file by a trained designer.
- Do not elongate or change the proportion of the dash in logo lockups, as it should remain a standard em dash.

**Color Information:**

- Primary color is Tyson's Pride blue, which is the deepest tone in their color palette. Used prominently for background, type, and elements such as the dash.
- White is used as a text color against Tyson's Pride blue for maximum legibility.
- The dash is typically the same color as its surrounding copy. This helps unify information and ensure harmony with surrounding content.
- For videos, text should be set in Tyson’s Pride blue or white, whichever provides the best legibility.

**Typography Information:**

- The primary typeface is 'Sentinel' by Hoefler & Co, a serif font known for its honesty, sturdy design, and industrial feel. Used for headlines, print body text, and H3 subheads.
- The secondary typeface is 'Proxima Nova' by Mark Simonson, a sans-serif font known for its geometric clarity. It is used for captions, digital body text, and specific subheads.
- Font weights for Sentinel: Book, Book Italic, Bold, Bold Italic.
- Font weights for Proxima Nova: Regular, Regular Italic, Bold, Bold Italic.
- Preferred fonts for system standard alternatives: 'Century Schoolbook' as a substitute for Sentinel and 'Arial' for Proxima Nova.
- Typographic hierarchy for print: Headlines use Sentinel Book (primary), subheads use bold weights of Sentinel and Proxima Nova (secondary), body copy is Sentinel, captions are Proxima Nova.
- For digital: Primary text uses Proxima Nova Book, secondary text uses Proxima Nova Book (gray), and links or call-to-action text use Proxima Nova Bold in Tyson's Pride blue.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography style emphasizes naturally lit images with appropriate lighting. Images are used with light or dark backgrounds to create contrast and allow space for overlaying text. Composition should have adequate negative space for text placement.
- Dynamic imagery is used to emphasize action and convey a sense of freshness and authenticity.
- When using an overlay for text on videos, a translucent layer (often black at 50% opacity) should be used to ensure text legibility over the video content.

**Spacing Layout:**

- The dash used in typography and logo lockups should have equal space on either side, centered midway between the heading and other text elements. Key examples provided are: Heads, Graphs, and Logos.
- Clear guidelines for typographic spacing in print: For headlines, use 54/58pt type with 11pt paragraph spacing.
- Videos should have lower-third captions aligned to the left, typically with names in Sentinel Book and distinct formatting for titles in Sentinel Bold (70% of name point size).

**Brand Voice:**

- The Tyson Foods brand represents food, family, a pioneering spirit, and a commitment to innovation, progress, and quality. Its purpose is to 'raise the world’s expectations for how much good food can do.'
- The Tyson Foods brand embodies honesty, integrity, and craftsmanship, represented through the chosen typefaces and design elements.
- Tone guidelines for communications prescribe optimism, motivation, and a clear sense of purpose aligned with raising the standards for food and family values.

**Notes:**

- The guidelines include strict rules on logo usage, typography, and photography style to maintain consistency and build a unified brand identity.
- The creation of assets such as logo lockups, typography, and visual compositions requires adherence to provided templates and professional design knowledge.
