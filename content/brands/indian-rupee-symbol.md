## Brand Guidelines

### 2009

**Logo Information:**

- The design philosophy of the symbol is derived 
from the Devanagari script, a traditional script deeply rooted in our Indian culture. The symbol also seamlessly integrates the Latin script which is widely used around the world. This amalgamation traverses boundaries across cultures giving it a universal identity, symbolizing our cultural values and ethos on a global platform. Simplicity of the visual form and imagery creates a deep impact on the minds of people and makes it easy to recognize, recall, and represent across various demographics and cultures.
- The symbol is designed using the Devanagari letter 'Ra' and Roman capital letter 'R.' The letters are derived from the words Rupiah in Hindi and Rupees in English, both denoting Indian currency. This symbol communicates the association of the rupee with Indian and global audiences, creating a direct relationship between the symbol and the rupee.
- The symbol preserves Shiro Rekha, a horizontal top-line unique to the Devanagari script. This essential feature, where letters hang from the top line instead of a baseline, clearly distinguishes itself and proclaims Indian origin.
- The symbol aligns with other existing global currency symbols. It blends harmoniously as part of the global family while retaining its individuality, establishing its purpose for clarity and worldwide cohesiveness.
- The interplay of ‘Ra’ and ‘R’ enhances comprehension for local and global audiences. Familiarity with the visual form improves recognition and makes the symbol easy to adapt universally.
- With its simplicity of three strokes, the symbol ensures high recall value and legibility. Its seamless construction integrates Devanagari and Latin scripts to cater to both national and global audiences.
- Designing the symbol for Devanagari and Latin typography is straightforward due to its similarity with the respective letters 'Ra' and 'R.'
- The symbol remains legible and effective when used in inverse or reverse color applications, even at smaller sizes.
- Balance is achieved by harmonizing the visual inclinations of the 'Ra' shape and horizontal strokes. The single, stable stroke ensures visual equilibrium.
- A harmonized version of the 'paisa' symbol can be designed to complement the rupee symbol as part of the same family.
- The symbol is adaptable across various media, surfaces, and sizes without compromising visual form or legibility.

**Color Information:**

- The two horizontal lines create a tricolor effect symbolizing the three stripes of the Indian national flag. This represents cultural values and national identity.
- The symbol performs effectively with inverse colors, ensuring legibility even in reversed applications.

**Imagery Photography:**

**Brand Voice:**
