## Brand Guidelines

### 2018

**Logo Information:**

- The Future logo is designed on a 15x7 grid. Focus marks are positioned inward horizontally and vertically to allow small scale usage.
- The focus marks are positioned a maximum of two grid spaces vertically and horizontally from the wordmark.
- The logo should always have a safe area equal to the width of one focus mark.
- Below a size of 55mm, the logo focus marks can be adjusted inward for tighter lockup.
- Logo usage guidelines include prohibitions on compressing, stretching, altering balance, distorting, or changing colors.

**Color Information:**

- Primary color palette includes white, black, and Pantone 187 with respective codes: CMYK (C:0 M:0 Y:0 K:0), HEX (#FFFFFF), and Pantone codes.
- Secondary color palette includes various shades with CMYK, RGB, and HEX codes listed, providing complementary variations.
- Gradients can use two colors to create contemporary effects, recommended at a 45-degree angle with complementary colors.

**Typography Information:**

- Headline fonts include Montserrat Medium and Montserrat SemiBold.
- Body copy fonts include Montserrat Regular and Montserrat Light.
- Uppercase typography is used sparingly for short headlines and sub-headlines to maintain emphasis.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Imagery style emphasizes dramatic, visually striking content with high contrast and motion effects to evoke energy and focus.
- Focus marks can frame and emphasize imagery, positioned one mark from the image edge.
- Static or generic stock imagery that lacks dynamism is prohibited.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Consistent spacing and a clear typographic hierarchy are essential for layouts, promoting legibility across visual materials.

**Brand Voice:**

- The tone of voice should reflect Future's identity, community connection, and brand vocabulary focused on inclusivity and technical expertise.
- Adjustments to tone are permissible for diverse audience groups while maintaining authenticity to brand values.
- Preferred language includes 'innovative', 'creative', and 'dynamic' while avoiding overly formal or generic terms.

**Notes:**

- Contact brand@futurenet.com for additional guidelines or resources.
