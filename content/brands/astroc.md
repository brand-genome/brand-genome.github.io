## Brand Guidelines

### 2014

**Logo Information:**

- Primary logos include Abbreviation Lockup ('CMU Astronomy Club') and Full Name Lockup ('Carnegie Mellon Astronomy Club'); intended for medium to large sizes (1 inch or greater).
- Logo clear space requires a minimum margin of '.25x' around the logo, preserving legibility. Clear space measured based on 'x' unit proportional to the logo design.
- Approved background colors for logo usage include black-and-white and faded images (50% opacity).
- Forbidden alterations include changing proportions, rotating/skewing, applying effects like drop shadows or glows, rendering at smaller than approved sizes, using unapproved colors, changing typefaces, and applying embellishments.
- Auxiliary logos include: Small Simplified Lockup ('Astroc') for social media or small sizes under 1 inch; Small Planet-Only Lockup for icon usage (also under 1 inch); and Horizontal Lockup for scenarios using horizontal logotype arrangements.

**Color Information:**

- Primary colors include Pantone 323 C (CMYK: 89/49/51/25), Pantone 7500 C (CMYK: 5/0/11/0), Pantone 180 (CMYK: 18/84/90/7), Pantone 114 (CMYK: 1/16/81/0), Pantone 137 (CMYK: 13/39/93/0).

**Typography Information:**

- Typography styles include Futura Std (Bold & Small-caps for headlines: 30pt, tracking: 100pt), Adobe Caslon Pro (Italic for subheadlines: 15pt), Futura Std (Light, Hex-Framed for Sidebar Heading: 19pt), Futura Std (Bold for Sidebar Subhead: 19pt), Futura Std (Light for Sidebar Body: 10pt, leading: 15pt), Adobe Caslon Pro (Regular for Body Text: 12pt, leading: 17pt).

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography must depict Astronomy Club events, trips, members, equipment, or Carnegie Mellon facilities. Abstract space-science imagery can be used sparingly.
- Photo treatments: Images are presented as-is or with naturalistic post-processing. When logos are overlaid, a blue wash is applied for contrast. Images should not be overly complex or distracting.
- Photos must be high-quality (preferably shot with DSLR) without artifacting or distortions. Proper composition, lighting, and exposure settings should always be followed.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Primary logo clear space is calculated as '.25x' (proportional to logo unit 'x') around all sides.
- Hexagonal tessellating patterns can be used as design motifs on official backgrounds or individual hex cells with blue fills/strokes.

**Brand Voice:**

- The tone reflects a forward-thinking, optimistic perspective reminiscent of the 1960s space program. It aims to convey excitement, discovery, exploration, and fun linked to astronomy.

**Notes:**

- Hexagon motif is widely integrated into visual identity due to its scientific and aesthetic relevance. Its use includes tessellating fields, article 'End Sign' icons, or individual graphic elements, avoiding overlap with official logos.
- Examples of branded materials include letterhead, apparel (hoodies, T-shirts), and branded presentations (e.g., PowerPoint or Keynote).
