## Brand Guidelines

### 2016

**Logo Information:**

- HWDSB provides a listing of school logos. Schools wanting to change their school logos must receive approval from the Superintendent of Student Achievement. Changes involve school community consultations and maintaining high design quality, professional appearance, alignment with HWDSB branding, and ensuring logos are copyright-free and reproducible effectively.
- Departments and programs are not allowed to create their own logos unless linked to specific Ministry of Education programs that use a logo.
- Incorrect usage is addressed as logos must align with HWDSB branding and visual identity standards under the Visual Identity Policy. Logos must not violate copyright or use designs outside professional standards.

**Color Information:**

- Pantone 288C is specified for board logos in signage. The signage design specifically references consistent color branding and signage policy adherence.
- High-contrast colors are recommended for readability. Black text on a white background is identified as optimal for accessibility in printed and electronic materials.
- Website design guidelines enforce the use of black text on white backgrounds as a safe color choice for accessibility, while discouraging background designs that obscure text.

**Typography Information:**

- School signage typography specifications include 'Legacy Sans Std' font family in weights Ultra and Book for school names, titles, and address information.
- For email signatures, guidelines specify Calibri Bold (12pt), Calibri Italic (10pt), and Calibri Regular (10pt), ensuring consistent typography across platforms and accessibility compliance.
- Accessibility standards recommend sans-serif fonts like Calibri and Legacy Sans for readability, opting for font sizes of a minimum of 10pt and a preference for 11-12pt for important content.
- Website design guidelines enforce the use of standardized fonts, maintaining an 11-14pt font size and consistent font usage across pages.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Website imagery guidelines suggest avoiding reliance on images for communication and require alternative descriptive text for accessibility purposes.
- Photography and imagery should avoid elements like animated GIFs, scrolling text, or graphics that bounce, spin, twist, or distract from the content.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Printed documents enforce safe margins with specified trim sizes, full bleed dimensions, and pixel guidelines for corporate signage or business materials.
- 'White space' usage is emphasized as necessary in printed and electronic materials under Visual Identity Policy for clean and accessible design.

**Brand Voice:**

- Tone consistency emphasizes simplicity and readability across materials and compliance with visual identity policy guidelines for public recognition and accessibility.
- Web content should prioritize readability, clarity, and concise language. Unnecessary jargon or education-specific language should be avoided to ensure accessibility.

**Notes:**

- Visual Identity Policy specifies roles for Corporate Communications to approve and monitor consistent branding usage across departments and programs, ensuring adherence to guidelines.
- Accessibility standards are rooted in legal requirements for public organizations and ensure ease of communication across materials for all users.
- School signage dimensions and design are managed under the Facilities Management department’s preferred vendor. Sign designs fall under Visual Identity Policy.
