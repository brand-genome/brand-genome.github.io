## Brand Guidelines

### 2005

**Logo Information:**

- The UNIFEM logo must be reproduced only from official artwork provided by the Strategic Partnerships and Communications Group. The logo includes a direct visual connection to the United Nations system through its color, typography, and symbols including the olive branch and dove.
- The relationship between the secondary text, acronym, and logo mark is fixed. Versions shown include English Large, Spanish Large, French Large, and their Small counterparts. Large versions are for reproductions 2.25 inches or wider, while Small versions are used below this size.
- Minimum size requirements: the Large logo version should not be reproduced at sizes below 2.25 inches (5.715 cm). The Small logo version has a minimum size of 1 inch (2.54 cm).
- The logo color is Pantone 285 Blue or its CMYK equivalent (C: 39, M: 14, Y: 0, K: 0). The letters 'UN' are a lighter blue—C: 89, M: 43, Y: 0, K: 0. Other approved variations include solid colors (black or white) and a 50% Pantone 285 blue tint for background. No other colors are permitted.
- Clear space or 'airspace' of 1/3 the height of the logo must be maintained around the logo on all sides. Where possible, larger spacing should be facilitated. If insufficient space exists, reduce the size of the logo rather than violating the airspace rule.
- Do's for logo usage: The logo may appear in its identity blue color, black, or reversed. It may appear on photographs in approved formats as long as legibility is maintained.
- Don'ts for logo usage: Do not use unapproved colors or apply stroke effects. Do not manipulate or distort the logo by separating elements, changing the secondary text, or creating patterns using logo parts. Text must not be placed over the logo.

**Color Information:**

- Primary color: Pantone 285 Blue (C: 39, M: 14, Y: 0, K: 0); secondary lighter shade for 'UN' (C: 89, M: 43, Y: 0, K: 0). Solid black or white are also permitted for one-color reproductions.

**Typography Information:**

- Official font: Helvetica Neue is the primary typeface for all print materials. Acceptable substitutes include other Helvetica fonts or Arial if Helvetica Neue is unavailable.
- Alternate typeface: Arial is the approved alternate when Helvetica Neue is unavailable.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Logos placed on photographs must maintain legibility and use only approved colors or effects.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Airspace: 1/3 the height of the logo must remain clear on all sides. Ensure adequate spacing for clear visibility. If space constraints occur, reduce logo size rather than violate spacing.

**Notes:**

- The logo must only be reproduced from official artwork provided by the Strategic Partnerships and Communications Group. Any deviations or customizations require prior approval from this group.
