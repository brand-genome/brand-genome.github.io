## Brand Guidelines

### 2018

**Logo Information:**

- The logo emphasizes a unique 'U' to celebrate individuality and inspire self-advocacy. Wordmarks are bespoke and designed to stand tall on their own.
- Wordmark is the hero asset of the brand; it should appear on all brand communication.
- Clear space diagrams specify the correct minimum amount of space surrounding the logo to maintain safe spacing away from other graphic elements.
- Logo misuse examples include rotating the logo, applying effects, using outlines, altering proportions or colors beyond guidelines, applying gradients, or other treatments not outlined in the brand book.

**Color Information:**

- The color system reflects diversity of emotions and topics illuminated through the brand, with compositions allowing sufficient space and breathing room.
- Recommended color usage examples include maintaining readability of typography with adequate color contrast on white backgrounds, overlaying shapes, and different color backgrounds.
- Shape colors should differ to avoid visual confusion.

**Typography Information:**

- Typography reflects editorial expressiveness combined with organic design. Domaine Text features organic flourishes with angular serifs, creating a warm personal presence suitable for editorial use.
- Matter fonts include Light, Regular, Medium, Semibold styles, with shapes bringing subtle warmth in a grotesk sans-serif design.
- Typography misuse guidelines include avoiding compression, stretched styles, overly tight or loose kerning, using outlines, effects, or colors beyond specified design rules.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography focuses on capturing real, natural tender moments in life, emphasizing humanity and living without focusing on the disease. This includes raw, optimistic, intimate, and transient approaches.
- Examples of misuse include staged stock imagery, overly edited visuals, overly dark images, and exaggerated angles.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Spacing considerations are present throughout the design toolkit, including ensuring logos, typography, and layouts feel spacious, avoid overcrowding, and maintain organic composition.

**Brand Voice:**

- Brand tone of voice emphasizes dialogue, inclusivity, optimism, and actionable narratives, while shifting perspectives positively and avoiding prescriptive characteristics.
- Examples of voice include showcasing intimate relations, momentary gratitude, and connections specific to realistic and non-prescriptive themes.

**Notes:**

- Brand applications section demonstrates gestural applications combining design toolkit, photography, and visual narratives for brand implementation.
- Illustration examples emphasize inclusivity of body types, genders, ethnicities, and flexibility in illustration styles tailored to Humanly's brand.
