## Brand Guidelines

### 2012

**Logo Information:**

- The Samsung Mobile Brand Guide references multiple types of logos: Master Logos, Device Logos, Campaign Logos, and other variations suitable for different contexts.
- The preferred Samsung logo for the USA is the Samsung lettermark. The Samsung ellipse logo is used globally but is rarely used in the Samsung Mobile USA communications.
- Logo clear space requirements dictate that there must be clear space equal to 2X (where X is the height of the wordmark) around the logo.
- The Samsung logo must only appear in the following color variations: Samsung Blue (PMS 286), black, white, or 60% gray. The letters within the positive ellipse logo should never be reversed out with background color or patterns showing through.
- Device logos like Samsung Galaxy Note and Galaxy S series logos must be used intact without color or style variation. Samsung text remains in black.
- Campaign logos are to be used for specific promotions and during specific timeframes (examples of campaign logos provided).

**Color Information:**

- The Samsung color palette includes: Pantone 286 C Blue, Pantone 539 C Deep Blue, Pantone 2995 C Light Blue, and additional primary, secondary, and accent colors with detailed CMYK, RGB, and HEX values. RGB is for digital, and CMYK is for print.

**Typography Information:**

- Helvetica Neue LT Std is prescribed for typography. Primary weights include: 35 Thin (Headlines), 45 Light (Subheadlines), 55 Roman (Body text and call-to-action), and 65 Medium (Small call-to-action text).

**Imagery Photography:**

- Samsung's photography style should reflect the richness of users' lives and brand values. Images should not be staged or overly posed but should be expressive, casual, and look as though taken with a phone. The use cases should dramatize device capabilities by highlighting user-generated stories and diverse interactions.
- Prohibited photographic styles include: images that lack focus, story, or purpose; situations that don't feel authentic; overly busy compositions; staged or model-like poses; gloomy or dark subjects.
- User-generated photography is encouraged to tell stories about people, places, objects, and memorable moments.

**Spacing Layout:**

- The grid layout specifies typographic hierarchy with defined alignments, proportions, and placement rules for headlines, subheadlines, and body copy. Typeface specifications include layouts showing examples for visual consistency.

**Brand Voice:**

- The Samsung Mobile tone of voice is described as inspiring, energetic, and approachable. Communication should be conversational, confident, and avoid technical jargon while focusing on benefits over technical specifications. Clear and memorable storytelling is emphasized.
- Messaging guidelines indicate empathetic and engaging communication. Use personal pronouns like 'you' and 'yours,' and ensure differentiation by highlighting unique features (e.g., sharing capabilities).
- Messaging should include direct yet intriguing headlines, supported by clear subheadings and detailed body copy. Examples provided for reference. Legal marks should only appear in the first mention (headline or body copy).
- Formatting guidelines: Avoid title case for headlines (use sentence case instead), include periods at the end of headlines and subheads, and use consistent date and time formatting.
