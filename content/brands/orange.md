## Brand Guidelines

### 2016

**Logo Information:**

- The Orange logo is the most important and recognizable element of the brand identity. It should always be positioned in a corner with a safety zone around it equal to half its size.
- A smaller version of the logo is optimized for digital visibility. Sizes are specified as follows: 50px for desktops and tablets, 30px for mobile screens, and 10mm for print materials.

**Color Information:**

- Primary colors: Orange (#FF7900), Black (#000000), and White (#FFFFFF). Secondary colors include Blue (#4BB4E6), Violet (#A885D8), Pink (#FFB4E6), Green (#50BE87), Yellow (#FFD200), and Light Gray (#333333). Color codes provided in CMYK, RGB, and HEX formats.
- The primary palette emphasizes Orange, Black, and White. The secondary palette is complementary and brightens the brand with limited use of blue, yellow, green, violet, and pink.
- Color usage rules: At least 80% of designs should use primary colors, with a minimum of 30% Orange as the emblematic color. Secondary colors must not exceed 20% of the design.

**Typography Information:**

- Typeface: Helvetica Neue. It is simple and clear, enabling focus on essential elements.
- Typography usage rules: Helvetica Neue 75 Bold is preferred. Helvetica Neue 55 Roman can be used for smaller or standard text bodies. Italics and additional bolding are prohibited. Text should appear in White, Black, or Orange (never in secondary colors).

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography guidelines: Images must reflect simplicity, proximity, and boldness in line with brand identity. Use natural lighting, colors, and saturation; avoid secondary colors. Photos should depict realistic and conversational distances.

**Spacing Layout:**

- The logo requires a safety zone equal to half its size. This reinforces visibility and prevents visual clutter around the logo.

**Brand Voice:**

- The brand language is close, simple, and bold. Avoid jargon, overly technical, promotional, or trendy language. Keep sentences concise, starting with capital letters and ending cleanly without unnecessary exclamation marks.

**Notes:**

- Icons are flat, monochrome (black, white, or orange), and follow a unified style. Orange is used for digital interaction (e.g., roll-overs, clicks). Secondary colors are avoided for icon accents.
- Circular callouts (termed 'pastilles') are always orange and can vary in size.
