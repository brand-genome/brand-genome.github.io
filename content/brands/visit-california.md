## Brand Guidelines

### 2016

**Logo Information:**

- The Visit California corporate logo is introduced as the official mark of the brand. Elements include the wave, representing the Pacific Ocean, and the 'California' logotype for leadership and confidence.
- Usage guidance: The logo should be used in instances when the objective is to position Visit California as the ultimate authority on tourism in California for audiences such as the industry, travel trade, media, or public at large.
- Other secondary corporate marks require partnership with the Corporate Communications team to ensure consistency in fonts, colors, and other elements to complement the primary logo.
- One-Color Logos: When reproduction or printing constraints prevent use of the color logo, alternative one-color blue, orange, or black logos may be used. Only approved provided electronic artwork should be used.
- Logo can be used in reverse against contrasting backgrounds like solid colors or photography, where the 'California' logotype is reversed out in white and the wave appears in Visit California Gold, ensuring high contrast.
- Logo guidelines for usage with clear space: The minimum required clear space surrounding the Visit California logo equals the x-height of the 'Visit' logotype.
- Incorrect Logo Usage: Do not add drop shadows or outer glows, alter the relationship between logotype elements, remove elements, or change to unapproved colors.

**Color Information:**

- Primary Color Palettes include: Visit California Blue (*PANTONE 7462 C for coated and 301 U for uncoated), Visit California Gold (*PANTONE 7406 C for coated and 7404 U for uncoated), and Visit California Orange (*PANTONE 716 C for coated and 152 U for uncoated). These reflect California's natural environment of blue ocean, blue sky, and golden sunshine.
- Secondary Coated Palette colors include: *PANTONE 540 C (R:0 G:48 B:87), *PANTONE 2925 C (R:0 G:174 B:239), *PANTONE 361 C (R:0 G:166 B:81), and other specific Pantone shades.
- Secondary Uncoated Palette includes PANTONE codes such as 295 U, 2915 U, 3125 U, 122 U, and accompanying CMYK and RGB values for these shades.

**Typography Information:**

- The Gotham font family is the primary typeface for corporate communications using Roman alphabets. It includes weights such as light, rounded, and condensed.
- Recommended substitution fonts for cross-platform use include Verdana with Roman alphabets.
- Accent fonts include Archer, Sentinel, Ethos, Celeste, and Lava. Each listed with usage like subheads, body text, and headlines. Details of Light, Italics, Book weights provided.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Imagery includes bold, aspirational photography of California’s diverse features. These images connect emotionally and contribute to communicating California's Dream Big message.
- Wave elements are key dynamic visual tools for design in communication materials. They should only be used under approved color schemes and must not be altered or removed.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Clear space around logo image should be a margin equivalent to the x-height of the text beneath the logo.
- When designing environmental logo applications, adequate white spaces must be preserved around the logo for clarity.

**Brand Voice:**

- The brand's tone is serious yet approachable, with an ambitious, influential, fresh, and friendly attitude. Prose is clean, concise, and thematic with a clear call to action.
- The overarching objective of Visit California is to inform and engage an audience of industry professionals, state leaders, lawmakers, and opinion leaders, ensuring the cultural and economic importance of tourism is recognized.

**Notes:**

- Digital assets and templates, including PowerPoint slides and presentations, are available to the staff online at slides.VisitCalifornia.com.
- Visit California business cards feature specific guidelines for font size, weight, and arrangement. Gotham Condensed Medium is primarily used on the front side, and backgrounds display California's landscape features.

### 2019

**Logo Information:**

- The logo™s design stays true to the fundamental characteristics of the consumer brand but incorporates several key changes to reinforce qualities of leadership, stature, and confidence.
- Logo Usage: There are two main questions to ask when determining which logo to use: 1. Who is the target audience? 2. What are the overall objectives?
- Corporate Logos: Visit California™s corporate logo should be used when the organization is a sponsor, exhibitor, presenter, and in all corporate communications.

**Color Information:**

- Guidelines for the corporate color palette focusing on vibrant, natural tones to reflect California's scenery.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary font: Open Sans. Use Bold for headlines, Regular for body text, and Light for captions.
- Typography use should be consistent throughout all corporate communications to maintain brand identity.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography should emphasize California's natural beauty, diverse cultures, and dynamic lifestyle.
- Images should use vibrant and warm tones, emphasizing outdoor landscapes and individual experiences.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Maintaining clear space around the logo: Ensure at least 'X height' spacing is maintained on all sides.
- Align elements consistently across all designs to maintain visual harmony.

**Brand Voice:**

- Visit California's tone of voice should be inviting, inspirational, and authoritative, reflecting the state's vibrant and aspirational spirit.

**Notes:**

- Business systems include standardized templates for all corporate communication such as emails, memos, and presentations, designed to ensure consistent branding.
