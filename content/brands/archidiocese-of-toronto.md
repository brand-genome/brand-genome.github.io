## Brand Guidelines

### 2013

**Logo Information:**

- The logo consists of the coat of arms with the wordmark 'Archdiocese of Toronto'. Includes multiple variations: full-color (preferred), two-color (green and black for print jobs), and one-color (for faxes, photocopying, glass etchings, etc.).
- Guidelines for minimum protected space: defined as '1x' the x-height of the wordmark. Maximum size: 5 inches wide. Minimum size: 1.35 inches wide.
- Incorrect logo usage guidelines provided (e.g., do not rotate, distort, or modify proportions). Examples of incorrect applications are included on this page.
- Preferred placement of the logo is in the bottom-right corner for most documents. Exceptions for stationery. Placement with guest logos: Archdiocesan logo bottom-right; guest logo top-left or bottom-left.
- Logos should not be downloaded from the webpage. Obtain official versions from the internal server or through authorized channels. Use proper download and resizing methods.

**Color Information:**

- Archdiocesan official colors defined using the Pantone Matching System: Red (Pantone 1788, CMYK 0|100|100|0, RGB 242|31|38), Blue (Pantone 660, CMYK 90|75|0|0, RGB 63|93|168), Yellow (Pantone 7411, CMYK 6|35|82|0, RGB 237|174|83), Green (Pantone 355, CMYK 90|15|100|3, RGB 20|152|70).
- Printing with one Pantone color should use Pantone 355 (green) or black. Colors may be adjusted for non-offset reproduction methods (e.g., silk screening or vinyl) to match Pantone as closely as possible.

**Typography Information:**

- The wordmark in the logo uses the 'Georgia' font. Address blocks on letterhead, envelopes, and business cards use 'Slate' font family.
- Recommended type sizes: Body copy uses 11pt Georgia, headers use 14pt Georgia. Style sheets are available in Word templates to ensure consistency.

**Spacing Layout:**

- At least 1x the x-height of the logo should be kept as protected space around it.

**Notes:**

- Guidance and approvals for logo or design usage must be sought from the Office of Public Relations & Communications. Email: communications@archtoronto.org.
- This document is available at www.archtoronto.org/graphicstandards for future reference.
