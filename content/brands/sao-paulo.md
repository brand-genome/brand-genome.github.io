## Brand Guidelines

### 2012

**Logo Information:**

- São Paulo brand visual identity is based on six visual elements: logo, colors, typography, secondary graphics, photography, and pattern. Individual or combined use is allowed based on application needs.
- The logo represents São Paulo's unique experiences. Element positioning can be altered but must follow guidebook rules to ensure integrity and legibility.
- Care must be taken when applying the logo on various backgrounds; colors must contrast with the backdrop. Only use color combinations from official electronic originals.
- Restricted logo versions are for exceptional use cases (due to technical limitations) and must be approved by the brand management team.
- The logo requires adherence to specifications for minimum dimensions and a non-interference area to maintain visual impact and legibility.
- Examples of inappropriate logo use include unauthorized alterations that compromise its visual impact and legibility.

**Color Information:**

- CMYK percentages are for offset quadrichromic printing in the Europa scale. RGB and Hexadecimal references are for digital media. Pantone Coated references provide colors for other applications.

**Typography Information:**

- Tipography is a highly important component of São Paulo brand. Messages communicate visually via Museo Sans, featuring multiple styles for various materials. Arial is optional for digital-only cases.

**Imagery Photography:**

- São Paulo brand photography emphasizes non-cliché, unexpected angles. Techniques such as overlays and lightpainting enhance city visuals to align with brand chromatic patterns.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Spacing rules align harmonious visual identity representing São Paulo Turismo, partners, and third parties. Alignment ensures identity preservation and requires official brand use authorization.

**Brand Voice:**

- The São Paulo brand represents an integrated visual identity for promoting the city's cultural, social, and experiential offerings with consistency in communication.
- The brand pillars under 'What are We Trying to Communicate?' focus on emotion, surprise, unique experiences, and unexpected values.
- Brand pursuit is to focus on the extraordinary, magical surprise experiences for culturally relevant messaging.
- The tagline 'Experience it All' reflects São Paulo's tourism strategy and city vibrancy. Licensed products and partnerships further the promotional utility.
