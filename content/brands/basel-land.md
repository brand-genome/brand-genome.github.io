## Brand Guidelines

### 2022

**Logo Information:**

- The logo combines the Baselbieter 'Rotstab' symbol with the text 'Basel-Landschaft.' The crossbar of the 'Rotstab' aligns with the hyphen in 'Basel-Landschaft,' forming a unified design. The logo must always appear on a white background and can be red (BL-Red) or gray (50% black), but never black to avoid confusion with Basel-Stadt. The logo text uses 'OCR-F Regular' in all caps (right-aligned). A clear zone ('x') must be maintained around the logo where no text or images can intrude.

**Color Information:**

- Color BL-Red is the most important and primary brand color and is used for the logo, titles, and highlights. BL-Gray is used for monochromatic printing where color is unavailable. Black is used for body text or graphical elements, but never for the logo. Color codes are as follows:
- BL-Red: Pantone 1795 U, CMYK 0/100/100/0, RGB 255/0/0, HEX #ff0000, RAL 3028.
- BL-Gray: CMYK 0/0/0/50, RGB 128/128/128, HEX #808080, RAL 7004.
- Black: CMYK 0/0/0/100, RGB 0/0/0, HEX #000000, RAL 9005.

**Typography Information:**

- Two font families are used in the brand:
- OCR-F Regular: Reserved for titles and highlights. Always applied in all caps and BL-Red color. It gives a modern, digital touch.
- Univers: The primary body font. 'Univers 45 Light' is used for normal text and 'Univers 65 Bold' for emphasis. Univers is a readable font known for its elegant simplicity.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Logo spacing rules specify that a clear zone ('x') must be maintained around the logo where no text or images can intrude.

**Notes:**

- The logo design and elements are legally protected with restricted use. The 'OCR-F Regular' text in the logo is right-aligned to emphasize 'Landschaft,' highlighting the individuality of the canton.
- The corporate design incorporates elements reflecting tradition while being modern and practical. Implementation targets were set for 2016 with allowances for exceptions.
