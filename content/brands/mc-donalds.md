### 2012

**Logo Information:**

- Always use the masterbrand version of the Golden Arches and include 'i'm lovin' it' as a logo lock-up or in the design.
- Logo readability can be enhanced by applying a drop shadow. For busy images, use a red background to support logo readability.
- Clear space around The Master Brand logo should be at least 'X'.
- Logo usage rules include: don't change the scale, recolor, outline, add shadow, recreate, retype 'i'm lovin' it', or distort the logo.

**Color Information:**

- Red is the primary branding color for McDonald's. It is recommended for OOH advertising as it complements most product visuals.
- Green backgrounds are suitable for fresh produce such as salads; blue backgrounds are recommended for desserts and cold drinks.
- Recommended colors include: Red for hot food and drinks, Blue for cold drinks and desserts, and Green for salads and fresh produce.
- Primary gradients should serve as accents, with emphasis around edges only.
- Avoid dark gradients that negatively affect mood and branding consistency.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary type family: Berthold Akzidenz Grotesk, including regular, bold, extended, condensed, and extra bold variants. Used for clean and readable OOH messaging.
- Recommended typography practices for OOH: Use medium weight for product names, maintain smooth and even letter spacing, avoid mixed weights, and ensure type is readable at a distance.
- Pricing treatments: Use Akzidenz Bold for price points. Price circles can enhance readability.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Fruit and salads are showcased on green backgrounds to enhance the appeal of fresh produce. Blue enhances the refreshing qualities of cold drinks and desserts.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Clear space around The Master Brand logo should be at least 'X', considering proportions 'Y' and 'Z' for balanced spacing.

**Brand Voice:**

- Ensure the tagline 'i'm lovin' it' is positioned as a solution to the thought posed in messaging, rather than starting the storytelling.
- The 'i'm lovin' it' script, using the Brisa typeface, should be incorporated when the Master Brand logo is part of a visual concept.

**Notes:**

- The brand's consistent use of the red color reinforces identity, especially in outdoor advertising (OOH), enhancing product visibility.
- Adding gradients to backgrounds can increase visual interest without excessive distraction, provided accents are limited to edges.

### 2009

**Logo Information:**

- Logo designed with red and yellow colors to symbolize freshness and the use of natural ingredients. Red conveys boldness and approachability, while yellow invokes hunger and serves as a call-to-action.
- Golden arches symbol iconically represent McDonald's while balancing flexibility for global market variations. Noted for its distinctiveness and its ability to invoke curiosity about the brand.
- Pantone colors used are PANTONE 485 for red and PANTONE 123 for yellow. CMYK values: Red (C:0, M:100, Y:91, K:0), Yellow (C:0, M:30, Y:94, K:0).
- Incorrect usage examples and reminders for not using logos in certain cases or for specific product advertisements.
- Icon-only, lettermark logotype, wordmark logotype mentioned as design trends influencing McDonald’s logo structures and styles.

**Color Information:**

- Primary colors: Red (PANTONE 485, C:0, M:100, Y:91, K:0), and Yellow (PANTONE 123, C:0, M:30, Y:94, K:0).
- Black and 30% screen variations mentioned for other visual purposes.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary fonts used: Arial and Helvetica Black for logo designs and textual identity.
- Supporting fonts mentioned: Handel Gothic Bold, Century Gothic, and Handel Gothic Medium for textual content complementary to logos.

**Imagery Photography:**

- The document does not provide explicit photographic guidelines but focuses on design elements and colors that work effectively together.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Grid systems and standard graphic elements to ensure logo sizing and adaptability for large-scale applications.

**Brand Voice:**

- McDonald’s communicates freshness, approachability, and an invitation to its offerings through choice of colors (red and yellow) and design elements.
- Positioning as a global brand bridging culture and commerce with an American origin but widespread popularity.

**Notes:**

- Comparison with other logos is noted (e.g., Burger King, Jollibee) highlighting strengths and weaknesses of illustration-based and text-based designs. McDonald's notably uses text-based designs.
- Design inspirations (Sci-fi font trends, iconic logotypes) contribute to McDonald's overall branding approach.
