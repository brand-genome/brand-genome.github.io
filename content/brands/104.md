## Brand Guidelines

### 2008

**Logo Information:**

- The logo uses three elemental geometric forms: line, circle, and triangle, symbolizing early-industrial shapes. Variations include 12 combinations of number logos and word logos, available in Light, Medium, Bold, and Extra Bold weights.
- Logos are flexible and designed for regular variation (e.g., weight changes from Light, Medium to Bold to represent work-in-progress).
- File formats for logos are provided in EPS files, such as 104_num_light.eps, 104_txt_bold.eps.
- Number logos can only be used if accompanying text ('Le 104 Cent Quatre') appears nearby. Otherwise, word logos must be used to represent the brand.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary typeface is Futura, designed in 1927 by Paul Renner. It is a geometric sans-serif font described as both modern and early-industrial. Variations include Light, Medium, Bold, and Extra Bold.
- Futura integrates custom shapes from the logo: triangles and lines replacing certain characters like 'A' and '1'.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Logo dimensions on all materials are 30 mm in height. Placement is standardized with detailed margins: e.g., 10 mm from edges for letterheads and large items, 5-7 mm for smaller items like business cards and A5/A6 cards.
