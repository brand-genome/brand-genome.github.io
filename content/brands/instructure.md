## Brand Guidelines

### 2021

**Logo Information:**

- Mentions about logo usage, including clear space rules and proper guidelines, as a refresher for employees and contractors.
- Horizontal usage of logo and its colors, clear space requirements, and minimum size of 35px. Details on friendly logo placement on light background and prohibited modifications.

**Color Information:**

- Details regarding the primary and secondary colors of the brand. Primary colors are blue (PMS 2149), red (PMS 179), and orange (PMS 144). Secondary colors include PMS 446 C, PMS 421 C, PMS 427 C, PMS 3258 C, PMS 213 C, PMS 7700 C, PMS 2915 C, and PMS 109 C. Usage emphasizes white space and matching palette pie distributions.
- Instructions on color usage including 'do not use colors other than listed options' and avoidance of secondary colors for headers, body copy in color, and mixing too many colors/gradients.

**Typography Information:**

- Proxima Nova is the primary typeface with Arial as backup. Tiempos Headline is a serif typeface for accents, numbers, and quotes. Examples show headline, subhead, extra bold fonts, and contrast fonts for accessibility. Emphasizes clarity and ease of legibility using fonts related to education and development.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography should be simple, strong, and highlight optimism and humanity. Use real-life photos of users impacted by Instructure products, or thoughtful, diverse stock images. Composition and perspectives should feel unique. Black & white, color, or gradient overlays are suggested styles.

**Brand Voice:**

- Voice aligns with Instructure's values: ownership, relationships, openness, simplicity. Key characteristics: Smart (data-informed, logical), Inspiring (optimistic, forward-thinking), Helpful (anticipates questions), Unconventional (humorous, creative, straightforward).
- Tone is situation-dependent but consistent with brand voice. Recommended tones: Observant, Knowledgeable (not too technical), Motivational, Consultative (tailored, non-pushy), Conversational (uses casual styles like idioms), and Direct (concise without abrasiveness).

**Notes:**

- Examples of stylistic conventions including Oxford commas, guidelines for ampersands, bulleted lists, dashes, and acronyms. Encourages adherence to specific tone/style for company consistency.
