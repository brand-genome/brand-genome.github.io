## Brand Guidelines

### 2014

**Logo Information:**

- Primary logo with descriptor should be used whenever possible. Clean version of the logo (without descriptor, 'Voyages | Travel') may only be used with approval. The 'icon-only' logo is reserved for social media and small spaces.
- Color versions of the logo include: PMS 485 + 3252 colors, black, and reversed variants over white.
- For co-branding, the Vision logo appears 15% larger than partner logos. A .25 pt keyline separates the logos.
- Logo clear space rules: The logo must maintain an 'x' unit of clear space around all sides.
- The minimum size of the logo with descriptor is 1 inch, wordmark-only is 0.5 inches, and icon-only is 0.25 inches.
- Prohibited alterations to the logo include changing the size, rotating, using at a smaller size than specified, outlining letters, altering placement of descriptor or wordmark, changing colors, stretching the logo, adding a drop shadow, or placing the logo inside an unapproved box.

**Color Information:**

- Primary colors: Vision Red (Pantone 485, HEX #CC3333, RGB 255, 35, 30, CMYK 5, 100, 100, 0), Vision Blue (Pantone 3252, HEX #00CCCC, RGB 25, 205, 200, CMYK 65, 0, 30, 0), White (HEX #FFFFFF, RGB 255, 255, 255, CMYK 0, 0, 0, 0), Black (HEX #000000, RGB 0, 0, 0, CMYK 0, 0, 0, 100).
- Supplemental colors include a monochromatic palette: 20% Grey (HEX #D1D3D4), 40% Grey (HEX #A7A9AC), 60% Grey (HEX #808285), 80% Grey (HEX #58595B).
- Color usage ratios for corporate communications: 25% Vision Red, 25% Vision Blue, 25% Black, and 40% White.
- Color usage ratios for leisure communications: Vision Blue 25%, Vision Red 5%, Black 25%, White 40%.
- Color guides for applications: Vision Blue is favored for leisure contexts, while Vision Red dominates corporate, meetings, and events contexts.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary typeface: Proxima Nova (weights: Light for headings/subheadings or creative typography, Regular/Semibold for body text/callouts). Optical kerning is used, with -10pt tracking for heading styles.
- Fallback typeface: Arial Regular. Bold weight may be used to highlight critical text.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Vision's imagery adopts a photojournalistic style, aiming to convey personal, emotional travel moments. Leisure imagery focuses on spontaneity, adventure, and amplified natural color with slightly askew angles and tight cropping.
- Corporate brand imagery focuses on stress-free moments in corporate travel, avoiding direct eye contact with the camera and inviting the viewer into a candid scene.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Horizons and globes are primary graphic treatments. Their color, scale, and orientation can change based on design requirements.
- Cropping of globes or horizons can create a dynamic on-brand visual language while accommodating time or design constraints.

**Brand Voice:**

- Brand attributes: Vision promises a personalized, responsive, and passionate approach to travel, emphasizing expertise and efficiency.
- Campaign theming centers around the tagline 'Seeing Travel Your Way', highlighting a focus on personal, traveler-centric experiences.

**Notes:**

- The Vision brand identity is designed to work flexibly across applications while maintaining brand consistency and recognition, using a unified visual system called the Brand Palette.
- For co-branded applications, specific spacing rules and size proportions between Vision and partner logos are to be followed.
- Corporate and leisure applications are distinguished through color and imagery. Leisure applications use Vision Blue and vibrant, colorful imagery, while corporate ones use Vision Red and professional, stress-free moments.
- Vision typography prioritizes simplicity and clarity, with specific typeface usage rules to maintain consistency in written communication.
- Vision emphasizes systematic use of horizons, globes, and well-balanced white space to create modern, dynamic graphic touchpoints.
