## Brand Guidelines

### 2020

**Logo Information:**

- The Be Equal logo is derived from Paul Rand's Eye-Bee-M 'Rebus' poster design. It uses a 4:3 proportion.
- There is no formal 'lockup' of the Be Equal logo and logotype. They can be used independently or together. When combined, the logotype should appear first, above or to the left of the logo, and sized relative to each other.
- The Be Equal logo and logotype may only appear on backgrounds with 60 or 40 color values. When displayed on these backgrounds, they should appear in black or white for proper contrast.
- Color combinations for the logo should be from different color families and values. Never use colors of the same family or value together.

**Color Information:**

- The Be Equal color palette is derived from IBM Design Language's overall palette. It includes designated colors such as Purple 80/60/40/20, Magenta 80/60/40/20, Teal 80/60/40/20, and others. Colors follow accessibility practices and can be used only in values of 40 or 60 for logotype backgrounds.
- Pattern colors represent specific Be Equal Communities: Black, Women, Hispanic, Veterans, Pan-Asian, Indigenous, LGBT+, and DiversABLE.

**Typography Information:**

- IBM Plex Sans Bold is used for Be-Statements, Communities, and the Be Equal logotype in Title Case. IBM Plex Sans Regular and IBM Plex Serif Regular are secondary type choices, with the latter reserved for first-person quotations.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Portraits of IBMers follow a democratic, consistent style, encouraging individuality. Technical specifications are available for creating images worldwide.
- Photo overlays have been designed consistently for each Be Equal Community. These overlays allow for palette customization.

**Spacing Layout:**

- The IBM 2x Grid is used as the framework for all visual elements and typography within the Be Equal system. It ensures alignment and consistency.
- Cropping styles such as Side-by-Side, Centered, and Hand-in-Hand are used in compositions.

**Brand Voice:**

- 'Be-' Statements like 'Be Bold', 'Be Honest', and 'Be Inclusive' represent diversity and intersectionality. These statements pair with the visual identity and should not exist without the Be Equal brand.
- Specific verbal identity rules include pairing 'Be Equal' branding with statements like 'IBM is a Be Equal employer.' or 'Paul Papas is a Be Equal Ally.' Avoid unique slogans for individual communities.

**Notes:**

- Advice and approvals for Be Equal brand materials can be obtained from the core team. Available contacts include Joy Dettorre (joy_dettorre@us.ibm.com) and Todd Simmons (simmonst@us.ibm.com).
- Official Be Equal resources are accessible on beequal.com, ibm.com/impact/be-equal, and related IBM properties.
