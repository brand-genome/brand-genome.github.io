## Brand Guidelines

### UNKNOWN

**Logo Information:**

- The Google for Work logo consists of the Google logo in four colors paired with a 'for Work' modifier. It must not be modified, altered, or distorted in any way.
- Product lockups follow similar rules to the main logo. They may appear with monochrome type and icon (lockups) or with the word 'Google' in 4-color typography.
- Google for Work logos integrate with other brands like Chrome, Android, Education, and Cloud Platform.
- Clear space must be equal to at least one x-height of the logo on all sides. Minimum size is set at 22px (0.175 inches).
- Color variations include full-color, one-color 54% black for light backgrounds, and one-color reverse for darker backgrounds. Appropriate variations should be used to maintain contrast.
- Logo misuse examples include altering the 'for Work' configuration, distorting or rotating the logo, changing proportions, modifying typeface, adding effects, and creating animated versions.

**Color Information:**

- Primary colors include: Gray (#757575, PMS Cool Gray 8), Blue (#4285F4, PMS 2173), Red (#EA4335, PMS 179), Yellow (#FBBC05, PMS 7408), Green (#34A853, PMS 7738). RGB and CMYK values are provided for all colors.

**Typography Information:**

- The primary typeface is Roboto, featuring geometric and open forms in various weights including Thin, Light, Regular, Medium, Bold, and Black with oblique variations.
- The alternate typeface is Arial, for use only when Roboto is unavailable. Arial should not be used in print.
- Type hierarchy: Headlines must be at least twice the size of body copy, with leading at 1.2x the font size. Body copy uses Roboto Light in Google Gray.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Use one-color logo variations on photographic backgrounds for better contrast. 54% black logo is suitable for light photos, while reversed logos are for medium to dark photos.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Clear space around the logo must be at least one x-height from the closest object on all sides.

**Brand Voice:**

- The brand voice is inspiring, empowering, and focused on end-users. It avoids directives, jargon, and ego, and instead emphasizes collaboration, exploration, transparency, and innovation.
- Guidelines for tone: Simple, smart, surprising, fun, and useful. Content should focus on end-user needs, avoid over-explaining, and emphasize the 'magic' of the products naturally.

**Notes:**

- Tagline 'Gone Google' emphasizes transformation and betterment for businesses adopting Google tools. Past tense ('Gone Google') and present tense ('Going Google') depend on the context of adoption.
- The vision statement 'Work the way you live' is a macro promise and should only appear in internal use cases like presentations and speaking notes.
