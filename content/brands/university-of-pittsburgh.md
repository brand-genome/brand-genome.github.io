## Brand Guidelines

### 2024

**Logo Information:**

- The University of Pittsburgh has three primary logos: the Institutional Mark (formal logo including the shield and signature), the Informal Mark (streamlined shorthand name logo), and the Spirit Mark (Pitt Script for athletics and student-focused initiatives).
- The Institutional Mark must maintain clear space measured by the height of the 'P' in 'Pittsburgh' (applies to all versions except subbranding). Minimum size requirements: Digital: 220px; Print: 1.25inch.
- Color versions of the Institutional Mark are available as three-color (Pitt Royal, Pitt Gold with white shield), reverse two-color (white type option), and single-color marks.
- Prohibited usages for the Institutional Mark: No distortion, rotation (other than 90°), recoloring, drop shadows, outlines, improper contrasts, element rearrangements, or additional elements.
- The Spirit Mark (Pitt Script) is available as a two-color and single-color mark, used primarily for athletics and nonacademic promotions. Restrictions include: No use in connection with tobacco, drug paraphernalia, sexually explicit materials, weapons, or gambling; not combined with other marks; not altered or modified.
- Cobranding of Institutional Mark and Spirit Mark is allowed on multi-sided items (e.g., front/back of a shirt) or as a repeating pattern, ensuring balance and consistency. Layout practices are outlined for co-branding creativity.

**Color Information:**

- Primary colors: Pitt Royal (HEX: #003594, RGB: 0/53/148, CMYK: 100/75/0/6, Pantone 661C) and Pitt Gold (HEX: #FFB81C, RGB: 255/184/28, CMYK: 0/31/98/0, Pantone 1235C).
- Secondary colors include New Sky (#DBEEFF), Gray (#C8C9C7), White (#FFFFFF), Medium Blue (#00205B), and Bronze (#B87333) with CMYK and RGB values.
- Accent colors: Golden Haze (#FFE939), Light Blue (#66B2E3), Light Green (#00AD6E), Orange (#F7911E), Infrared (#FF5B45), White Peach (#FFDECD), Merlot (#770538), and Black (#000000).
- Color hierarchy: Primary colors are used for large elements and to maintain brand recognition; secondary colors support primary palettes (e.g., subheadings); accent colors enhance visuals sparingly (e.g., icons), with no more than four accent colors at once outside of data visualizations.
- Accent colors can be paired with primary and secondary colors to enhance visual clarity through contrast and hierarchy.

**Typography Information:**

- Alternate Gothic Compressed is the primary hero typeface used in all caps for impactful designs. It features weights including Black, Heavy, Bold, Demi, Medium, and Regular.
- Instrument Serif is a serif typeface emulating the Cathedral of Learning architecture and used for sophisticated typographic needs. It is unsuitable for body copy and available via Adobe and Google Fonts.
- Supporting fonts: Alternate Gothic for subheads, Merriweather for body copy and readability, and Roboto for practical sans-serif usage across digital and physical mediums.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Images must stay authentic, avoiding excessive editing. Photos should reflect diversity (genders, races, ethnicities). Balance subject matter including student life, academics, athletics, and faculty imagery.
- Photo content should align with brand personality words: Inquisitive, Transformative, Approachable, Collaborative, and Determined.

**Spacing Layout:**

- The Pitt Shield Pattern can be used as an edge element in design layouts for structure and flexibility; consistent usage ensures alignment with brand identity.
- Clear space around Institutional Marks is determined using the height of the 'P' in 'Pittsburgh,' ensuring unimpeded visibility and consistency.

**Brand Voice:**

- Tone words defining the brand include: Inquisitive, Transformative, Approachable, Collaborative, and Determined. The voice should adapt to audiences but remain grounded and approachable.
- The creative platform revolves around 'Hail to Possible,' serving as a rallying cry and extension of the University's brand purpose.

**Notes:**

- License and trademark usage, including graphic approvals, require a review by the Assistant Athletic Director for Licensing and Branding.
- Contact for brand inquiries or guidelines: brand@pitt.edu.
