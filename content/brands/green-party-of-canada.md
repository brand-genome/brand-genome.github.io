## Brand Guidelines

### 2025

**Logo Information:**

- A bold, green circle represents a thriving, sustainable planet and the universal call to action: Go Green. Logo represents simplicity, democracy, and inclusivity.
- The Green and VERT secondary symbol can be used for special activations. [Logo variant example mentioned]

**Color Information:**

- Primary color palette inspired by nature includes Verdant, Moss, Egg. Verdant Pantone 2422C, Moss, and HEX #20A242 are suggested replacements for black/white in logo contexts. HEX #20A242 used for web-compliant designs.
- Full palette: Verdant (Pantone 2422C), Moss, Egg, Sunrise, Petal, Ice, Gulf, Marigold, Black (restricted). Includes HEX, RGB, CMYK values for each color.

**Typography Information:**

- GP Neue Montreal font customized by Pangram Pangram foundry (Sans-serif), inspired by Expo 67 Montreal designs. Party type set in all caps for bilingual equal punctuation.
- Typography pairing: GP Neue Montreal (modern sans-serif) with Gelica (heritage-inspired). GP Neue Montreal substitutes: Helvetica/Arial for everyday communication.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Illustrations designed by Cecilia Castelli and Francesco Ciccolella. Art supports storytelling themes tied to the Green Party's election platform (inspired by brand workshops).
- Leverage Green Party brand elements with art mix to create visual contrast and connect uniquely with specific audiences.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Different candidate branding templates available for signs, cards, flyers. Templates designed for lengthy names use semi-squeezed type.

**Brand Voice:**

- Tone emphasizes action, inclusivity, and clarity. Reinforced via consistent visual branding on stationery and communication design assets.
- Professionalism embodied through letter templates and advocacy messaging e.g., addressing political leadership with constructive dialogue.
