## Brand Guidelines

### 2010

**Logo Information:**

- The Shell Pecten symbol must appear in red and yellow with a white keyline (even on white backgrounds).
- For black-and-white ads, use the black Pecten with a white keyline or reversed white on dark backgrounds.
- The Primary Lock-up replaces the Wave Branding Zone and consists of the Pecten Tile and Energy Tile. It provides flexibility in positioning but must not let the Pecten touch the edge.
- Incorrect logo usage includes using the outdated 'Wave/Branding Zone' as per visual examples.

**Color Information:**

- Primary colors include Shell Yellow (Pantone 116 C: #F2C830, CMYK 0 14 100 0), Shell Red (Pantone 485 C: #DA291C, CMYK 0 95 100 0), and White (#FFFFFF, CMYK 0 0 0 0).
- The supporting palette consists of 10 secondary colors, while the neutral palette includes Black, PMS Cool Grey 10C, and neutrals using 80%, 60%, and 40% black.
- Primary and supporting colors can be applied at 20%, 40%, and 60% tints.

**Typography Information:**

- Four weights of Futura typeface are recommended for flexibility within the same style (Light, Book, Medium, Bold). Verdana is specified for HTML text.

**Spacing Layout:**

- The grid system organizes layout elements such as icons and typography. The Primary Lock-up is positioned on 4x2 grid squares.
- Layout sizes include Half Letter (8.5" x 5.5"), Letter (8.5" x 11"), and Tabloid (11" x 17") all aligned to grid squares.

**Brand Voice:**

- The visual identity aims to drive consistency and coherence across all Shell communications, making them appear as coming from the same company.

**Notes:**

- The Pecten remains unchanged as the symbol of Shell's brand. It must be used in its own free space wherever possible.
- The Energy Tile emphasizes Shell brand power through its yellow color.
- The branding transition from the Wave to the Primary Lock-up reflects an updated structural approach to Shell identity.
