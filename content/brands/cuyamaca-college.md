## Brand Guidelines

### 2020

**Logo Information:**

- Primary logo in full color and reverse versions. Secondary logo also presented in both color and reversed formats. Minimum size and clear space rules defined. Clear space is specified as '1/3 x,' where 'x' equals the size of the logo. Minimum size requirements are also provided for various logo elements, such as '5/16', '5/8', and '3/4' measurements.
- Approved use specifies only one logo for Cuyamaca College academic, administrative, and business offices.
- Restrictions on logo use include not altering logo proportions, rotation, adding effects, or mismatched typeface. Background must not distract.

**Color Information:**

- College brand colors presented: Blue (CMYK: C100 M96 Y36 K32; RGB: R31 G36 B84; HEX: #1F2454), Tan (CMYK: C5 M1 Y18 K0; RGB: R240 G243 B214; HEX: #F0F3D6), Black (CMYK: C75 M68 Y67 K90; RGB: R0 G0 B0; HEX: #000000), White (CMYK: C0 M0 Y0 K0; RGB: R255 G255 B255; HEX: #FFFFFF).

**Typography Information:**

- Primary typeface specified as the Scala Font Family for main components of the brand identity.
- Secondary typeface specified as Scala Sans Font Family, likely used for supporting visual hierarchy.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Clear space around the logos is defined as 1/3 of 'x,' measured relative to logo size for consistent spacing.

**Notes:**

- Logo proportions, colors, and typefaces must remain unchanged. Rotation, distortion, or added effects are explicitly prohibited.

### 2005

**Logo Information:**

- The primary version of the Cuyamaca College identity consists of the logo and the 'Cuyamaca College' typographic signature. A horizontal lockup is presented as the secondary version for space or design constraints.
- Clearspace rule: A clearspace of 1/3 of the identity's height must be maintained around the logo. This also applies to edges of any application.
- Minimum size requirement: The Cuyamaca College identity must never be smaller than 3/4” wide and 9/16” high.
- Logo color options include formal two-color identity with PMS 2768 (C100, M83, Y0, K56) and PMS 614 (C0, M0, Y15, K6), or a less formal one-color version in PMS 2768 or black.
- Do's for logo usage: The logo must not interfere or compete with other design elements, should not have drop shadows or a 3D look, and must be resized proportionally when resized.
- Don'ts for logo usage: Do not dismantle, alter, or create new artwork using components of the logo. Avoid reversing the identity out of the background.

**Color Information:**

- Primary identity colors: PMS 2768 (C100, M83, Y0, K56) and PMS 614 (C0, M0, Y15, K6).
- Complementary background colors for print collateral with PMS and CMYK values provided for Pantone colors 471, 273, 556, 544, 437, 154, 434, 5585, 4535, 475, 5445, 727.
- For web environments, the RGB screen equivalent of PMS 2768 is R19, G23, B32.5. Complementary background colors are provided in RGB values and hexadecimal.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary fonts: Scala for identity typography. Typefaces such as Arial and Times New Roman may also be used in practical applications like letterheads and memos.
- Font sizes include Scala 10pt with 12pt leading for content and Scala Sans 8pt for signature blocks in applications like memos and letterheads.

**Imagery Photography:**

- The college identity must be distinct in all applications and should stand alone with sufficient clear space. Avoid using competing or cluttered backgrounds.

**Spacing Layout:**

- All applications must maintain a clearspace of 1/3 of the identity's height.
- Margins for standard memo/fax layouts: Top margin of 2.25”, left and right margins of 1”.

**Brand Voice:**

- "Learning for the Future" is the tagline associated with Cuyamaca College.
- Tone reflects professionalism and establishes the college as a high-caliber institution of higher learning.

**Notes:**

- Usage of mascot and seal: The mascot is not the main identifier but represents the student body. The college seal is for official documents like diplomas.
- Vendors should contact the Graphics Office at (619) 660-4413 to obtain official logos and ensure adherence to guidelines.
- Electronic versions of logos and the manual (in various formats) are available in the public folder in Outlook under the images subfolder.
