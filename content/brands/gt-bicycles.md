## Brand Guidelines

### 2014

**Logo Information:**

- The wings logo must be used in all advertising, websites, and point-of-purchase (POP) displays to represent the GT brand. The primary execution of the GT Bicycles logo is in Pantone 109C, white or black. The yellow wings version is mandatory when appearing on photos. Additional wings may feature secondary executions.
- The GT 'Race' logo is used for product-related collateral, such as bike graphics, packaging, and parts. It is not to be used as the primary logo for marketing materials but can be used as a corporate logo for internal communication.
- ‘Earn Your Wings’ lockup is used in campaign material in Pantone 109C. It must not be resized or altered.
- The GT Vintage Circle Logo is a homage to the original BMX head badge. It can be used as an accessory brand element (e.g., product graphics, on-site graphics) but only when supported by the appropriate brand logo.
- Do's and Don’ts of the logo: Logos should not be distorted, recolored, or placed in low-contrast areas, and should only use approved executions of the GT, Race, or Circular logos. The minimum clear space around the logo should be maintained at all times.

**Color Information:**

- The primary brand colors include Pantone 109C Yellow, White, and Black. There is a strong emphasis on consistency in the use of these colors across all GT materials.
- Pantone 109C is mandatory for the primary logo execution and headlines in campaigns.

**Typography Information:**

- Baskerville Bold should be used in capitals for headline treatments in ‘Earn Your Wings’ campaign assets. Use Pantone 109C for the primary headlines.
- Baskerville Regular is used for the body copy in 'Earn Your Wings' materials. Text should only be in sentence case.
- Georgia is a system font that is used in Web HTML, Microsoft Word, and Excel documents as a substitute for Baskerville when necessary. Use Georgia Bold for headlines and Georgia Regular for body text.
- Typography hierarchy suggests body copy and call-to-action text should be approximately 40% of the headline height in layouts. Examples are provided on page 26.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography should portray the essence of GT: action-oriented, showing speed and power. Ad visuals should feature GT riders, expressing boldness and the brand's identity.
- BMX Marketing assets must prominently feature the GT BMX Logo and align with the GT photographic style guidelines.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Body copy and Call-To-Action should be approximately 40% of the headline height in layouts. Always respect the minimum spacing guidelines when placing logos or brand elements.

**Brand Voice:**

- The GT brand tone of voice is casual, conversational, approachable, and never preachy or authoritative. It speaks the language of riders reflecting authenticity and independence.
- Messaging focuses on surpassing limits and relies on themes of strength, independence, action, and a fearless, confident attitude.
- Advertising headlines and support copy must be written in the GT Brand Voice, including a clear call-to-action that invites the audience to learn more at gtbicycles.com.
