## Brand Guidelines

### 2017

**Logo Information:**

- The Capgemini logo consists of two parts: the wordmark and the spade symbol. The wordmark is inspired by Serge Kampf's handwriting, making the logo a signature expression of humanity and commitment. The spade symbol is an iconic representation that has historically symbolized value and success.
- Clear space rules: The exclusion zone around the Capgemini logo must be equal to the width of the spade symbol. In extreme circumstances where space is restricted, the exclusion zone can be reduced to 50% of the spade's width.
- Minimum size rules: The logo must not be displayed smaller than 30mm in print or 175px on screens.
- Logo variant rules: The primary two-color logo is preferred. Single-color versions are available in gray, black, and white. These are to be used when production methods limit the primary logo's quality.
- Logo positioning guidelines: The logo should ideally be placed on the top left or top right of layouts for consistent visibility. Centered positioning is used for special cases such as signage or exhibition stands.
- The relationship between the logo and slogan: The slogan 'People matter, results count' should always appear with the logo, aligned horizontally or vertically, and use the Capgemini blue color in Ubuntu Medium font with tracking -20.
- Incorrect logo usage examples: Do not outline, redraw, apply drop shadows, resize components disproportionately, recolor, box the logo, distort, or add patterns to the logo. Refrain from using the wordmark without the spade symbol.
- The spade symbol can be used independently for specific use cases like favicons, app icons, or business cards when space is insufficient for the full logo. However, it is not a replacement for the full logo and must not overlap with it.
