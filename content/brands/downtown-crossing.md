## Brand Guidelines

### 2009

**Logo Information:**

- Rules for logo spacing: Appropriate spacing around logos should always be the height of the letter 'D'.
- Incorrect logo usage includes using patterns or photographs within the logo, changing colors, and breaking clear space guidelines.
- Examples of logo application on construction barriers, signs, books, and promotional products.

**Color Information:**

- Color codes with CMYK details for colors. Colors include Pantone DS6-4C: C:10 M:10 Y:90 K:0, and others in various tonal ranges.
- Meet Dots color palette provided with tonal range specifications based on brand-approved colors.
- Do's and Don'ts for using brand colors in patterns: Don't change colors or disrupt proportions; only approved patterns and their colors should be used.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary typeface Leitura, a serif font, available in 4 weights and italics. Usage across body text, small type, and headlines.
- Leitura News, a serif font optimized for >12pt sizes and large headlines with a classic newspaper feel.
- Leitura Sans, a sans-serif clean typeface, available in 4 weights and italics for a modern and versatile tone.
- Leitura Display for headlines, a serif with decorative swashes, should not be used below 15pt size.
- Leitura Headline, heavy and condensed sans-serif, suitable for impactful stories but sparingly used.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography must align with 3 styles: Documentary (natural environments), Portraiture (diverse, energetic individuals), and Street Portraiture (diverse people in area-specific settings).
- Illustrations showcase future development potential. Access to illustrations requires specific authorization.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Measurements for logo spacing should be based on the 'D' height for proportional consistency.

**Brand Voice:**

- Copy tone is friendly, optimistic, and conversational reflecting Downtown Crossing as Boston's meeting place.
- Key messaging includes 'Meet <Blank>' phrases, representing inclusiveness and diversity. Avoid abbreviations like 'DTX' or 'DTC'.
- No tagline is to be used; strict brand naming rules dictate using 'Downtown Crossing' only.

**Notes:**

- Usage of brand elements requires explicit consent from Boston Redevelopment Authority and Downtown Crossing Partnership.
- Strict adherence to these guidelines is mandatory; any modifications must receive prior approval.
