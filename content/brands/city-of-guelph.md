## Brand Guidelines

### 2009

**Logo Information:**

- Vector-based logo file formats include .eps and .ai. Bitmap file formats include .tif and .jpg at a resolution of 266–350 dpi for high-quality printing.
- Guidelines for placement: The logo should usually appear either at the top or bottom right on documents when in doubt. Clear space rules must be followed.
- Two-color logo for embroidery: Green = LT Green 5510, Blue = Teal 4103; minimum size 3.5 inches wide. Screen printing minimum size: 2.5 inches wide.
- Incorrect usage of Crest: Do NOT show the crest without logotype, adjust colors or proportions.

**Color Information:**

- Pantone color codes for promotional items: Green = Pantone 369 C, Blue = Pantone 7460 C. Recommended background colors include black, navy blue, white, and beige.
- City crest official colors: Four-color process including Pantone Blue 286 and Black. Vector format required for scalability and minimum one-inch width.

**Typography Information:**

- Corporate typefaces: Garamond and Verdana. Recommended email signature font: Verdana 10 pt for Outlook and Courier 11 pt for Blackberry.
- Service identifiers should use Verdana Bold for primary text and Pantone 7460 Blue for printed materials.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Promotional graphics included guidelines for embroidery and screen printing. Embroidery suitable for clothing, cloth bags, hats; screen printing suitable for drinking vessels and glassware.
- City of Guelph promotional program logos should be placed on the left-front side of program materials. Logo optional for right sleeves.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Minimum clear space and placement rules for logos depend on document type but general guidance suggests top or bottom right corners maintaining consistency.

**Brand Voice:**

- Website promotion guidelines: guelph.ca should be bold-faced and clearly visible on all documents alongside contact information.

**Notes:**

- The City crest is strictly for official communications from the Mayor's Office and should not be used for public information materials, corporate ads, or brochures.
- Promotional items must adhere to embroidery/screen printing guidelines and recommended colors. Enterprise units are encouraged to use their standalone logos.
