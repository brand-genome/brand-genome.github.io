## Brand Guidelines

### 2018

**Logo Information:**

- Logo file naming system includes configurations for primary, horizontal (2-line and 1-line), centered, and wordmark-only variations. Color options include primary (gold and blue), all-blue, all-black, reversed white, and gold/white combinations. File formats include EPS, PNG, JPG, and WMF.
- Bank Gothic font with slight alterations is used in the Northern Arizona University logo.
- Reversed logo versions include gold and white type with a gold mountain graphic and an all-white option.
- The primary university logo must be used for on-campus signage when space allows. In cases of limited space, horizontal or alternate unit configurations may be used. Clear guidelines for branding interior signage and placement.
- Restrictions for placement and permission to use secondary logos alongside official NAU logos, strictly controlled by University Marketing policies.

**Color Information:**

- Primary coloration includes blue (Pantone 281) type and gold mountain elements. Variations supported include all-black, all-blue, reversed white, and reversed gold/white.
- Specific guidelines for single-colored imprint items require a single-color 'True Blue' (Pantone 281) version of the institutional logo.
- Reversed coloring, including all-white and combinations of gold and white, are for use over dark backgrounds.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary institutional font for branding is 'Industry', used with Bold Italic for headlines and Book for body copy.
- Undergraduate recruitment font includes 'Stenciletta Solid Bold' for titles, with 'Roboto Regular' and 'Roboto Bold' for supplementary text.
- Additional campaign fonts for titles may include customization for audiences in specific outreach contexts, captured with welcoming tones.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Guidelines emphasize appropriate banner sizes and locations for displays on campus. Locations must be aesthetically pleasing and avoid historic district buildings.

**Notes:**

- All digitized materials shared must meet accessibility standards, adhering to ADA and Section 504. Accessibility resources are available through the Usable Materials Center.
- Publication equal opportunity statements for university branding include required language in job postings and marketing materials.
- University-trademarked merchandising requires vetted usage approvals and adherence to specific vendor licensing agreements.
