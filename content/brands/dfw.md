## Brand Guidelines

### 2016

**Logo Information:**

- The DFW logo represents key brand elements such as clarity and the idea of connection. The primary logo version uses DFW Orange and can be used on most touchpoints, especially inside the airport. Secondary logo versions add the full name, 'Dallas Fort Worth International Airport,' and are available in black and reversed versions.
- Clear space guidelines mandate a minimum of 1.75x the journey line's thickness. The DFW logo uses a proportional relationship to ensure clarity within its usage.
- The logo variations include a primary logo, secondary logo with additional context, and monochrome (black and white or reversed) options. Guidelines emphasize maintaining whitespace for cleanliness.
- Avoid incorrect usage of the journey line, which includes forming maze-like or complicated shapes, adding embellishments, letting it interact improperly with text or secondary elements, or using unsupported colors.

**Color Information:**

- Primary brand colors include DFW Orange, Light Gray, Dark Gray, Blue, Purple, and Yellow. Each color includes multiple system guidelines, including embroidery threads (Madeira), plastics (Pantone Q-codes), paint references (Matthews Paint), and vinyl specifications (Scotchcal).
- Color proportions: Most backgrounds should use white or DFW Light Gray. DFW Orange and Light Gray are the primary colors used for text and highlights. Secondary palette colors should be used sparingly. Tints of gray should be avoided, with recommended increments of 20% for other colors.

**Typography Information:**

- The primary typeface is Neue Haas Unica, selected for its warm and sophisticated look, with weights available in Light, Regular, Medium, and Bold (including italic styles). For accessibility and system compatibility, the Arial typeface is used as a secondary font in digital applications.

**Imagery Photography:**

- The journey line in graphic and photography elements illustrates the brand's core ideas, enhancing visual interest or drawing attention to key information. It must have a clear space for proper use and may weave around photography subjects.

**Spacing Layout:**

- The journey line's minimum clear space is 1.75 times its thickness. Its thickness should align with the channel of the DFW logo and its size must never fall below certain proportions for cohesiveness and legibility.

**Brand Voice:**

- The messaging tone of voice focuses on warmth, connection, optimism, advocacy, and trust. Central messaging themes include discovery, connection, and growth. The brand narrates experiential stories about journeys and future advocacy for travelers.

**Notes:**

- Important trademark notices: DFW, the DFW logo, and other marks are registered trademarks of the Dallas Fort Worth International Airport.
- All fonts and typefaces should ensure system compatibility before installation or purchase. The recommended paneuropean character map for extended linguistic support is included.
