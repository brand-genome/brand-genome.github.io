## Brand Guidelines

### 2020

**Logo Information:**

- Logo usage on social media accounts. Primary logo should be used for corporate accounts, and inverted versions allowed for affiliate accounts to differentiate clearly.
- Logo guidance for signage. Use the primary logo on solid backing plates, and inverted logos for individual letters on buildings. Planning consent may be required for larger signs.

**Color Information:**

- Corporate color palette includes primary colors like Indigo (Pantone: Blue 072 C), Grass Green, Teal, Sky Blue, Orange, Charcoal, and White along with their CMYK, RGB, and HEX codes. Secondary palette includes additional tints.
- Additional color palette reflects Buckinghamshire landscapes with further color options including Pantone codes. Advisable to limit designs to no more than three colors from any palette.
- Color formats like CMYK for print, RGB for digital, and HEX codes commonly used for web design.

**Typography Information:**

- Guide for typography usage: Calibri Bold for Headings/Subheadings, Calibri Regular for body text. Headings should be 4pt larger, Subheadings 2pt larger, and body text minimum of 12pt.
- Accessibility recommendations in typography: Avoid light colored text on light backgrounds or dark text on dark backgrounds.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Imagery guidelines prioritize diversity, showing relatable people, good lighting and composition, and high resolution. Stock images require careful checks for copyright compliance.
- Avoid using clip art, dated stock images, low-resolution images, or stretched/compressed images.
- Consent forms are required for photographs showing individuals, in compliance with GDPR.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Visual chart guidelines like avoiding 3D graphs in corporate documents.

**Brand Voice:**

- Messaging tone for professional branding using consistent templates. Avoid graphical inserts in email signatures, and use formal disclaimers with accessibility in mind.
- Email message for out-of-office notification: Clear indication of unavailability, urgent contact information, and a return date.

**Notes:**

- Stationery: Templates for letters, reports, and PowerPoint presentations enforce brand integrity. Document titles recommended on each page along with page numbers.
- Marketing materials: Clear guidelines for third-party campaigns supported by Buckinghamshire Council and lead campaigns by the Council.
- Signage/vehicle graphics: Approval required from the communications team. Examples provided as concepts.
- Glass manifestation requirements for compliance with Building Regulations, including height levels and contrasting visuals.
