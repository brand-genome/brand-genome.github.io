## Brand Guidelines

### 2014

**Logo Information:**

- Guidelines for logo usage on solid color backgrounds, emphasizing visibility and contrast: The full-color version of the logo should be used whenever possible. When it's not possible to use a full-color version, the logo can be reversed.

**Color Information:**

- Primary graphic identity print colors: Pantone® 132, Pantone® Black 6. Digital equivalents are provided.
- Complementary print colors: Pantone® 400, Pantone® 4535, Pantone® 168; equated to CMYK and RGB values as follows: Pantone® Process Black: CMYK C35 M31 Y32 K100 / RGB R0 G47 B108, Pantone® 132: CMYK C0 M28 Y100 K30 / RGB R255 G255 B255.
- Primary colors for digital media: DarkGoldenRod (#B8860B), Black (#000000). Complementary digital colors are Blonde (#FAF0BE) and White (#FFFFFF) with RGB equivalents provided.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary typography: Gotham; Commonly used weights/styles: Book and Light. Gotham Narrow is recommended for limited space scenarios. Uses include headlines, subheads, body copy, infographics, navigation systems.
- Secondary typography: Celeste; Weights: Regular and Bold. Includes small caps and old-style numerals. Uses include headlines, subheads, body copy, pull quotes.
- Typography settings: Suggested point sizes for different roles such as headlines (50 pt Gotham Light), body copy (14 pt Gotham Book), pull quotes (36 pt Celeste Regular). Examples of type tracking and leading provided.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography style: Inspired by brand character—candid, warm, authentic, and natural. Focus on capturing moments in a non-staged manner, incorporating natural lighting and environment.
- Photography usage notes: Communicate a complete story in one photograph when possible. Type and graphic overlays can be layered into photographs.
- Cropping guidelines: Focus on storytelling via image isolation and purposeful composition. Cropping helps emphasize subjects, adjust focus, scale, or mood.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Grid structure: Design framework for page layouts to ensure consistency while allowing infinite combinations of text and images.

**Brand Voice:**

- Tone of voice: Communicated through name, logo, appearance, and tone. Described with traits like genuine, imaginative, and irreverent.

**Notes:**

- Brand glossary and architecture details: Includes terms like 'Mission', 'Values', 'Promise', 'Touchpoints', and 'Icons'. Notes how these elements contribute to overall brand strategy.
- Contact information: Director of Communications—Bryan J.A. Kelly (443.923.3114, bjakelly@apa1906.net) and Communications Coordinator—Joshua S.D. Harris (410.554.0040, jharris@apa1906.net).
