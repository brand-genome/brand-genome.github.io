## Brand Guidelines

### 2015

**Logo Information:**

- The wordmark includes the traditional shield and the name 'University of Chicago Laboratory Schools'. The wordmark should always be reproduced from the digital art provided. Allow at least 1/4" clear space around the wordmark to preserve its graphic integrity.
- The wordmark can be reproduced in black, white, maroon, or dark gray.
- Guidelines for alignment when paired with address: Align the address with the 'C' in 'Chicago'. The space between the wordmark and the address should be at least equal to the height of the logo.
- File formats for digital wordmark download are available through University Publications.

**Color Information:**

- Primary Palette: Chicago Maroon (Pantone 202 U, CMYK: 0/100/70/50, RGB: 128/0/0, HEX: #8000); Chicago Dark Gray (Pantone Warm Gray 11 U, CMYK: 0/15/30/70, RGB: 118/118/118, HEX: #767676).
- Secondary Palette includes Lab Green (Pantone 576, CMYK: 58/22/91/4, RGB: 122/156/73, HEX: #789d4a), Lab Blue (Pantone 645, CMYK: 50/29/11/0, RGB: 132/161/195, HEX: #7da1c4), Lab Orange (Pantone 143, CMYK: 2/32/92/0, RGB: 247/179/52, HEX: #f1b434), and more.
- Color System aims to balance Chicago’s traditional colors with vibrant additions to reflect the dynamic character of the Lab Schools.

**Typography Information:**

- Recommended typefaces: Adobe Garamond (including Garamond Expert) and Akzidenz Grotesk (Light, Bold, Black). Use Adobe Garamond for text and Akzidenz Grotesk Bold or Black for bold text or headlines.
- For web typography, substitute Times New Roman for Adobe Garamond and Helvetica for Akzidenz Grotesk. Use bold options for headlines online.
- Do not use design software features to create bold weights or small caps unless specified.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography guidelines: Choose simple compositions with a focal point, avoid posed or contrived situations, layered images, montages, or special effects, and seek to reflect diversity.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Typography guidelines specify 1/4" clear space around the wordmark, body text flush-left and ragged-right, no centering or justification.
- Design guidelines emphasize avoiding horizontal or vertical scaling of text and prohibiting drop shadows or drop caps.

**Brand Voice:**

- Tone reflects respect for tradition, knowledge, creativity, diversity, and intellectual inquiry. Avoid inconsistent presentation, and align messaging with values stated in communications.
- Editorial style aligns with Chicago Manual of Style to ensure consistency in written communications.

**Notes:**

- Laboratory Schools identity integrates traditional university values with modern dynamic elements, while maintaining consistency in visual and editorial representation.
