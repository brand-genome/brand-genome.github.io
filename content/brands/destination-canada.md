## Brand Guidelines

### 2019

**Logo Information:**

- The official logo of Destination Canada should always be red, except when appearing against a red or dark background. In such cases, a white reverse logo should be used.
- The logo protection space ensures symmetry. The right margin is narrower to make it easier to center the logo in a page layout. A logo without the leaf is used only for avatar purposes.
- Minimum logo sizes: 10 mm or 28 pixels for optimal readability. Avatar version to be used only as profile pictures on social media. Favicon usage is also noted.

**Color Information:**

- Primary color: Canada Red. Pantone Red 032 C and Red 032 U. CMYK: C:0 M:100 Y:100 K:0; RGB: R:245 G:0 B:20.
- Secondary color palette includes Prairie Yellow, Great Lakes Blue, Maple Red, Aurora Purple, Forest Green, Sunset Pink, Pacific Green, Rocky Mountain Black with corresponding Pantone, CMYK, and RGB codes.

**Typography Information:**

- Title font: Separat Black. Described as offbeat yet approachable. Used in titles and epigraphs.
- Main font: Suisse Int'l. Modern and reader-friendly, available in multiple languages for versatile usage across markets.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Logo protection space ensures symmetry, with a narrower right margin to facilitate centering in layouts.

**Brand Voice:**

- The brand behavior emphasizes six pillars: Spark change, Invite love, Unleash pride, Stay open, Be helpful, Speak from the heart.

**Notes:**

- Additional logo and brand assets are downloadable from brandcanadalibrary.ca/logo. Contact brand-marque@destinationcanada.com for questions.

### 2017

**Logo Information:**

- The marketing logo must follow the clear space/exclusion zone rule: A clear space equal to the height and width of the 'C' in 'Canada' must be maintained. Minimum size: the width should be at least 2 cm (0.79 in).
- The Canada wordmark should be 50% of the width of the marketing logo. For example, if the marketing logo is 4 cm wide, the Canada wordmark will be 2 cm wide.
- Relative scale rule: The Canada wordmark should be the same height as the 'C' in the 'DESTINATION CANADA' corporate logo. Logo placement: Portrait-oriented documents - corporate logo in the upper-left corner, wordmark in the bottom-left corner. Landscape-oriented documents - corporate logo in the bottom-left corner, wordmark in the bottom-right corner.
- Logos can be applied in corporate and marketing communication materials in specific colors: Pantone 1795, CMYK process (C:0 M:100 Y:99 K:4), RGB (R:224 G:30 B:43), and HEX (#E01E2B).
- Examples of logo use in online advertisements: Leaderboard 728x90 px, Wide Skyscraper 160x600 px, Half Page 300x600 px, Medium Rectangle 300x250 px.
- Marketing logo is used at the end of videos as end slates. Custom end slates for YouTube are available for download.
- Corporate logos are used in stationery templates such as letterheads, business cards (bilingual with photos on one side), mailing labels, and No.10 envelopes.

**Color Information:**

- The primary color for the brand is red (Pantone 1795). Other primary colors include black (Pantone Process Black) and white. Grey tones (20K-90K) are used as secondary colors and should use black only without cyan, magenta, or yellow. Cyan is an optional secondary color with values C:70, M:0, Y:20, K:0; RGB (R:22 G:190 B:207).
- Pantone 1795 color codes for logos: Spot color, process color (C:0 M:100 Y:99 K:4), RGB (R:224 G:30 B:43), HEX (#E01E2B).

**Typography Information:**

- Primary typeface: Helvetica Neue (licensed from Linotype). Recommended weights: Helvetica Neue 45 Light, Helvetica Neue 55 Roman, Helvetica Neue 75 Bold.
- Secondary typeface: Arial is used when the primary typeface is unavailable. Weights include Arial Regular, Arial Bold, Arial Italic, and Bold Italic.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography must depict authentic, unstaged, and warm images of real people in natural settings. Images are intended to capture a breadth of relatable experiences.
- Photography style guidelines: Photos should convey first-person perspectives with genuine, candid moments. The use of natural light and minimal staging is emphasized.
- All photos must include credit lines where appropriate. For large applications (over 30 cm/12 in), use 7 pt Helvetica Neue Light. For smaller applications (under 30 cm/12 in), use 5 pt Helvetica Neue Light.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Clear space/exclusion zone requires maintaining an area equal to the height and width of the 'C' in 'Canada' around the logo.
- Portrait-oriented layouts: Corporate logo in the upper-left corner, wordmark in the bottom-left corner. Landscape-oriented layouts: Corporate logo in the bottom-left corner, wordmark in the bottom-right corner.

**Brand Voice:**

- The tone is casual, friendly, and genuine, focusing on emotion and storytelling. Writing should evoke sensory experiences and inspire connections.
- Writing style checklist: Use conversational tones, identify unique aspects of Canada, keep it simple with shorter sentences, and use active verbs and colorful nouns.
- Tips for writing for travelers: Emphasize personal and authentic experiences, create emotional resonance, and offer clear connections to Canadian destinations.

- A Canada map is available for download in editable formats, suitable for print and digital applications.
