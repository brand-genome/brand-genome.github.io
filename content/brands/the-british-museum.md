## Brand Guidelines

### 2012

**Logo Information:**

- The British Museum logo is a key element of the brand design. It can be used in sizes measured to three columns of the grid on various formats.
- The logo should not be flipped, colorized, retouched, or distorted in any way. It is important to preserve its integrity.
- The nine-column grid system dictates that the logo must align with specific grid measurements for consistency.
- Logos can be used vertically, still conforming to grid alignment rules.

**Color Information:**

- The guidelines specify certain color palettes for use in promotional material. For instance, marketing materials use dark or black backgrounds for special exhibitions, while free exhibitions use pale backgrounds to emphasize object colors.
- The text on marketing materials is designed to use two prominent colors: black and white. Designers have some flexibility in complementing with another accent tone.

**Typography Information:**

- The British Museum employs Baskerville as the official serif font, reflecting its historical roots, and Akzidenz Grotesk is the sans-serif typeface.
- Font hierarchies on posters include Akzidenz Grotesk Pro Medium for primary emphasis, with varying sizes applied to headers and body text.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Images play a pivotal role in clarity and consistency. Designers should avoid retouching or overlaying text on imagery.
- Selected visuals must focus on narrative object highlights, ensuring respect for cultural objects.

**Spacing Layout:**

- The visual structure is integral; design centers around a nine-column grid to ensure consistency.
- All designed material requires proper spacing that aligns the logo, grids, and typography.

**Notes:**

- Design guidelines document authored in March 2012 includes templates, system-wide certifications, and addressing third-party dissemination.
