## Brand Guidelines

### 2016

**Logo Information:**

- The document specifies that the logo must always have sufficient spacing around it, equivalent to the letter height 'i' in the logo itself, to maintain clarity and avoid visual clutter.
- Minimum size is outlined for both digital and print: vertical version at 30 pixels wide and horizontal version at 85 pixels wide for digital applications; 10 mm and 30 mm respectively for print.
- Instructions on acceptable color variations of the logo for different applications, including full color, white, black, and reversed versions.
- Strict prohibition of logo distortion, re-coloring, or misguided placement. Encourages adhering to provided templates for consistency.

**Color Information:**

- Primary color palette specified as Blue (PMS 7461C/3005U, HEX #007FC2, RGB 0,127,194), and Gray (PMS 431C, HEX #71787D, RGB 113,120,125). Print materials should use CMYK or Pantone, while digital media should use HEX or RGB.
- Guidance provided on the use of colors for digital screens versus printed materials; RGB/HEX for digital, and CMYK for physical prints.

**Typography Information:**

- Arial font is recommended for most applications, specifying 7pt size (minimum) and font usage roles for different content elements depending on the medium.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography should focus on showcasing UN volunteers in action, with authentic, non-posed images demonstrating pride, effort, and engagement. Photos should include the UNV or partner agency logos when applicable.
- Ethical guidelines for photography include gaining consent before capturing or publishing images, avoiding revelation of sensitive identities (e.g., individuals with specific illnesses), and maintaining the dignity of photographed individuals.

**Spacing Layout:**

- The layout should ensure sufficient space around all visual elements. Headers and spacing elements are carefully specified.

**Brand Voice:**

- The tone of communication emphasized confidence, clarity, and pride in the mission and contributions of UN Volunteers. Messaging should feature concise facts paired with emotional appeal through testimonials and compelling stories.
- Core communications should inspire pride in UN Volunteers' achievements and provide clear, facts-based arguments to build trust and partnerships. Emphasize human-centered stories with a balance of emotional and factual elements.

**Notes:**

- The document mentions a dedicated online resource at www.unv.org/branding/tools for downloading brand assets, tools, and guidelines in line with the core brand strategy.
