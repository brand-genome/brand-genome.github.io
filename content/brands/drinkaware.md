## Brand Guidelines

### 2013

**Logo Information:**

- Different variations of Drinkaware logo include: (1) Alcohol awareness logo used for general health topics, (2) Responsible marketing logo for alcoholic product promotion, (3) Brand logo for referring uniquely to Drinkaware organization.
- Exclusion zone specified: the surrounding zone for the logo should maintain the lowercase letter 'n' height distance from other text, logos, or imagery. No elements should encroach the exclusion zone.
- Logo should not be distorted, tilted, blurred, rotated, or placed on heavily patterned backgrounds. Usage must preserve legibility and integrity.
- Incorrect examples for logo application noted, highlighting distortions, reduced legibility, heavy effects, and improper placements.
- File formats available: EPS, TIFF, JPG, PNG for both print and screen usage. Print format supports CMYK (300 dpi); screen format uses RGB (72 dpi).
- Minimum logo width set at 25mm for print to ensure legibility and proportional representation.
- For broadcast usage: Logo should appear for at least 4 seconds and for at least 10% of the screen time during longer advertisements, ensuring visibility.

**Color Information:**

- Primary palette: Black (Pantone Black): CMYK(0, 0, 0, 100), RGB(0, 0, 0), HEX(#231F20); Red (Pantone 200): CMYK(0, 100, 63, 12), RGB(211, 17, 69), HEX(#D31145); White (Pantone White): CMYK(0, 0, 0, 0), RGB(255, 255, 255), HEX(#FFFFFF).
- Reversed and mono styles include black text on white background, or reversed white text.
- Custom palettes may be used for sponsorship or tagged campaigns, subject to approval.

**Typography Information:**

- Recommended font for labeling or website URL alternatives is Arial, ensuring a consistent typeface.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Spacing in layouts must maintain the exclusion zone (lowercase 'n' height). Placement flexibility includes bottom right corner for best practices.

**Brand Voice:**

- Drinkaware brand voice emphasizes factual, health-oriented messaging about alcohol's effects, focusing on awareness and informed decisions.
- Promotional materials must avoid endorsing, encouraging excessive drinking, drunk imagery, or lobbying to change alcohol policy.

**Notes:**

- Drinkaware offers voluntary schemes for labeling alcoholic drinks, including providing URL-only logos.
- For web integration with the logo, use SEO-friendly text links—'for the facts about alcohol'—and ensure proper code implementation.
- Quick start guide available for logo usage: register trademark, adhere to exclusion zones, ensure proportional sizing, and link logo effectively.
- Contact details for obtaining trademark licenses, requesting downloads, or clarifying guidelines: email at logos@drinkaware.co.uk or call 020 7766 9900.
