## Brand Guidelines

### 2016

**Logo Information:**

- The logo consists of three elements: national reference using French flag colors, a dynamic silhouette symbolizing sports, and extended lettering 'BILLARD' for clarity and distinction.
- Clear safe zone rules: 'Value A is equal to 1/5 of the height or 1/8 of the width.' For example, 50 pixels of margin should be maintained around the logo in all implementations.
- Logo is structurally designed with specific proportions: bounding box with an 8:5 ratio and an angle of 10.3° between the two colors. Example dimensions: 400 pixels wide by 250 pixels tall.
- Two monochrome versions of the logo are available (A: with frame, to be used on rectangular supports; B: without frame, for circular or constrained layouts). Usage depends on background luminosity: on black (40-100% darkness), use white logo; on white (0-40% darkness), use black logo.
- Variations of the logo are accepted for regional use with guidelines already provided but subject to Federation approval. Logos can have square, horizontally extended, or vertically extended shapes (while maintaining the 8:5 or other given ratios), without element distortion.
- Usage guidelines for logo: Ensure no deformation or modifications to structural elements of the logo, colors, or typography. Examples of incorrect usages include modifications to the structure, stretching, changing fonts, or altering specific logo elements such as silhouette or accompanying text.
- Specific acceptable file formats are AI (Adobe Illustrator), PDF, and PNG (at 400px, 800px, and 1200px resolutions). These files are available in both vector and bitmap formats.
- File formats and systems of usage: For digital screens, RGB logos should be used. For printed material, CMYK logos are mandatory. Vector and bitmap logos are available in suitable formats such as .AI, .PDF, and .PNG.

**Color Information:**

- Primary brand colors include RED (#e10a19, RGB: 225, 10, 25, CMYK: 0, 100, 100, 0), BLUE (#1964af, RGB: 25, 100, 175, CMYK: 100, 60, 0, 0), and WHITE (#ffffff, RGB: 255, 255, 255, CMYK: 0, 0, 0, 0).
- Extended color palette includes different shades of black and lighter tints of the main colors: e.g., BLACK (100% BLACK - HEX: #000000, RGB: 0, 0, 0, CMYK: 40, 40, 40, 100), various grays (e.g., 75% Black - HEX: #606060), and lighter tints such as #F7C2B3 with corresponding CYMK and RGB notations.
- Rules for CMYK-based print and RGB screen usage: CMYK should be used for print while RGB/Web values are suited for screen applications. CMYK 'rich black' values may intensify the black color for printing.

**Typography Information:**

- The chosen font is Exo 2.0. It is a modern, geometric sans-serif font family designed to convey both elegance and a futuristic feel. It has 9 weights, each with true italic versions, and is highly adaptable for different use cases. The font is distributed under SIL Open Font License 1.1.
- Three recommended font weights are Extra Light, Regular, and Bold. While other variations are available, those are reserved for specialized cases. Variants such as italics are displayed and encouraged for stylistic use.
- Ligatures are available in the Exo 2.0 font family but should only be used wisely. Particular caution is advised when writing the Federation's identity 'ffbillard,' as certain ligatures could distort the initial visibility of 'f.'
- For basic or administrative content, classical sans-serif fonts such as Arial, Franklin, Gill Sans, Myriad, or Verdana may also be used.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Example applications of branding: During the 2015 French Championship at Evian-les-Bains, the logo was presented along with different font weights and logo variations in promotional materials and displays.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Logo safe zone must be equivalent to 'Value A,' equal to 1/5 of the height or 1/8 of the width. For example, 50-pixel clear space around the logo.
- Maintaining the correct proportions of logos is mandatory. Specific transformations such as extending the logo vertically or horizontally must only occur without any distortion.

**Brand Voice:**

- The voice of the Federation aims to project a unified identity, embodying modernity, clarity, and inclusion while retaining national and sports-specific symbolism.

**Notes:**

- Regional logos have been prepared in alignment with administrative changes in France. Updates include matching logos with Regional Olympic and Sports Committee names and INSEE codes.
- File catalog includes: approved formats and complete archives of logo variants (.AI, .PDF, .PNG) and the full Exo 2.0 font family (OpenType format).
