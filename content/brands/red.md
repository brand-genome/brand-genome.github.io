## Brand Guidelines

### 2019

**Logo Information:**

- The (RED) logo is used as the primary brand mark that communicates the master brand and the organization's efforts to fight AIDS. Specific rules for logo usage include: not changing the font inside the parentheses, avoiding drop shadows, color outlines, and other effects, and maintaining its appearance within approved red or white color knockouts.
- (PRODUCT)RED logo is a specific mark required for use on products, packaging, and partner websites. Special guidelines for this logo include using approved red or white colors on various backgrounds, avoiding changes to fonts or styles, and ensuring the logo is always visually represented.
- Rules prohibit incorrect usage of the logo such as breaking the parentheses, altering typefaces, applying special effects, or failing to use the logo visually for (PRODUCT)RED.

**Color Information:**

- Brand colors include the following: Red (PMS 187C, CMYK C23 M100 Y87 K15, RGB R171 G26 B45, HEX #AF1E2D), White, and Black. Concerns about tonal similarities with red—such as pinks or oranges—should be avoided to maintain visual consistency.
- Other specified colors include Medium Gray (10% Black) and Light Gray (5% Black), with recommendations for delicate use.

**Typography Information:**

- The primary typeface is Trade Gothic Bold Condensed No. 20 (ALL CAPITALS). The secondary typeface is Gordita, used in various weights and applications.
- Partners are responsible for obtaining typefaces independently.

**Imagery Photography:**

- The (RED) brand employs parentheses as a powerful graphic element in marketing, used around images or text to elevate messaging. Imagery includes positive visuals of people, partner items, and optimistic imagery of (RED)'s work. Negative or victimizing imagery and profane language are prohibited.
- Partner logos on products, packaging, and additional marketing (e.g., social imagery) are encouraged to include visual representations using approved logos.

**Brand Voice:**

- (RED) maintains a positive, hopeful tone that inspires action rather than guilt and avoids patronization or victimization. Approved language includes phrases such as 'People living with HIV,' and 'Contributions support (RED)’s fight,' while unapproved terms include 'AIDS patients' or 'The AIDS epidemic.'

**Notes:**

- Partners are strongly encouraged to adhere to brand guidelines strictly, embedding logos visually rather than substituting written forms within marketing copy to ensure compliance.
- Packaging guidelines specify the required inclusion of (RED) marks, contribution lines, and copyright symbols while allowing optional elements like additional impact language.
