## Brand Guidelines

### 2013

**Logo Information:**

- The CTA logo color scheme is inspired by California's natural landscapes. The preferred logo is in full color, but alternatives such as black and white versions (standard and reversed) and 1-color variants are provided for specific applications.
- Clear space around the logo must be equal to the height of 'X,' defined as the distance from the top of the type to the blue curve in the logo. Minimum size is 3/8 inch for the vertical height. Illustrations explain measuring 'X' and space preservation.

**Color Information:**

- The color palette connects the logo with branding and draws inspiration from California landscapes, maintaining professional aesthetics suitable for a trade association. Primary colors include Pantone 2925 (HEX #0096DA, RGB: 00/150/218, CMYK: 80/27/0/0), Pantone 292 (HEX #63AFE1, RGB: 97/175/225, CMYK: 58/17/0/0), Pantone 124 (HEX #EDAB00, RGB: 237/171/0, CMYK: 7/34/100/0), and Cool Gray 10 (HEX #626265, RGB: 98/98/101, CMYK: 61/53/50/22). Secondary palettes include additional blue shades and neutral gray tones.

**Typography Information:**

- Preferred typefaces for print communications include Gotham (primary, suitable for display type like headlines and subheads) and Univers (secondary, for body text due to its readability at smaller sizes).
- Web typography preferences are Proxima Nova for display text and Arial for body copy, to be used as substitutes when Gotham and Univers are unavailable.
- Icon sizes are standardized to 37px, used primarily within flat button containers in any brand color unless specified otherwise. Iconography complements the web design.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Imagery should authentically represent California transit modes and systems. Stock imagery may be used cautiously, avoiding photos that directly feature systems outside California. Style emphasizes simplicity, authenticity, and unique perspectives.
- Transit images should convey motion with balanced motion blur, avoiding distortion, while optionally identifying transit systems depicted.
- Photos of people should capture candid moments prioritizing realism and vitality, with attention to detail and depth of field. Images of people in motion support the dynamic brand narrative.
- Environmental photography focuses on relevant locales or transit facilities, keeping shots uncluttered while optionally including transit riders for human connection.
- Images highlighting the less visible aspects of public transit, such as planning, regulation, and maintenance, are important to reinforce the significance of providing essential services.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Clear space and minimum size guidelines ensure ample visibility and legibility for the CTA logo, maintaining design integrity across various applications.
- Business collateral designs use the logo and geometric patterns derived from it. Layouts adhere to clean, calm aesthetic principles that preserve space for content while enhancing brand visibility.

**Brand Voice:**

- The brand emphasizes California Transit Association’s role as a champion for public transit while reiterating transit's essential role in improving quality of life. Taglines and positioning align with advocacy narratives.
- Messaging strongly supports claims about the necessity and benefits of transit in enhancing communities, stimulating economies, and protecting the environment.

**Notes:**

- CTA’s Brand Standards Guide provides details on visual and verbal branding elements, sample applications, and communication techniques tailored to advancing public transit advocacy in California.
