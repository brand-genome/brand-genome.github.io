## Brand Guidelines

### 2012

**Logo Information:**

- Logo clear space rules are defined to ensure the logo stands out. Clear space must be maintained around the logo to maximize legibility and brand impact. Examples of improper uses include cropping the logo, squashing it horizontally or vertically, using the positive version on dark backgrounds, or placing the logo on images.
- Logo variations include Standard Positive, Standard Negative, Mono Positive, and Mono Negative. These are used in different contexts like heritage representation and other default uses for consistency.

**Color Information:**

- Primary brand colors include PMS286 (C:100 M:66 Y:0 K:2, RGB: 0,93,170, HEX: #005daa), PMS423 (C:0 M:0 Y:0 K:44, RGB: 159,161,164, HEX: #9fa1a4), PMS124 (C:0 M:28 Y:100 K:6, RGB: 238,177,17, HEX: #eeb111). White (C:0 M:0 Y:0 K:0, RGB: 255,255,255, HEX: #ffffff) is key for text on blue backgrounds. Secondary colors are used for corporate communications.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary typefaces are Franklin Gothic Condensed for corporate headlines and Futura Bold for body copy. Franklin Gothic Condensed allows for tight tracking (~100%) and versatile text density. Italic and bold variants are available. Typeface hierarchy includes specific fonts for corporate headlines, events, and body copy.
- Typeface texture is used for dramatic effect. Fonts are degraded with texture overlays. The method involves converting text to curves, importing textures, and customizing the overlay.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Imagery should represent the club's action, dynamism, and crowd engagement to align with the Leinster Rugby narrative.
- To create Leinster's mono images, use image editing techniques like dodging and burning to enhance atmosphere and character.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Spacing and layout rules include defining clear boundaries and margins for logo placement, text alignment, and motif integration to maintain brand consistency.
- Hero area consists of sections for message text, a subheading, call to action or contact details, and the motif area. Text is placed prominently with attention-grabbing sub-elements.

**Brand Voice:**

- The tagline emphasizes matchday excitement, continuing the 'Believe in Magic' theme. It positions Leinster matchdays as events that must be experienced live.

**Notes:**

- The motif design includes four harp strings representing Leinster and 12 components symbolizing counties of the province, ensuring a deep connection to heritage.
- All elements like logos, text, and motifs must adhere to safe zone and clear space guidelines. These instructions aim for consistent application.
