## Brand Guidelines

### 2015

**Logo Information:**

- The Green Party logo should be placed prominently for immediate identification, including versions for local and national use.
- Different straplines can be used in Green tags for campaigns. Tags must not obstruct faces, with consistent design across materials.
- Vertical logo versions may be used within larger graphic designs, such as on digital and social media.
- The tag should not be recreated in different fonts or colors, and it should not obscure faces or important visual elements.

**Color Information:**

- Green Party Green color is prominently used along with a defined palette. Ensure color consistency across all materials.
- Dark green or Green Party Green should be used for text in taglines and graphic elements, with all colors limited to the official palette.

**Typography Information:**

- The primary font used for campaigns and materials is Bebas Neue in large, minimal styles for clarity and branding recognition.
- Avoid replacing fonts in the Green tags; use the designated typography for brand consistency.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography should center subjects with additional space for content. Minimum image dimensions: 1024x768.
- Web banners and campaign materials should feature clear, engaging photography with ample spacing for text overlays.
- Candidate photos should show relevant local landmarks, and Green tags should not cover any part of the subject.

**Spacing Layout:**

- For social media graphics, maintain a 1:7 vertical ratio for square formats. For Twitter/Facebook links, use 1:6.
- Encouraged to use extensive white space in materials to enhance readability and design ethos.

**Brand Voice:**

- The brand tone is consistent, promoting eco-friendly initiatives, fairness, and vibrant economy-focused messaging.
- Messaging should focus on standing up for real change and connecting emotionally with readers.

**Notes:**

- Do not use unauthorized or editorial-use-only images for public campaign purposes.
