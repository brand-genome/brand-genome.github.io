## Brand Guidelines

### 2019

**Logo Information:**

- The logo is composed of continuous sound waves in the form of arrows surrounding the European Union flag, representing dialogue and communication.
- Logo composition rules and elements explained, including proportions and typography integration.
- Language versions of the logo provided for multilingual purposes.
- Clear space area rules established around the logo for optimal visibility.
- Negative and reversed color versions are defined.
- Guidelines for logo display on various backgrounds (light and dark).
- Minimum size for logo usage specified.
- Mute logo version and applications are described.
- Common misuses of the logo are presented, with visual examples.
- Detailed co-branding rules with European institutions and CoR political groups.
- Multilingual logo applications are emphasized.

**Color Information:**

- The official color palette is defined for the visual identity, including primary and secondary colors.
- CMYK, RGB, and HEX codes are specified for accurate reproduction.
- Rules for logo applications on different background colors (light, dark, and multicolored backgrounds).

**Typography Information:**

- Main and secondary typography are outlined, detailing font styles and weights.
- Alternative typography for office documents specified.
- Typography usage for titles, body text, and other applications established.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Style for photography emphasizes mood, composition, and regional focus.
- Graphic lines, such as white diamond shapes, defined for visual identity.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Grid layouts for stationeries, brochures, websites, and other materials established with precise dimensions.
- Margins and padding are clearly defined for all formats, ranging from print to digital.
- Positioning of logos, titles, and content specified as part of general structure guidelines.

**Brand Voice:**

- The tone emphasizes open communication, dialogue, and inclusivity.
- Dedicated tagline for reinforcing the brand voice: 'Deepening European Cohesion and Communication'.

**Notes:**

- The document is an official guideline for European Committee of the Regions, last updated in June 2019.
- Extensive use of consistent visual identities, including multilingual applications, co-branding, and regional adaptations.
