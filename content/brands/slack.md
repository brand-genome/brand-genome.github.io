## Brand Guidelines

### 2020

**Logo Information:**

- Logo use guidelines
Ensure that there is adequate space between the logo and surrounding elements. For the horizontal logo, the clear space around it should always be greater than or equal to the size of the octothorpe. For stacked logos, the clear space should equal the length of one lozenge in the octothorpe.

**Color Information:**

- Core colors and secondary colors with codes:
Examples:
RGB: 222-137-105, HEX: DE8969, PMS: 2225 C, CMYK: 48-0-16-0
Secondary colors available with usage limitations outlined:
Use only black or white text as specified for accessibility and do not use secondary colors for text unless paired with core colors.
- Accessible color combinations described:
All examples shown pass contrast standards except for small blue text on aubergine background. Reserve blue text on aubergine backgrounds only for larger headlines.

**Typography Information:**

- Typography: Larsseit
Larsseit is used for headlines. Always typeset with optical kerning, tracking set to 0, and sentence case. Letter spacing: -1px for smaller headlines, -2px for larger ones.
- Typography: Slack Circular
Slack Circular is used for text paragraphs. Always typeset with optical kerning, tracking set to -10, and sentence or title case. Letter spacing: 0px for regular paragraphs, -0.2px for hero paragraphs.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Illustrations: Bold, elevated, dimensional, defined by negative space and perspective. Created to distinctly capture Slack brand identity.
- Icons: Used across different brand touchpoints such as marketing, products, events. They offer symbolic simplicity and conceptual clarity.
- Photography styles:
Avatars: Relatable, human, quirky, warm but not silly.
Still Life: Attainable, clear, concise, demonstrates Slack’s personality.
Lifestyle: Aspirational, collaborative, confident, environmentally inclusive.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Typography spacing rules:
Larsseit: 0 tracking for print collateral, -1px letter spacing for smaller headlines and -2px for larger headlines. Optical kerning is mandatory.
- Slack Circular tracking and spacing rules:
Title or sentence case use. Set tracking to -10 for embedded collateral. Letter spacing: regular paragraphs: 0px, hero paragraphs: -0.2px.
- Clear space rules in Partnership Lockups:
Clear space equals the width of one octothorpe (octothorpe size divided equally by vertical line between partner and Slack logos). Alignment rules provided such as baseline alignment.

**Brand Voice:**

- Guidelines for Slack Voice:
Speak directly to the user conversationally but professionally. Maintain relatable yet intelligent tone depending on context/audience.
- Tone attributes listed:
Confident yet respectful, friendly without ingratiation, conversational & witty but not silly; avoid jargon, clichés, offensive language or exclusionary terms. Eloquence is emphasized while ensuring substance is clear.
- Voice adherence rules:
Avoid words like 'slacker/slacking/slackee', e.g., no phrases such as 'Happy Slacking!'. External representation remains authentic and considerate.

**Notes:**

- Slack trademarks and terms include:
Slack name, logo, phrases such as 'Where Work Happens', 'Slackbot', and 'Several People Are Typing'. Usage requires adherence to specified guidelines for marketing materials.
- Trademark guidelines for marketing materials:
Use registered mark ® symbol on the most prominent use of Slack trademarks for U.S. usage. All marketing materials must include copyright notice.

### 2019

**Logo Information:**

- The Slack logo consists of an octothorpe and a logotype set in Hellix Bold. The horizontal logo is the primary version, while the stacked logo is for large-scale applications but not intended for small sizes. A small-use logo exists for dimensions as small as 15px to 90px wide.
- Black, white, and aubergine (Slack's brand colors) background options for logos. Avoid using full-color logos on photographs unless placed on black or white areas.
- One-color logos are to be used on photographs and backgrounds in Slack's color palette. Only black and white versions are valid one-color options.
- List of incorrect logo usage: do not add shadows, change transparency, re-create using different fonts, shuffle colors in the octothorpe, outline logotype, apply rotation, crop, distort, or use any effects.
- Clear space requirement for Slack logo with partner logos is equal to the width of one octothorpe. Logos should be aligned optically to the baseline of Slack's logotype.
- Department, adjacent brands, and internal Slack programs have distinct branding guidelines using a mix of Slack's existing logo styles and customizations for different contexts.

**Color Information:**

- Core brand colors include black (HEX: #000000), white (HEX: #FFFFFF), aubergine (HEX: #4A154B), green (HEX: #2EB67D), red (HEX: #E01E5A), yellow (HEX: #ECB22E), and blue (HEX: #36C5F0). Accessible alternatives are also specified for blue (#1264A3) and green (#007A5A) for text and web states.
- Secondary brand colors span various tonalities with distinct HEX codes, like #7C2852, #185F34, #C05B8C, and more. Secondary colors are not intended for text and should only be used in limited combinations with core colors.
- Accessibility rules: Text on colored backgrounds should pass contrast standards. Do not use small blue text on aubergine backgrounds unless used as headlines.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary headline typeface is Larsseit for embedded and printed collateral. Headlines are to be typeset in Optical kerning with specific letter spacing adjustments based on size.
- Slack Circular is the primary typeface for body text and paragraphs. Kerning and tracking adjustments vary depending on content and placement with unique spacing for paragraph vs hero paragraphs.
- When Slack's brand fonts are unavailable, 'Proxima Nova' is the recommended web-safe fallback. Proxima Nova substitutes are used in environments like Google Slides and Docs.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography approach is segmentally based on three styles: Avatars (relatable, warm, human); Still Life (attainable, focused, simple); and Lifestyle (aspirational, indicating collaboration).

**Spacing Layout:**

- Master brand offerings align the Slack logo with text using optical kerning and tracking rules. Defined spaces such as the distance equal to one octothorpe are commonly used in layouts across different use cases.
- Guidelines for aligning and separating adjacent brand logos or departmental sub-brands utilize spacing proportionate to the Slack octothorpe size.

**Brand Voice:**

- Slack's brand voice focuses on a spectrum from relatable to aspirational. For instance, relatable content (human, humorous, clear communication) is balanced with heroic descriptors.

**Notes:**

- Localized language-specific considerations include font size reductions for Japanese (15%) and Western languages (10%) due to structural differences in alphabets and character density.
- Slack utilizes its octothorpe as a design element to foster identity continuity between master brands, partnerships, and departmental uses. Typography choices like Hellix Bold and strict tracking adjustments ensure consistent branding.

### 2019

**Logo Information:**

- Octothorpe geometry uses a 19×19 grid creating consistent shapes, sizes, and spacing.
- The Slack logo is composed of an octothorpe (#) and logotype set in Hellix Bold. Horizontal logos are primary and preferred.
- Stacked logo intended for large-scale use; avoided for small sizes due to legibility concerns.
- Small logos are recommended for sizes between 50px–90px width, and small icon versions for sizes between 15px–20px height.
- Clear space rules: Horizontal logo space must equal the size of the octothorpe; stacked logo space must equal one lozenge length.
- Incorrect logo usage: Don’t crop, outline, rotate, distort, or modify transparency or colors of the logo.
- Usage guidelines emphasize exclusive and unaltered Slack assets, discouraging old or recreated versions.

**Color Information:**

- Official Slack palette includes 'Aubergine' (primary), 'Blue', 'Green', 'Red', 'Yellow', with HEX and Pantone codes provided.
- Accessible alternatives: Blue (#1264A3), Green (#007A5A), and variations of Aubergine for accessibility and design states.

**Typography Information:**

- Headlines set in Larsseit Bold with tracking adjustments (-1px or -2px), kerning rules, and optically aligned sizes.
- Body text set in Circular Pro, tracking (-10 for embedded collateral, web hero paragraphs -0.2px). Avoid all-caps formatting.
- Web-safe substitute font: Proxima Nova used when Slack brand fonts are unavailable.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Full-color Slack logos should only appear on white, black, or aubergine backgrounds. Avoid photographs unless logos are placed on black/white areas.
- One-color logo versions used for limited color projects, photography, or Slack palette colors with priority toward full-color representation.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Proper spacial alignment between octothorpe and logotype critical for consistency and design cohesion.
- Clear space rules when integrating Slack logos with affiliated brands; maintain space greater or equal to octothorpe size.

**Brand Voice:**

- Do not mirror or imitate Slack's product design; avoid creating user confusion regarding endorsements or integrations.
- Prohibited practices: Using Slack's name or logos as part of third-party brand names, trademarks, or merchandise production.

**Notes:**

- Slack assets including logos, marks, and word designations must not imply sponsorship or endorsement.
- PDF provides detailed typography style stacks matching Larsseit and Circular fonts, allowing design adaptation with point specifics.
