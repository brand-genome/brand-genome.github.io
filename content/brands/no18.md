## Brand Guidelines

### 2019

**Logo Information:**

- Partner logos should have equal weight and space as the No18 logo. Logos must maintain a clear space equal to the height of the 'N' from the No18 logo, with a 100% black dividing line positioned according to the '8' in dual branding lockups.
- Dual branding examples involve placing No18 logos alongside partner logos, respecting local language rules when required. Uses fonts like Noto for legal language versions alongside No18 branding.

**Color Information:**

- Primary brand colors include: Black (C:0 M:0 Y:0 K:100, R:0 G:0 B:0, #000000), White (C:0 M:0 Y:0 K:0, R:255 G:255 B:255, #FFFFFF), Pantone Process Black, and Gold (C:44 M:45 Y:75 K:17, R:132 G:117 B:77, #84754d, Pantone 871 C). These must always be used at 100% tint value.
- Accent colors are used only for highlights and motifs (e.g., graphs, charts). Colors: Aqua (C:37 M:0 Y:21 K:0, R:150 G:229 B:216, #96e5d8), Blue (C:47 M:24 Y:0 K:0, R:133 G:171 B:223, #85abdf), Yellow (C:2 M:38 Y:100 K:0, R:246 G:168 B:3, #f6a803), Beige (C:16 M:18 Y:42 K:0, R:215 G:198 B:157, #d7c69d). Accent colors should not be used as backgrounds.
- Usage percentage for branding: Black as the base color (80%), White (15%), Gold (4%), and Accent colors (1%). Black must dominate designs unless reversed due to printing issues.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary typefaces: Gill Sans Std (Light and Regular) and Adobe Garamond Pro (Regular, Italic, and Semibold Italic). Both are used across printed communications and online.
- Supporting typefaces: Noto is used only when Gill Sans Std or Adobe Garamond Pro do not support the required language script. Alternative choices for device-reliant fonts are Arial (replacing Gill Sans Std) and Georgia (replacing Adobe Garamond Pro).
- Headlines formatted using Gill Sans Std Regular with tracking set to 200. For web, CSS 'letter-spacing: .2em' must be applied.
- Casing rules: Text is written in uppercase and sentence case. Title case is limited to names only.
- Example usage: Headlines use tracking and punctuation (e.g., apostrophes, question marks, no full stops). Subheaders and body copy include full punctuation while bullet points exclude full stops.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography style focuses on storytelling with shallow depth of field to highlight key elements of office, meeting room, and open space shots.
- Capturing portraits and wider views of people working or enjoying No18 venues. Emphasis on moments that contribute to a larger narrative.
- Photographs of No18 products and spaces should present them at their best, focusing on aesthetics and function.
- General dos and don’ts for venue photography: Use clean compositions with minimal clutter, good weather, and portrait lenses. Avoid wide-angle distortion or shooting on overcast days.
- Photography involves capturing authentic, everyday people utilizing No18's amenities like meeting spaces and offices.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Buttons for digital collateral are square designs with white text on a gold background (or black/white text if required). Ensure sufficient padding around text.
- Icons are designed with consistent line width, single color, and minimal usage (usually gold on black background). Alternate color use permitted to ensure visibility.
- Examples show layouts for brochures, posters, stationery, meal cards, digital banners, merchandise, and signage. Common design elements prioritize clarity, resonance, and hierarchy.

**Brand Voice:**

- Tone emphasizes an aspirational, enriching, and cosmopolitan identity with attention to customer experience and modern business lifestyle. Incorporates wellness and quality of life.
- Messaging focuses on members’ pride in their workspace and an inviting, stimulating environment promoting positive experiences and outcomes.

**Notes:**

- Document outlines merchandise examples such as branded stationery and meal cards.
- Photography must showcase No18 spaces, emphasizing functionality, aesthetic appeal, user experiences, and high-quality visuals.
- Dual branding alignment guidelines specified for collaborations.
