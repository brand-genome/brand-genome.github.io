## Brand Guidelines

### 2020

**Logo Information:**

- Clear space for logo specified as 1x height, padding rules for digital logos at least 3x around logo.
- Minimum sizing requirement of logo: examples for embroidery, promotional items (1.5 inches).
- Digital logo formats supported include; mention of PNG and SVG.
- Improper uses of the logo such as tilting, adding shadows, adding gradients, rearranging symbols, or applying busy photo backgrounds.

**Color Information:**

- Primary colors listed: New Growth Green (HEX: #B1BB00, PMS 390 C, CMYK: 20 0 100 8, RGB: 177 187 0). Black (HEX: #231F20, CMYK: 0 0 0 98, RGB: 35 31 32).
- Accent colors listed: Gale Force (HEX: #3B4559), Chambray (HEX: #43609F), Robin's Egg (HEX: #6FD3E0). Tertiary colors include Enliven and Orchid.

**Typography Information:**

- Font family used: Segoe UI. Weight and purposes indicated: Segoe UI Regular 14pt and Semibold 55pt for web styles; Segoe UI Bold for office text.
- Typography in video and digital forms specifies brand fonts, weights, and use-case setup.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Logos in photography: Use the full-color logo on light backgrounds; use white/reversed (KO version) on darker images.
- Social media photo guidance: Focus on the vehicle being the hero; avoid cluttered environments or areas showing proprietary tools.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Margins required at 0.5”. Use of 12-column layout grid and spacing consistency with legal watermarks for letterhead designs.
- Stationery (envelopes) grid and layout structure for corporate consistency: align symbol proportions accurately.

**Brand Voice:**

- Adherence to Associated Press stylebook recommended; uniform tone emphasizes professional but clear writing.
- Brand post formats for social media emphasize simple, clear call-to-action style wording. Avoid emojis, exclamation marks.

**Notes:**

- Thread colors for embroidery listed: Green (Madera 1169 Classic), Black (Madera 1199), and White (Madera 1286). Confirm alternatives via Marketing.
- Color compliance strongly advised using Pantone reference chips to ensure consistency on printed outputs.
