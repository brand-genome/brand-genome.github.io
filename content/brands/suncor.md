## Brand Guidelines

### 2021

**Logo Information:**

- Usage of Suncor, Petro-Canada, and Sunoco logos, word marks, and design marks is restricted to approved suppliers and third-party licensees. Proper trademark notices must be used for third-party versions of the trademarks.
- Guidelines state: For single-page documents, the logo with the trademark symbol and notice should appear once, typically at the bottom of the page or in the footer, away from the main logo. It should match the document font.
- Four-color logo versions preferred: Four-color reverse and single-reverse. Single-color positive logo usage also allowed, provided that it is placed on an appropriate background.
- Avoid placing logo on a background with insufficient contrast, such as other logo colors or colors that make the logo illegible. Clear examples are provided for 'Best use' practices: Midnight and Dusty Blue.

**Color Information:**

- Logo-specific colors: Suncor Blue (CMYK 100/58/0/21, RGB 0/85/150, PMS 294, HEX #005596), Gradient Yellow (CMYK 0/24/94/0, RGB 255/196/41, PMS 123, HEX #FFC429), Gradient Orange (CMYK 0/60/100/0, RGB 245/130/32, PMS 158, HEX #F58220), Gradient Red (CMYK 0/90/86/0, RGB 239/65/53, PMS 032, HEX #EF4135). These colors are used only in the Suncor logo.
- Brand categories: Neutrals, Accents, Greys. Each category has standard colors and can be tinted at 70% or 40% opacity for subtle effects. 
- Neutrals: Midnight (CMYK 86/69/50/45, RGB 37/55/70, PMS 7546, HEX #253746). Greys: Dark Grey (CMYK 15/0/0/88, RGB 56/65/71, PMS Cool Grey 11, HEX #384147), Light Grey (CMYK 0/0/0/20, RGB 209/211/212, HEX #D1D3D4).
- Accents: Dusty Blue (CMYK 66/40/28/11, RGB 91/124/145, PMS 5415c, HEX #5B7C91), Moss (CMYK 65/31/63/0, RGB 106/147/119, HEX #6A9377), Gold (CMYK 0/30/90/0, RGB 253/185/51, PMS 130, HEX #FDB933), Orange (CMYK 0/61/99/10, RGB 222/117/30, PMS 715).
- Accent Gray 'Dusk' (CMYK 70/60/14/0, RGB 98/109/161, HEX #626DA1), Sky (CMYK 54/0/10/0, RGB 102/203/225, PMS 305, HEX #66CBE1), Sand, etc.
- Usage rules for color: Accents best used for headlines, charts, graphs, icons, buttons, and callouts. Avoid use for overlays and body copy. Neutrals are not appropriate for text overlays or headlines.
- Accessibility guidance: Use contrasting colors to ensure legibility for text, buttons, and icons. Certain combinations are AA or AAA compliant, validated for accessibility for people with disabilities.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary typeface: Noto Sans. Four weights are used: Light, Regular, Bold, and Black (including italics). Ideal for print and digital media, it supports 800 languages and is chosen for accessibility and inclusion reasons.
- Typography rules for Noto Sans: Light for headlines, sub-headlines, or small sizes (e.g., labels, charts). Bold for headlines, subheadings, and callouts. Regular is versatile for headlines, body text, labels, and captions. Black is reserved for large headlines only in color.
- Guidance for typography layouts: Combine type weights and sizes to create contrast and visual hierarchy. Avoid mixing too many weights; the pairing should have at least two levels of separation (e.g., Light with Bold).
- Secondary typeface: Arial. For use in environments where Noto Sans is unavailable (e.g., PowerPoint, word processors). Supports Regular, Italic, and Bold styles only.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography must feature people for emotional connection. Emphasize images that are candid, diverse, and authentic. Employee photos should follow safety standards, with individuals in uniform when required (internal photographs follow EHS guidelines).
- Preferred subject matter categories: In action (employees engaged in activity, candid shots); Portrait (formal employee portraits, not for stock images); and Asset (machines, landscapes, or production plants, sparingly used).
- Photography style preferences: Look for dynamic composition with natural lighting and clear space for copy. Avoid gradient or solid color overlays, unnatural lighting, and black-and-white photography.

**Spacing Layout:**

- The 'Energy Path,' a subtle yet key design element, is used sparingly once per layout. Gradients go from left to right (Yellow to Red). Can be printed in varied stroke widths based on asset type: e.g., 4-point or 20' wide.
- Transparent overlays add text legibility over images for better layout. Ideal colors include Midnight, Dark Grey, and Dusty Blue with 80–90% opacity. Avoid using gradients, fades, or overlays covering faces entirely.
- Grids are important for organizing information. Use a rule of thirds for visual variety, align text to the left, and ensure white space for clarity and legibility. Recommended grid structures include a 12-column layout with gutters.

**Brand Voice:**

- The brand voice and visuals are professional yet dynamic, promoting inclusion, diversity, and authenticity across communication platforms.
