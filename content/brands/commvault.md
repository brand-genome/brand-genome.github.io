## Brand Guidelines

### 2015

**Logo Information:**

- Primary logo and one-color logo are provided alongside a reversed version. Logos are shown in proper color variants, including regular, reversed, and one-color reversed formats.
- Logo clear space/safe zone is defined as the height of the 'V' in the logo, and must be maintained at all times to maximize visual presence.
- Logo misuse examples and rules include avoiding placement on busy or dark images, avoiding usage within a white box overprinted on busy images, not applying drop shadows, avoiding scaling below 0.75 inches in print, and avoiding distortion or use of unauthorized typeface.
- Legacy logo designs and elements are prohibited from use with the new brand.

**Color Information:**

- Primary colors and their codes:
Vector Red / Pantone 185 C / CMYK: C0 M95 Y90 K0 / RGB: R238 G49 B47 / HEX: #EE312F
Deep Blue / Pantone 2965 C / CMYK: C100 M0 Y0 K100 / RGB: R0 G6 B36 / HEX: #000624
Bright Blue / Pantone 2995 C / CMYK: C75 M5 Y0 K0 / RGB: R0 G180 B235 / HEX: #00B4EB
Text Gray / Pantone 8400 C / CMYK: C0 M0 Y0 K60 / RGB: R124 G129 B132 / HEX: #7C8084
Slight Gray / Pantone 428 C / CMYK: C2 M0 Y0 K10 / RGB: R223 G229 B232 / HEX: #DFE5E8
Pure White / CMYK: C0 M0 Y0 K0 / RGB: R255 G255 B255 / HEX: #FFFFFF.
- Text Gray is solely for body copy, and shades of Deep Blue, Bright Blue, and Text Gray can only be used for accents in tables and charts.
- Color usage rules:
Vector Red is used as a highlight element exclusively in 'vector' contexts.
Deep Blue is used to anchor elements and break large areas of white space.
Bright Blue is used for highlighting call-outs and features.
Slight Gray is used on the pattern and less critical highlights.

**Typography Information:**

- Approved typefaces and fonts:
DIN Next:
- DIN Regular: for most applications.
- DIN Light: for larger display sizes.
- DIN Medium: for points of emphasis.
Tahoma:
- Tahoma Regular: used for presentations and internal communications.
Tahoma is a system font resembling DIN and should be used as the default fallback option.
- Typography usage roles:
- DIN Next is designated as Commvault’s brand typeface, intended for graphic/web design by authorized users.
- DIN Light: Ideal for display headings.
- Tahoma: Recommended for system communications and Microsoft PowerPoint presentations.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Primary photography style features subjects conveying confidence and professionalism. Subjects should be dressed in business attire and images should avoid clichés.
- Support photography should focus on subjects in non-cliché modern working environments that complement the primary visual narrative.
- Photo treatment includes applying gradients (Bright Blue transitioning to transparent) with soft light transparency and reducing layer transparency to 60%.
- Photo placement guidelines:
- Cover pages use larger image proportions.
- Copy-heavy documents use bleed images off the left side or cropped on the right side.

**Spacing Layout:**

- A grid structure of 18 x 18 is mandated for all graphic elements. Layout guidelines discourage clutter and enhance clean placements.
- Anchor bars, when required, equate to 1/9 of a grid module height and are colored Deep Blue.

**Brand Voice:**

- Commvault embraces brand essence elements such as confidence, simplicity, sharper thinking, and value proposition. Messaging centers around innovative, customer-centric solutions that enhance strategic conversations.
- Brand attributes highlight optimism, openness, and dynamism combined with rich, practical customer experience.

**Notes:**

- Legacy design materials, including old logos and color palettes, are strictly prohibited in the new branding.
- For assistance with any brand or graphic elements, contact brand@commvault.com.
