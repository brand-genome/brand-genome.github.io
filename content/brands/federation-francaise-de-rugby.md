## Brand Guidelines

### 2019

**Logo Information:**

- The FFR vertical logo is composed of the coq symbol and the 'FFR' acronym. The coq’s design, typeface, colors (blue and two shades of red), and composition cannot be altered or deformed. The vertical version in color is preferred wherever possible.
- The horizontal logo for FFR is a technical alternative used when the vertical logo is not legible on particular materials. Its use should remain rare.
- The simplified FFR logo is designed for small-scale applications, ensuring brand quality in reproductions of very small sizes. Recommended usage is when the coq height is 12mm or smaller.
- The typographic FFR logo does not include the coq symbol and is identified as a complementary element mainly to sign institutional materials. It is not to be used alone.
- Clear space rules for FFR logos specify dedicated margins where no other graphical elements can intrude. Vertical logo protection zone: 1/6x around the edges. Horizontal logo zone: 1/3x around edges. Typographic version should also follow this spacing logic.
- Incorrect FFR logo usages include modifying proportions, colors, typography, or symbols; animating or reorganizing elements; and adding or removing components.
- The vertical France Rugby logo combines the coq with the 'FRANCE RUGBY' wordmark and shares the same design rules and priorities as the FFR logo. The vertical version in color is preferred.
- The horizontal version of the France Rugby logo positions the coq symbol between the 'FRANCE' and 'RUGBY' wordmarks. This version is a technical alternative for better legibility in specific formats and is recommended for rare use.
- The simplified France Rugby logo is intended for very small-scale reproduction (12mm coq height or less), especially in digital applications.
- Clear space rules for France Rugby logos protect the area around the symbol. Vertical logo: X/6. Horizontal logo: X/2. These margins forbid any encroachment by other graphic elements.
- Incorrect France Rugby logo usages include altering proportions, colors, typographies, or symbol; animating the design; reorganizing or misorienting components; and adding unauthorized elements.

**Color Information:**

- The FFR and France Rugby color palettes include Pantone 281 C (Blue, CMYK: 100, 85, 10, 40; RGB: 0, 42, 97; HEX: #002a61), Pantone 485 C (Light Red, CMYK: 0, 95, 95, 0; RGB: 226, 31, 32; HEX: #e21f20), and Pantone 1805 C (Dark Red, CMYK: 0, 100, 100, 25; RGB: 182, 7, 24; HEX: #b60718).

**Spacing Layout:**

- For both FFR and France Rugby, logos must maintain defined clear space zones. Vertical: Minimum spacing of one-sixth of the logo's dimensions for clear areas. Horizontal: One-third distance from edge to ensure no intrusion.

**Notes:**

- All graphics in the guidelines are exclusively owned by Fédération Française de Rugby, with strict reproduction rights. Only predefined elements provided in the guidelines should be used.
- The brand comprises two identities: the institutional FFR brand and the commercial France Rugby brand, each associated with specific roles and audiences.
