## Brand Guidelines

### 2011

**Logo Information:**

- The Verge logotype is presented in multiple color styles. Examples include Orange, Purple, and Black & White versions.
- Clearance rule: Maintain a minimum space around the logotype equal to the height of the 'V' in the logotype.
- Do's and Don'ts for logotype usage: Do not change the color of the logotype; do not distort the logotype; do not apply effects; do not cut or change proportions of any part of the logotype; and do not use gradient colors on the logotype.

**Color Information:**

- Primary colors include Orange (#FF0000), Black (#363534), White (#FFFFFF). Orange: R:255/G:0/B:0.
- Secondary colors include Purple (#472E97), Light Grey (#F0F0F0), and Dark Grey (#363534).
- Print Color Palette: Orange (Pantone 172, C0/M75/Y90/K0), White (C0/M0/Y0/K0), Black 7 (C0/M0/Y15/K82), Cool Grey 1 (C0/M0/Y0/K6), Pantone 2736 (C0/M2/Y0/K68), and Pantone Black (C60/M40/Y40/K100).
- Logotype on gradients: Multi-color gradient, single-color gradients (Purple, Black, Orange).

**Typography Information:**

- Primary Typography: Helvetica Neue Regular and Bold. Helvetica Neue is to be used for top-level elements.
- Secondary Typography: Georgia Regular and Bold. Georgia is to be used for body text and secondary-level elements on screen.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Logotype on images: Examples include Black & White images with single-color gradient, clean/minimal images, and basic images with multi-color gradient.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Maintain a minimum spacing around the logotype equivalent to the height of the 'V' in the logo.

**Notes:**

- The intent of the document is to define visual identity guidelines for The Verge across multiple mediums, ensuring the brand's unified graphic identity.
- The combination of individual elements, clear and consistent use of logos, bold contrasts in typography and colors, and simplicity with minimal embellishments represent the essence of The Verge's visual identity.
