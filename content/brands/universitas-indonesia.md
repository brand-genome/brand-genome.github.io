## Brand Guidelines

### 2014

**Logo Information:**

- Logo UI is composed of three main components: the symbol (Makara), logotype, and tagline. Two signature types are defined: Complete Set Signature (all components) and Simple Set Signature (symbol and logotype only). The Complete Set Signature is for general purposes and publications, while the Simple Set Signature is used for special purposes such as invitations, diplomas, and official backdrops.
- Symbol UI dates back to 1952 and has undergone changes over time. Official changes include adding a pentagon in 1959 and removing it in 2000 when the university stood as BHMN (State-Owned Legal Entity). Original shapes include symmetrical circular designs created following a detailed grid system.
- The logo's visual standardization is mandated to avoid design distortion caused by past manual reproduction methods. The original symmetric, circular design from 1952 has now been restored based on archival and design studies.

**Color Information:**

- Official colors for the UI logo are defined in multiple formats: CMYK (C=0, M=15, Y=100, K=0), RGB (R=246, G=219, B=0), HEX (#f6DB00), and PMS (Pantone 109 C).

**Notes:**

- A significant focus on preserving the original Makara logo demonstrates the institution's commitment to its historical and symbolic integrity.
- Interpretations by Prof. Srihadi highlight the philosophical meaning of the logo's elements representing knowledge and education.
