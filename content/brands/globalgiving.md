## Brand Guidelines

### 2021

**Logo Information:**

- The GlobalGiving primary logo consists of a logo icon and wordmark. Icon colors represent people and organizations supported. The preferred application is the horizontal version of the logo.
- The stacked secondary logo version is used in vertical applications or confined spaces. It is recommended for situations where the primary logo doesn't fit well.
- Minimum size requirements state the full-color logo must not be reduced to less than 1.2 inches, and the one-color logo must not be reduced below 1.0 inches.
- Safe zone rules require clear space equal to the width of the logo icon around all sides of the logo.
- Logo misuse examples include: Adding shadows or strokes; using outdated versions or incorrect colors; rotating or stretching; altering proportions; changing letter alignment or spacing; turning into an outline.

**Color Information:**

- Primary palette includes Orange (#F08B1D), Blue (#457389), Green (#9FA617), and Red (#97341B). Secondary palette features variations of gray, plum, other greens, yellows, and blues.
- Usage rules: Primary palette should be used for calls-to-action (Orange), hyperlinks (Blue), growth-related content (Green), and disaster content (Red). Secondary colors supplement the primary palette.
- Correct color applications include creating contrast, using transparency on dark colors for overlays, and combining primary palette with secondary palette strategically.
- Incorrect usage prohibits placing logos over non-neutral primary palette colors, using transparent light colors over photos, and neglecting contrast.

**Typography Information:**

- GlobalGiving uses Aleo (serif) and Open Sans (sans-serif) fonts. Aleo is used for headlines, callouts, and quotes, while Open Sans is used for body text.
- Usage rules: Avoid using Aleo in italics or in all caps. Pair Aleo headlines with Open Sans body copy for aesthetic appeal.

**Imagery Photography:**

- GlobalGiving imagery emphasizes hopeful faces, diversity, and high-quality photographs that represent project themes, ensuring all photos maintain dignity and avoid perpetuating stereotypes.
- Incorrect photo usage includes low-quality images, stock photography, and images portraying individuals as helpless victims or in discomfort.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Combining layout elements includes jumbo quotes, diagonal patterns, and diamond world map graphics to create clean and versatile compositions.

**Brand Voice:**

- GlobalGiving employs People First language, avoiding labels and promoting dignity. Storytelling principles include showing agency, bridging divides, and highlighting results-driven processes.
- Key terms: 'GlobalGiving Gift Cards,' 'community-led organizations,' and specific capitalization rules for terms like GG Rewards. Consistency in terms throughout communications is emphasized.

**Notes:**

- Questions regarding the guidelines can be addressed via email or phone, or visits to offices in Washington, DC, and London.
