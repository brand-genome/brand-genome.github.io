## Brand Guidelines

### 2018

**Logo Information:**

- THE LOGO AND SEAL
- The Ivy Tech Community College of Indiana logo serves as the focal point of the brand's graphic identity system. It appears on buildings, signs, stationery, promotional media, and more. The logo is trademarked, and campus or statewide marketing departments must approve all usage prior to production.
- Identity elements include:
- Icon (stacked 'I-V-Y').
- Logotype (the name 'Ivy Tech Community College').
The logo should only use master artwork provided electronically. Redrawing the icon or logotype is prohibited.
- File formats and color options guide:
EPS (Vector, recommended for professional printing).
JPG/PNG (Bitmap for web and internal documents), available in RGB color.
- Minimum size requirements:
- Horizontal logo: Minimum width 2 inches.
- Vertical logo: Minimum width 0.75 inches.
- Guidelines on improper logo use include:
- Treating the logo as part of a phrase, sentence, or headline.
- Changing proportions or colors other than the specified palette.
- Examples of common logo errors and correct/incorrect usage include logo placement, resizing, and color violations.
- Specific colors for logos include combinations of Ivy Tech Green (PANTONE 342 C), black, white, and gray. Icons and logotypes are provided in horizontal and vertical orientations.

**Typography Information:**

- Font family recommendations for body and headlines include:
- Avenir: Six weights from Light to Black.
- Arial: Various styles such as Bold, Italic, and Condensed.
- Trajan: Regular and Bold weights, used sparingly.
- Merriweather: A serif typeface recommended for body text.
- Sans-serif and serif fonts should be used in a complementary fashion, avoiding overuse of one type. Avenir is emphasized for general usage.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Minimum widths for logos are as follows:
- Horizontal logo: Minimum width 2 inches.
- Vertical logo: Minimum width 0.75 inches.
- Alignment and consistent positioning guidelines are essential in maintaining brand integrity, especially across signage and promotional items.

**Notes:**

- This style guide establishes a consistent and uniform brand identity. Questions about usage outside these guidelines should be directed to the Systems Office Marketing department.
