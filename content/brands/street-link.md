## Brand Guidelines

### 2012

**Logo Information:**

- Three versions of the campaign logo are available: 1) With strapline, telephone, and website; 2) With telephone and website; 3) Without strapline, telephone, and website. Each version is available in primary color, grayscale, and white color formats.
- Primary color logos are preferred and should be used whenever possible. Grayscale logos are for use when color printing isn't available. White logos are for darker background materials to ensure legibility.
- Logos are available in EPS, JPG, and PNG formats.
- Minimum logo size: 25mm in width. Use logo version without strapline or website for sizes smaller than 25mm, down to a minimum of 20mm.
- Clear space around the logo is equivalent to the width of the map pin motif element within it.
- Do's: Use only original artwork and follow guidelines for usage. Do Not: Use alternative colors, change typefaces, distort the logo, alter the map pin icon, or remove the map pin.
- The logo can be localized by adding location text. This text is set in Verdana Regular, with -20 character spacing, and it aligns with specific elements of the logo (e.g., map pin center, website). The local name text color should use the blue from the brand’s color palette.

**Color Information:**

- Primary campaign colors: Blue (PMS 3035c, C:81 M:0 Y:0 K:78, HEX:#004054, RGB(0,64,87)), Red (PMS 485c, C:0 M:100 Y:100 K:10, HEX:#d71920, RGB(209,0,25)), Green (PMS 7474c, C:81 M:0 Y:23 K:30, HEX:#008b99, RGB(0,135,152)), Yellow (PMS 123c, C:0 M:18 Y:100 K:5, HEX:#e8bc07, RGB(246,200,0)).

**Typography Information:**

- Primary typeface: Museo (weights available: 300, 500, 700, 900; 300, 500, and 700 are free to download, whereas 900 is paid).
- Secondary typeface: Verdana (weights: Regular, Regular Italic, Bold, Bold Italic). Verdana is a standard font preloaded on most devices.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Illustrations use simple shapes and block colors to portray interventions StreetLink provides. They incorporate a light source to reflect the campaign aspiration (shedding light on homelessness). Images are loosely based on photographs.
- Photography should use only original specially commissioned bright and friendly portraits to emphasize the role of people. Imagery contrasts darker illustrations, with crops designed to highlight individuals and allow space for quotes.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Clear space rules specify that elements around the logo must not encroach on the width equivalent to the map pin motif.
- Spacing rules for localized logos ensure equal distances, such as equal to the diameter of the map pin’s center for spacing between the local name text and logo elements like the strapline.

**Brand Voice:**

- Brand tone of voice guidelines include: Simple (make using the service easy to understand), Urgent (create a sense of urgency so people take action), and Inclusive (avoid language creating division; use terms like 'connect,' 'homeless,' 'rough sleeper/sleeping,' 'people').
- Messaging should motivate people to engage with StreetLink by making service use straightforward and explaining barriers, impacts of homelessness, and the opportunity to connect rough sleepers to local services.
- Key messaging emphasizes public action: makes people aware local services may exist to help rough sleepers, alerts authorities to connect individuals to services/services available.
