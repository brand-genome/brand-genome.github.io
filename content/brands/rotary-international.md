## Brand Guidelines

### 2019

**Logo Information:**

- LOGOMARK construction and inspiration: a magnifying glass and iris. Primary colors: Tangerine orange (#F15A22) and Fuchsia red (#FC024D).
- Do's for logo usage include maintaining clear space and positioning correctly relative to partner logos.
- Don'ts for logo usage include rotating, distorting, reshaping, using on busy backgrounds, or altering colors.

**Color Information:**

- Primary Colors: Fuchsia red (#FC024D) and Tangerine orange (#F15A22).
- Gradient usage: combines Fuchsia red (#FC024D) and Tangerine orange (#F15A22) at a 45-degree angle.
- Color variations to represent different hues, including palettes for Fuchsia and Tangerine.
- Monochrome colors included: Davey's grey (#555555), Dim grey, and Gainsboro.
- Secondary colors include Violet (#501CAA), Forest Green (#009B51), and Buttercup Yellow (#FFD813).

**Typography Information:**

- Primary font: Muli. Characteristics include a minimalist, bold, clean, and modern design. Suitable for digital and mobile-friendly layouts.
- Font styles available: Extra-Light, Light, Regular, Semi-Bold, Bold, Extra-Bold, Black, and their italic variants.
- Typography usage: Bold/Semi-Bold for headlines and sub-headlines, Regular/Light for body copy.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Image style should be vibrant, rich, natural, warm, and candid. Effects should emphasize warmth.
- Illustration style options include character-based designs and scenes that complement brand aesthetic.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Clear spacing rules specify maintaining minimum spacing around logos and graphics.

**Brand Voice:**

- The tone of voice should be friendly, relatable, and positive. Writing should express, resonate with people, and showcase crisp humor.

**Notes:**

- The brand embodies bold, organic, vibrant, and refreshing design qualities. Color and typography should align to affirm these values.

### 2016

**Logo Information:**

- The Rotary brand includes several logo configurations: Masterbrand Signature (official logo), Mark of Excellence (wheel emblem), Rotary Wordmark, and Rotary Wheel. The Mark of Excellence must accompany the Masterbrand signature for proper use.
- Color versions of the Masterbrand logo include full color (Rotary Royal Blue and Gold), one color (100% black, 100% white, Rotary Azure), and reversed versions for dark backgrounds. Full-color is recommended for both printed and digital use.
- Clear space around the Masterbrand signature is defined as the height of the capital 'R' in 'Rotary.' Minimum size for print is 13mm (0.5 inches) and digital usage minimum size is 60px.
- Minimum size for the Mark of Excellence (Rotary Wheel) is defined as 4x the height of the wheel in the Masterbrand signature. The wheel must not be cropped or distorted.
- Single-page communications should feature the Mark of Excellence prominently. For high-impact visuals, the logo can be enlarged to make a bold statement.
- Rules for improper logo usage: Do not crop, distort, or place logos inside non-standard shapes. Use specified Rotary brand colors for logos.

**Color Information:**

- Primary Rotary Leadership Colors include Azure (Pantone 2175C, Hex #0050a2, R0 G93 B170), Sky Blue (Pantone 2202C, Hex #019fcb, R1 G180 B231), and Royal Blue (Pantone 286C, Hex #0c3c7c, R23 G69 B143). The Rotary Gold variant uses Pantone 130C (Hex #f7a81b, R247 G168 B27).
- Additional secondary colors include Cranberry, Violet, Turquoise, and more, alongside a range of neutrals such as Silver, Ash, and Platinum.
- A touch of gold and shades of blue are the primary palette to establish leadership and compassion. The color formulas for each color must be strictly adhered to in print and digital media.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary licensed font: Frutiger (recommended variations: Light Condensed and Bold Condensed). Use ALL CAPS for headlines and subheadings, and regular for dense body text.
- Free alternative font options when Frutiger is unavailable: Arial and Helvetica.
- Typography usage guidelines: Headlines should be all caps, and secondary roles like body text and infographics should use non-condensed styles.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography guidelines suggest focusing on connections and community with images of diverse and active Rotarians. Include natural backgrounds, candid expressions, and avoid posed or heavily edited shots.
- Rules for indoor photography: Use blurred or neutral backgrounds to avoid distracting elements. Beneficiary images must show active engagement with Rotarians.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Clear space rules for logos dictate surrounding space should match the height of the 'R' in the Rotary wordmark. For print communications, size requirements are 13mm minimum for logos.
- Layout for club banners emphasizes positioning the logo at the top-right corner while allowing a live area for custom illustrations.

**Brand Voice:**

- The tone of voice for Rotary should be inspiring, purposeful, and compassionate. Messages should focus on connectivity, leadership, and action.
- Key phrases include 'Join Leaders,' 'Exchange Ideas,' and 'Take Action.' These phrases supplement, but do not replace, main headlines.

**Notes:**

- The guidelines provide a new visual identity system with updated logos, color palettes, and merchandise options to ensure consistent branding across communications.
- Merchandise guidelines include using proper branding on Rotary T-shirts, mugs, banners, and name badges. Fonts specified for merchandise include Frutiger and Arial Narrow.
- Contact information for general inquiries is via graphics@rotary.org and licensing-related inquiries via rilicensingservices@rotary.org.

### 2019

**Logo Information:**

- Rotary Masterbrand Signature and Masterbrand Signature Simplified versions are provided. The Mark of Excellence (the Rotary wheel) is to be used alongside the wordmark for maximum visibility and recognition.
- Various logos for subgroups under Rotary, such as Rotaract, Interact, and others, have separate Masterbrand Signature and Simplified designs. Logos can’t be altered, and club/district names should follow specified formats.
- Clear space for each Masterbrand Signature is defined as the height of the capital 'R' in 'Rotary'.
- Minimum size use requirement for logos: Logos should be no smaller than 1.25 cm (0.5 inches).
- Masterbrand Signature should be used on a contrasting background without outlines, special effects, or substitutes for the wordmark. Elements should not be distorted or rearranged.
- Use the color version of the Masterbrand Signature when printing in full color. For embroidery, consider using a simplified version of the logo.
- Incorrect usage of logos includes distorting elements, replacing the wordmark with a font, applying effects or a holding shape, and separating the Mark of Excellence or cropping any components.

**Color Information:**

- Rotary Leadership Colors include three shades of blue and one gold: Rotary Azure (PMS 2175C), Sky Blue (PMS 2202C), Royal Blue (PMS 286C), and Gold (PMS 130C).
- Secondary colors include Cranberry (PMS 214C), Violet (PMS 2070C), Turquoise (PMS 7466C), and Orange (PMS 2018C).
- Pastels include Slate (PMS 2165C), Lavender (PMS 665C), Mist (PMS 2162C), Moss (PMS 7537C), and others.
- Neutrals include colors like Charcoal, Storm, Smoke, and others.
- Appropriate formulations (Pantone, CMYK, RGB, Hexadecimal) for each color in the palette are provided. Colors should not be altered or screened.
- Rotary Azure is to be used most often, followed by Sky Blue and Royal Blue. Gold should be used sparingly as an accent or highlight color.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary licensed font: Frutiger LT STD. For headlines, main navigation, and secondary headlines. Used in all caps, condensed style for headlines.
- Free font options: Open Sans Condensed and Arial Narrow. These are suggested alternatives for digital applications and Microsoft Office use.
- Secondary licensed font: Sentinel. Used for body text, captions, and callouts. Alternately, Georgia can be used in digital or Office applications.
- When using licensed fonts, appropriate licenses and permissions must be ensured.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography should focus on relationships, community impact, and genuine storytelling. Emphasis on non-posed, documentary-style photographs of Rotary participants in action to showcase community impact.
- Diversity and inclusivity in imagery is strongly encouraged. Whenever possible, show multiple Rotarians and participants.
- Signed, written permissions must be secured for all individuals visible in photos or videos. For minors, parental/guardian permission is required.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Rotary Masterbrand Signature requires a clear space equivalent to the height of the capital 'R' in 'Rotary' surrounding it in all uses.
- Sizes for physical signage, such as road signs, meeting signs, and apparel, are specified. For instance, outdoor signs need specific proportions for the 'Mark of Excellence'.
- Specific sizes for club merchandise like name badges, coffee mugs, T-shirts, and hats are specified for uniformity.

**Brand Voice:**

- Rotary's voice focuses on showcasing itself as a unique and consistent brand of people of action who create lasting change globally and within their communities.
- Messaging should align with key values of uniting leaders, embracing diversity, and inspiring change through humanitarian work.
- International consistency in messaging and communication style is crucial to presenting the Rotary brand as cohesive and professional.

**Notes:**

- Templates and resources such as graphics files for creating compliant logos and merchandise designs are available on the Rotary Brand Center website: rotary.org/brandcenter.
- Merchandise using the old pre-2013 wheel design is strictly prohibited, except for specific legacy items like member pins.
- All products featuring Rotary branding must be approved by Rotary International Licensing Services.

### 2015

**Logo Information:**

- Interact signature consists of Interact wordmark above Rotary endorsement line and the Mark of Excellence (Rotary wheel) in Sky Blue. The Rotary endorsement line must always be used to link back to Rotary.
- Color versions: Full color (Sky Blue), black, white (reversed).
- Logo file formats for various uses: Print (.eps, spot or CMYK), Embroidery (.eps, spot or CMYK), Silkscreen (.eps, spot or CMYK), Word Doc (.png, RGB), PowerPoint (.png, RGB), Digital/Web/Email/Tablet/Mobile (.png, RGB).
- Rotaract signature consists of Rotaract wordmark above Rotary endorsement line and the Mark of Excellence (Rotary wheel) in Cranberry.
- Rotaract logo color versions: Full color (Cranberry), black, white (reversed).
- Rotaract logo file formats for use: Print (.eps, spot or CMYK), Embroidery (.eps, spot or CMYK), Silkscreen (.eps, spot or CMYK), Word Doc (.png, RGB), PowerPoint (.png, RGB), Digital/Web/Email/Tablet/Mobile (.png, RGB).

**Color Information:**

- Sky Blue (Interact): HEX #01b4e7, PMS 2202C, RGB (1, 180, 231), CMYK (96, 0, 6, 0).
- Cranberry (Rotaract): HEX #d91b5c, PMS 214C, RGB (217, 27, 92), CMYK (0, 100, 22, 0).

**Typography Information:**

- Interact and Rotaract club/system use Frutiger LT STD 45 Light for type, with sizes like 12pt provided for various configurations, and Rotary Royal Blue or Sky Blue for text alignment.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Signature positioning and size relationships between wordmark, Rotary endorsement line, and wheel are fixed and must not be altered. Examples include alignment rules (e.g., 0.25" spacing alignment for Rotary logos).

**Notes:**

- The document includes specific clear branding and usage rules for Interact and Rotaract brand identities with details on logo configurations and integrity maintenance.
