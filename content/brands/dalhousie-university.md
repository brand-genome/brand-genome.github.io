### 2022

**Logo Information:**

- The core logo features a geometrically refined eagle and clean font, making it versatile across branded touchpoints. It introduces 'beak gold' for vibrancy and includes horizontal versions in full color, one color (black), and reverse (white).

**Color Information:**

- Dalhousie's brand colors are identified as a crucial element of brand identity, presented as complementary to the logo. They are highlighted as important to maintaining recognition and consistency in branding.

**Typography Information:**

- The primary typeface is Public Sans—a neutral font suitable for interfaces, text, and headings. It comes with multiple weights, such as Light, Medium, Bold, and Black (and their italics).
- The secondary typeface, Franklin Gothic, is used when Public Sans isn't available, such as in specific software or contexts. It supports weights like Book, Demi, Medium, and Heavy.
- Accessibility guidelines emphasize clean design, wide margins, high contrast, appropriate font size (12-18 points), spacing between lines, and the use of Dalhousie’s official fonts, all improving readability.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Dalhousie communicates its brand visually through authentic photography and video showcasing diversity, fostering inclusiveness, and emphasizing real emotion over stock imagery.
- Core brand imagery captures attention with wide-open spaces, unique textures, and authenticity to reflect Dalhousie's values, avoiding artificial or staged appearances.
- Campus life imagery focuses on real, candid moments that highlight learning and research environments, ensuring the presentation appears natural and unposed.

**Brand Voice:**

- Dalhousie's voice is vibrant, curious, driven, aspiring, and open/inclusive, ensuring consistent communication across platforms. It avoids jargon while celebrating shared values and diversity.
- Tone is context-sensitive, adapted to audiences (e.g., students, colleagues) to ensure alignment with expectations and values. It incorporates positivity, authenticity, and empathy.
