## Brand Guidelines

### 2021

**Logo Information:**

- The UNDERLINE in 'GROUP' of the logo is based on a serif font in 1997 and transitioned to DIN 2014 font by 2020. Simplified logo focuses on minimalism and modern representation.
- Logo evolution emphasizes modernity and digital relevance in the design aesthetic.

**Color Information:**

- Main color palette includes White (cleanness, perfection), Black (strength, luxury), Violet (creativity, wisdom), Red (dynamism, passion), and Orange (motivation, warmth). Color sharing percentages are 60% White, 30% Black, and 10% accent colors.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary typography is 'DIN 2014' used for professionally designed marketing materials. Secondary typography is 'Barlow' for in-house materials like presentations. Tertiary typography is 'Calibri Light' for email and open documents.
- DIN 2014 replaces the earlier serif font for 'GROUP'. This reflects Unity Group's modern, digital brand identity.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photos emphasize professional yet personal traits of working people, showing their openness and satisfaction in activities. Focuses on people, both in profile and at work.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Key Visuals use selective blocks of colors and asymmetrical, arranged geometry. White serves as the base background while accent colors (red and black) highlight critical information.

**Brand Voice:**

- Uses US English; tone is professionally informal. Avoids boasting and emphasizes client value rather than self-promotion.
- Claims are used exclusively in English for brand-specific communication.

**Notes:**

- Unity Duck serves as a mascot for employer branding. Friendly and humorous, the mascot represents company culture but is not used in client or sales-focused materials.
- Unity Group contrasts traditional expertise with modern trends, emphasizing positive impressions through clean design and minimalism.
