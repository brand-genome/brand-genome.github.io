## Brand Guidelines

### 2017

**Logo Information:**

- The new wordmark is the core part of Bombora's logo identity. The logo no longer uses the dot symbol.
- Guidelines on how to style the logo: examples include using the blue logo on white, black logo on white, and white logo on various background colors (e.g., dark blue, orange, grey).
- Do not use colors outside the brand palette, noisy or low-contrast backgrounds, or attempt to recreate the logo using fonts. Do not stretch the logo or use the blue logo with colors other than white.
- Clear space guidelines: Maintain spacing around the logo that equals at least half the height of the letter 'o' in the wordmark.

**Color Information:**

- Primary color palette includes blue (C87 M42 Y17 K1 / R00 G125 B170 / #007DAA), white, and black. Accent colors include dark orange, sand, grey, dark blue, dark grey, orange. Colors are used for various elements like illustrations and buttons.
- White logo styling is applied consistently over backgrounds like dark blue, orange, grey, and dark sand imagery.

**Typography Information:**

- Typography for PowerPoint: Trebuchet MS (Regular, Italic, Bold), known for readability on screens and print.
- Typography for website: Uses a font named Matteson from Google Fonts, designed to be legible on screen and print.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography style includes focusing on beach, ocean, and wave imagery. Semi-transparent overlays ensure consistency and text legibility.
- Categories of photography include strong waves, calm waves, and beaches.
- Use white (#FFFFFF) bold font weights for headings to ensure text is readable on imagery.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Ensure consistent margin around the logo equivalent to half the height of the 'o' character in the wordmark.

**Brand Voice:**

- Bombora's imagery and typography reflect approachability, trust, and boldness. Semi-transparent overlays reinforce consistent messaging.

**Notes:**

- Brushstroke textures are used across website and materials to add emotion and personality.
