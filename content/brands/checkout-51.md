## Brand Guidelines

### 2017

**Logo Information:**

- The primary logo is the preferred version for use. In cases where spacing does not allow for proper application of the logo, a stripped-down secondary icon can be used.
- Clear space around the logo and icon must be equal to the height of the 'k' in the logo, ensuring maximum legibility and impact.
- Minimum size requirements must be followed: 0.75 inches for print (logo), 0.2 inches for print (icon) and 75px for digital (logo), 22px for digital (icon).
- Logo or icon usage on different backgrounds: It can appear in green on white or light backgrounds (preferred), white on colored/dark backgrounds, white on uncluttered/blurred photos, and black only when color or white options are not feasible.
- Do not alter the logo in any way, including adding effects, changing colors, or rearranging its elements.
- Checkout 51 logo and store badge lockups are used as the main call to action for non-clickable communication materials. Either 'Get the app' or 'URL' lockup can be applied depending on layout.
- In partnerships, the logo should maintain visual weight parity with the partner logo(s). Logos are separated by a vertical bar (0.5pt stroke, the height of the circle), and the minimum 'k' clear space is maintained.

**Color Information:**

- Primary Color: Checkout Green (CMYK: 60, 0, 83, 0, HEX: #6AC460).
- Secondary Color Palette includes Beeswax (CMYK: 0, 30, 78, 0, HEX: #fdba52 for secondary CTA), Blue Curacao (CMYK: 63, 0, 22, 0, HEX: #4ac3cc for secondary alert), Ian Cinnabar (CMYK: 3, 87, 71, 0, HEX: #e84a4a for errors and warnings), Light Grey (CMYK: 70, 61, 64, 58, HEX: #323532 for copy), and Regatta (CMYK: 74, 28, 0, 0, HEX: #2e96d3 for main CTA/action).
- Our colors are light, energizing, and expressive; they convey freedom and positivity. All secondary palette colors are used to highlight and draw attention by contrasting with the primary palette. None of the colors should vary but can be adjusted tonally (adding white/black) to complement a design.

**Typography Information:**

- The official font family is Avenir LT Pro. Avenir Light is used for headlines. Avenir Heavy is used for subheaders or main headlines (coupled with Avenir Roman body copy). Avenir Roman is for body copy and supporting text.
- Type appearing in 16pt or larger should use -25 tracking for proper kerning.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography should be clean, bright, and inviting. Avoid overly produced photos; people should not look posed or directly into the camera. Favor relatable scenes (e.g., families). Food photography should appear natural, appetizing, and highlight aesthetic qualities. Use photos incorporating Checkout 51 brand colors wherever possible.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Clear space around the logo and icon must be the same as the height of 'k' for all sides to maximize legibility and impact.
- Separate logos in partnership materials using a vertical bar of 0.5pt stroke, maintaining the minimum 'k' clear space.

**Brand Voice:**

- The brand voice is informal, approachable, relevant, and sometimes cheeky. It avoids technical jargon. Ad copy should aim to be relatable, not overly 'sales-y.' Copy can be funny, witty, or edgy (avoiding vulgarity) to grab attention. All Calls-To-Action (CTAs) should start with action words.

**Notes:**

- Animations should feel bouncy, playful, and informal, employing techniques like overshoot for a natural effect. Avoid animating opacity—introduce objects by animating movement or scaling from outside the frame. Animations must use easing techniques (ease-in/ease-out) at 30 FPS for smoothness.
- When screens from the app are used in communication, ensure they are up-to-date with the current app design. Use white phones for mockups to align with the brand's clean and bright image.
- Use the checklist on the last page to ensure guidelines are followed, including proper use of logo, colors, typography, photography, app screens, and CTAs.
