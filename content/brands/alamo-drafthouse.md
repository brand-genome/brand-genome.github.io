## Brand Guidelines

### 2017

**Logo Information:**

- The Alamo Drafthouse Cinema logo represents the entire company, including show, merchandising, advertising, and sponsorships. The logo consists of the words 'ALAMO DRAFTHOUSE CINEMA' set in a custom logotype within a marquee housing. It is available in reversed variations and various formats suitable for print, on-screen, and online applications.
- General usage rules specify that the logo be used as a complete unit, and words or elements should not be used independently. The logo can be reproduced in one or two colors and in reverse (or negative) when appropriate.
- The Alamo Drafthouse Cinema condensed logo is a secondary mark designed for small sizes. It is preferred for online usage and social media profiles. It must also be used in scenarios where patrons are familiar with the primary brand, such as inside cinemas on employee uniforms and merchandise.
- Clear space rule: The minimum amount of clear space around the logo is the height of the 'A' in the logotype. The logo must never be crowded by other design elements.
- Minimum size requirements: For print, the full logo must not be smaller than 1.375 inches wide, and for web, not smaller than 125 pixels wide. The condensed logo may be used as small as 0.35 inches wide for print and 25 pixels wide for web.
- Incorrect uses of the logo include using unapproved colors, compressing or expanding it, using the logo without the shield, rotating or flipping it, using older versions, recreating it, applying gradients or special effects, filling the shield with patterns or photographs, or placing the logo inside another shape.
- There are two logo versions: Standard and Reversed. Use of the standard version is recommended for backgrounds with white to medium gray values. The reversed version is appropriate for darker backgrounds.
- Color separation rules: Two Color versions use Black (Black C) for the housing and white (paper stock) for the interior. One Color versions use either Black (Black C) or white (paper stock or ink) as the entire logo.
- Logo placement over colors or imagery must prioritize visibility and readability of all signature elements. The two-color logo is recommended for backgrounds, with proper contrast maintained.

**Color Information:**

- The Alamo Drafthouse Cinema color palette includes primary, secondary, and neutral colors. It is crucial to use these colors to maintain brand consistency.
- Primary color: PMS 7409 (Pantone 7409). HEX: #FEB811. RGB: R:254 G:184 B:17. CMYK: C:0 M:29 Y:100 K:0.
- Secondary colors include PMS 1795 (Pantone 1795), HEX: #E52521, RGB: R:229 G:37 B:33, CMYK: C:0 M:93 Y:95 K:0. Additionally, PMS 2612 (Pantone 2612), HEX: #7E44A1, RGB: R:126 G:68 B:161, CMYK: C:67 M:87 Y:0 K:0.
- Neutral colors include Black (hexadecimal Black C, #000000) and White (hexadecimal White, #FFFFFF). These colors are used extensively in combination with the primary and secondary palettes.
- The color rules specify the acceptable usage of the primary and secondary palettes across various mediums, including ensuring proper contrast and avoiding diluted or overly saturated variations.

**Typography Information:**

- The Alamo Drafthouse Cinema corporate typeface is 'Trade Gothic'. It is used across all marketing collateral and communications to maintain consistency.
- Trade Gothic weights include Light, Regular, Bold, and Condensed. Usage varies across different hierarchies of text.
- Headlines should predominantly use Trade Gothic Bold Condensed to provide impact and draw attention.
- Body copy should use Trade Gothic Regular or Trade Gothic Light for readability. These fonts are appropriate for paragraphs and extended content.
- For special formats like captions or disclaimers, Trade Gothic Condensed or Light Condensed may be used.
- Size guidelines: Body text should be no smaller than 8pt, and headlines should maintain proportions with design hierarchy.
- Typographic hierarchy is critical. Headlines, subheadings, and body text must be styled differently to establish clear structure and flow in layouts.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography style is described as vintage-inspired, cinematic, and focused on storytelling. Images should evoke a sense of nostalgia and creativity.
- Image treatments include adjusting lighting and color grading to achieve a mid-century film aesthetic with warm tones.
- Avoid overusing filters or effects that detract from the intended cinematic look. Authenticity in photographic style is critical.
- Composition guidelines: Focus on balance, symmetry, and intentional use of negative space to draw attention to the subject.
- Lighting should be natural or staged to match the overall mood and ambiance of Alamo Drafthouse Cinema’s brand. Avoid unnatural and harsh shadows.

**Spacing Layout:**

- All layouts must adhere to an established grid system. The grid ensures order and structure across all mediums.
- Recommended margins: At least 0.5 inches for printed materials and 15 pixels for digital assets.
- Elements such as logos, headlines, and body copy must align to grid lines to maintain consistency.
- For digital layouts, maintain a safe zone of at least 20 pixels around key elements to ensure responsiveness and legibility.
- Alignment: Text should predominantly be left-aligned unless a specific design requires center or right alignment for emphasis.

**Brand Voice:**

- The Alamo Drafthouse Cinema brand voice is fun, honest, and authentic. It reflects the company's unique culture and aims to create a personal connection with patrons.
- Tone of voice descriptors: Conversational, witty, creative, and sometimes irreverent. The objective is to sound approachable and relatable rather than overly formal.
- Key messaging must reflect a blend of vintage mid-century movie culture and the rebellious spirit of D.I.Y. culture, creating an engaging narrative.
- Do's: Emphasize originality, express authentic enthusiasm, and maintain consistency in tone across all platforms.
- Don'ts: Avoid corporate jargon, over-promising, and language that doesn't align with the brand's adventurous and creative identity.

**Notes:**

- All public-facing designs must be approved by the Design Department. This applies to all venues and personnel unless they have completed Design Certification.
- For design or branding queries, contact design@drafthouse.com. Updated assets can be accessed at drafthouse.com/branding.
