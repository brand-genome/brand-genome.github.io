## Brand Guidelines

### 2009

**Logo Information:**

- The MassArt logo uses a cropped 'S' in 'Mass' and an angled 'A' in 'Art' to bring 'Art' to the foreground. It conveys the institution's history and commitment to making art and design prominent.
- Primary logo configuration includes 'Massachusetts College of Art and Design' in full, with 'Art and Design' elements following the color of 'Art' in the logo. Alternate configurations are included for use when horizontal or vertical space constraints require.
- The logo is built from a customized version of the Gotham typeface, emphasizing urban modernity and legibility across various mediums. Simplicity ensures compatibility with imagery and a contemporary style.
- Three primary color options and gray are available for the logo. Art elements are in primary colors (pages 10, 18 for specifics). Alternate logo designs ensure flexibility in visual expression.

**Color Information:**

- The logo utilizes three primary colors along with gray, reflecting foundational elements of art and providing visual flexibility.

**Typography Information:**

- The logo uses a customized Gotham typeface. Gotham’s draftsman-like qualities reflect MassArt’s modern, urban character, emphasizing legibility across applications such as signage, letterheads, and web.

**Imagery Photography:**

- The logo and imagery merge to ensure the overall simplicity and timelessness of various media, allowing images to complement text fluidly.

**Notes:**

- The guide emphasizes consistent application of the logo and visual elements to reflect the college’s excellence and commitment to art education.
- The visual identity system positions MassArt as an influential and credible institution, reflecting its cultural and artistic stature.
