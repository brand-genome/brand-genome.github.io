## Brand Guidelines

### 2018

**Logo Information:**

- The institutional logo is used strictly for institutional purposes (letterhead, business cards, correspondence cards ...) or when the City of Nevers must appear as a partner.
- Clear space rules for the institutional logo are defined as twice the thickness of the chevrons surrounding it.
- The logo exists only in blue on a white background or white on a blue background. Exceptionally, it can be used in white on photographic or color backgrounds, ensuring it remains readable.
- Minimum sizes for logo usage: 15mm in height or 17.5mm in width.
- When used on a photographic background, the white version of the logo must be applied. Special attention should be paid to the logo's readability, and if necessary, use a white background behind the logo.
- On colored backgrounds, the white version of the logo must be used, ensuring proper readability.
- Misuse of logo: It must not be distorted, modified, or disassembled in any way.

**Color Information:**

- The primary colors for Nevers branding: Blue of Nevers, derived from cobalt oxide used in faience since the 16th century, serves as the city's identity color.

**Typography Information:**

- The primary font is 'Modelica,' characterized as minimal, robust, and pragmatic with excellent readability even in small sizes.
- The geometric design of Modelica complements the structural simplicity and strength of the logo, reinforcing the visual identity.

**Imagery Photography:**

- In flyers, designs can use arrows in three colors at a maximum. Using transparency levels between 85-90% is encouraged.

**Spacing Layout:**

- The logo is positioned on the bottom right within its triangle while slogans are placed diagonally opposite in institutional posters.
- Typography size for the slogan on institutional material should be approximately half the size of the title text font size.

**Brand Voice:**

- In institutional communication, a slogan can be added to the design in the top-left corner. The associated triangle should match the size of the logo’s triangle.
- For co-signature communication (e.g., with other institutions), the top-left corner is reserved for the co-signing logo, and the bottom-right remains dedicated to the City of Nevers logo.

**Notes:**

- The document emphasizes the importance of solid geometric and minimal designs in conveying stability, simplicity, and openness, as seen in the logo design derived from arrows forming an 'N'.
- The blue color used is derived from Nevers' historical use of cobalt oxide in faience manufacturing, dating back to the 16th century.
