## Brand Guidelines

### 2018

**Logo Information:**

- Logo marks
- Say 'hi' to our primary logotype. It’s very friendly and welcoming…
- The logo’s orientation should always be horizontal for best readability.
- Primary logo is a single color: use Guru Green on a white background or white on colored background.
- Black/gray colored versions acceptable when color is unavailable.
- RGB logos in PNG format for screens; CMYK logos in JPEG format for print applications.
- Clear space: Min 25px around logos.
- Minimum size requirement for print: 0.25 inches; 25px for digital.
- Main tagline: FF Mark Bold, secondary tagline: FF Mark Regular. Should occupy 1/3rd height of logo.
- Icon-only logo ('G logo') used in Guru Green or white; suitable for small spaces.

**Color Information:**

- Guru Green (Primary brand color): PMS 355 U | HEX #00D084 | RGB (0, 208, 132) | CMYK (88, 0, 72, 0).
- Navy: PMS 296 U | HEX #00324A | RGB (0, 50, 74) | CMYK (100, 40, 27, 81).
- Pink is used to signify 'stale/outdated knowledge' or negative associations.
- Gradient and low-opacity halftone patterns recommended to break up large color blocks.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary font: FF Mark (Bold and Regular). Friendly, clean, preferred typeface.
- Secondary font for web-based applications (e.g., Google Slides): Montserrat.
- FF Mark Bold, 24 pts: Used for headlines, lowercase or sentence case (never all caps).
- FF Mark Regular, 16 pts: Used for body text. Avoid font sizes under 10 pts.
- Uppercase typography discouraged; sentence case preferred for readability.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Images and diagrams preferred over text for ease of communication.
- Backgrounds should remain white; custom visuals preferred over generic templates.
- Neutral and dramatic, black-and-white aesthetic for headshots; color versions available upon request.
- Icons and illustrations should remain simple; avoid combining elements unnecessarily.

**Brand Voice:**

- Tone descriptors: Empowering, empathetic, witty, serious (adjusted per audience).
- Know your audience: Dial up/down wit and poise based on the context (e.g., sales call vs blog post).
- Tone on Twitter: Push boundaries for culture/personality showcase; less focus on business gains.
- Content guidelines: Aim to establish thought-leader status while balancing humor engagingly.

**Notes:**

- Core values of the brand include transparency, grit, advocacy, and a focus on human connection.
- Employees/customers required to share high-quality headshots for publication alignment.
- Business cards feature headshots to enhance personal touch.
- Social media banners available for LinkedIn and Twitter; optional for employees.
