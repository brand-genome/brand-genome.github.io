## Brand Guidelines

### 1970

**Color Information:**

- Color coding for each subway line using an eight-color code with letters and numbers visible as white text on colored discs. Colors are reproduced for directional signage and train maps. If necessary, reduction of size for discs is permitted, but it must be done photographically. Specific swatch tables must be matched by suppliers to ensure proper line distinction.

**Typography Information:**

- Type sizes and leading for signage. Standard modular grids are defined using uppercase x-height as the reference. Uppercase x-height specifies proportions for spacing and text leading. In exceptional cases, proportional reductions are permitted, but consistency must remain across the signage. Modular grids are divided as: Grid 3 uses x/height Upper Case, Grid 2 at 4 3/4", and Grid 1 at 1 5/8".

**Spacing Layout:**

- Diagram of Basic Sign Distribution defines layout for station signage placement, including features for street level station name signs, maps, directional flow signs, exit/transfer signs, and station identification signage. Double-sided signage structure is visually aligned; single-sided messages still follow dual-side sign structures (color white).
- Diagram of the Information Tree presents the layout sequence for subway riders, dividing signage categories into Identification (station names), Directional (station-to-train wayfinding), and Informational (general and transportation-related info). Signs are placed based on decision points only — no pre-emptive or delayed placement.

**Brand Voice:**

- Focused design philosophy aiming to eliminate visual clutter and ensure clarity of communication in subway environments. Riders are provided information only at the point of decision to reduce confusion. Categories include Identification, Directional, and Informational signage for streamlined navigation.

**Notes:**

- Manual explains a detailed signage system for visual harmony in subway navigation. Deviations from the design norms are discouraged. Signage removal for old installations mandated to avoid overlap. Future revisions and issuance of updated manual pages stated explicitly.
