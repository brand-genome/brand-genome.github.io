## Brand Guidelines

### 2018

**Logo Information:**

- Clear area and minimum size rules for the logo: The clear area surrounding the logo must be kept free of text and graphics with measurements determined by the height of the 'n' in the logotype. Additionally, the logo's gear icon should not appear smaller than 1/4 inch (4.8mm).
- Incorrect logo usage: Do not reproduce the corporate signature in colors other than UTC Blue, black, or white. Avoid placing imagery inside elements of the corporate signature, adding boxes, using drop shadows, or distorting, stacking, skewing, compressing, or stretching the signature. Specific visual examples provided.

**Color Information:**

- Primary corporate color is UTC Blue, with color usage detailed for digital and print applications. The color values for UTC Blue are: Pantone: PMS 286, CMYK: 100%/80%/0%/6%, RGB: 0/51/149, HEX: #0033AB.
- Accent palette colors provided include Verdigris (Pantone: PMS 3262, CMYK: 76/0/38/0, HEX: #00BCB4), and Yellow Green (HEX: #75B80C, RGB: 117/184/12).
- Grayscale and interface response colors include various shades of gray (e.g., Gray 200 HEX: #F4F4F4, RGB: 244/244/244). Guidelines state darker shades can be used for text, with lighter shades reserved for borders, lines, and backgrounds.

**Typography Information:**

- The primary typeface used is a sans-serif font with legible numbers that render well at all sizes. Larger and body-sized text is used for primary content, while smaller text is used for secondary content. All content must meet the WCAG 2.0 Level AA contrast ratio (minimum 4.5:1).
- Guidelines on headers: Proper semantic headings (H1 to H5) must be used to create a clear hierarchy. Each heading is distinct in aesthetic and value.
- Paragraph style specifications: Guidelines for lead, editorial, body, and legal paragraphs are provided. Editorial paragraph examples include specific text content (e.g., 'Please check your network connection and try again.').

**Imagery Photography:**

- Product imagery guidelines emphasize simple, straight angles that highlight the size, elegance, and simplicity of machines. Focused or macro shots aim to emphasize intricate details, particularly areas where UT Digital has an impact.
- People imagery must feel candid, authentic, and inclusive. Interaction and collaboration shots should highlight inclusivity and emphasize the role of UT Digital teams.
- Lighting guidelines for photography: Lighting should feel natural and soft for wide-angle product and environmental shots. Narrow and macro shots can use lighting to enhance detail.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Grid spacing guidelines dictate the minimum clear space around logos and text, determined by the height of the 'n' in the logo.

**Brand Voice:**

- The tone of the brand is described as calculated, monotone, and flat, but it emphasizes human interaction to breathe life and energy into the products. The narrative highlights the intersection of human and machine innovation.

**Notes:**

- Email templates and signatures: Email templates combine bold colors, sharp typography, and clean photography. Pre-formatted templates must be requested via a dedicated email address.
