## Brand Guidelines

### 2020

**Logo Information:**

- The primary logo is fixed artwork representing the PAAB brand, designed for use on both dark and light-colored backgrounds. It is specified as the preferred version for maintaining brand identity.
- The secondary logo is reserved for cases where the primary logo cannot be used, such as one-color (black and white) print materials or placement over photography with proper contrast.
- Clear space and sizing specifications state that the logo must be surrounded by a margin equivalent to half the height of the logo to create an isolation area. This ensures legibility and brand consistency.
- Incorrect usages of the logo include modifications, addition of special effects, distortion, opacity changes, border removal, rotation, and other unauthorized alterations. Approved art files must only be used.

**Color Information:**

- PAAB Navy Blue (Pantone 2755): C-99 M-98 Y-35 K-31, R-36 G-33 B-85
- PAAB Light Blue (Pantone 7683): C-73 M-51 Y-18 K-1, R-85 G-118 B-161
- PAAB Leaf Red1 (Pantone 185): C-0 M-100 Y-100 K-0, R-237 G-28 B-36
- PAAB Leaf Red2 (Pantone 7621): C-18 M-100 Y-100 K-11, R-184 G-31 B-37
- PAAB Leaf Red3 (Pantone 1815): C-29 M-100 Y-100 K-39, R-124 G-19 B-21
- PAAB Blue (Pantone 325): C-55 M-0 Y-20 K-0, R-104 G-201 B-208
- PAAB Green (Pantone 380): C-19 M-0 Y-98 K-0, R-217 G-224 B-39
- Consistent use of these primary colors aids in PAAB brand visibility and recognition.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Secondary logo placement over photography must ensure appropriate contrast by positioning it over dark areas or negative space.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Logo clear space requires margins equivalent to half the height of the logo to maintain isolation and legibility.

**Brand Voice:**

- Our brand identity is the face of PAAB, as important as the services we provide, and must not be compromised. This guide maintains brand integrity.
- Change was inspired by purpose, focusing on trustworthiness and relevance. The logo conveys APS independent review to meet high standards supporting health and safety.

**Notes:**

- Logo must only be reproduced from approved art files and not redrawn, re-proportioned, or modified.
- The new PAAB logo will be used on all APS approved after October 1, 2020. APS approved before this date are grandfathered until approval expiry, i.e., October 1, 2021.
- Contact information for inquiries regarding brand guidelines is Jennifer Carroll, Senior Reviewer of Communications (jenniferc@paab.ca).
- Files for branding resources can be downloaded at www.paab.ca/resources.htm.
