## Brand Guidelines

### 2019

**Logo Information:**

- The WorldSkills logo consists of two components: the icon and the wordmark. The icon represents the hands of youth reaching out for new skills. It is monochrome and can be used in dark blue, white, black, or grey. The wordmark, based on Frutiger font, can be separated from the icon only when design constraints require it.
- Clear space around the WorldSkills logo is defined as the x-height of the lowercase letters in the wordmark. A simple background without busy patterns is required to maintain readability.

**Color Information:**

- Primary colors: Pantone 1585C, 0821C, 7704C, 2955C, 2617C, 214C, and 102C. Corresponding HEX values: #00B219, #E40134, #FFD923, #005CB9, #FF420D, #E80CB0. CMYK values and RGB values are also specified for these colors.
- Color roles: Colors are categorized into 'Belgium Team', 'Skill Sectors', and 'Corporate'. They are used to reflect the engaging and energetic nature of skills.

**Typography Information:**

- Frutiger is the primary font family used across the brand. The specified weights are: Frutiger 45 Light, Frutiger 46 Light Italic, Frutiger 55 Roman, Frutiger 56 Italic, Frutiger 65 Bold, Frutiger 75 Black, Frutiger 95 Ultra Black.
- Inria Serif is another font family used for variation and emphasis in designs.
- If Frutiger is unavailable, Arial is used as a substitute.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Imagery style is described as youthful, energetic, and inspiring, reflecting skills development and building a prosperous future.

**Spacing Layout:**

- The building blocks are versatile elements that may be open or closed, solid or empty. They can be stacked, rotated, and resized while maintaining clarity. Blocks may also be removed to accommodate imagery or text.
- Callouts are used to emphasize a statement and assist with layout design when using large images.

**Brand Voice:**

- The brand inspires young people to develop a passion for skills and pursuing excellence through competitions and promotions. Focus is on professional excellence, self-esteem, pride, and creativity.
- The brand voice encourages societal change by helping young people enhance their skills and prosper in economic and social terms.

**Notes:**

- The WorldSkills logo is designed by Teaho Yang, a graphic design student, in 2000 following a global design competition for students.
- The visual identity consists of key components including the logo, colors, building blocks, fonts, and imagery. These elements work together to present the WorldSkills brand.
