## Brand Guidelines

### 2021

**Logo Information:**

- The logo should be used in its entirety with no alterations or additional elements. Versions include full color, outlined, alternative, and full color (reverse). Ensure clarity of the logo on backgrounds—use full color on light backgrounds and reverse logo on darker and black backgrounds. Logos can be placed over photography as long as the elements remain visible.
- Exclusion zone is determined by the width of the capital 'C' (x). No predetermined sizes exist; proportion should be maintained based on available space while ensuring visibility. Minimum size for all versions is 40mm. The logo cannot be stretched, squeezed, or resized disproportionately.

**Color Information:**

- Main brand colors include green, blue, red, and yellow. Greens and blues convey seriousness, while secondary colors add differentiation and vibrancy—used in diagrams and icons. Primary and secondary colors should be used purposefully.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary font is Circular Std, used confidently for headlines, standfirsts, pull-outs, and statistics. Circular Std Bold is used for impact, while Circular Std Book is used for regular text settings.
- Secondary font is Brandon Grotesque, associated with clarity and definition. Supports the primary font and is applied in sub-headings and body copy. Brandon Grotesque Bold and Regular weights are specified for respective roles.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Illustration style is sophisticated yet approachable with an isometric look and a comic book feel that reinforces the Cape 'hero message.' Premium pieces should strive to include illustrations supporting literal or abstract messages.
- Illustration creation should reference the Vectorpouch on Adobe Stock for inspiration. Most assets can be purchased and tailored for specific use. Key considerations include isometric design, Cape red/blue combinations, and dotted shadows.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Exclusion zones around logos ensure isolation from encroaching text, headlines, or visual elements. Width of the capital 'C' determines space requirements.

**Notes:**

- The guide emphasizes consistency in application and defines the brand as a blend of visual and written expressions. Key contents include logo, color, typography, imagery, and illustrations.
