## Brand Guidelines

### 2009

**Logo Information:**

- The Honeywell logotype is the company's primary identifier. It is used in three approved ways: Freestanding logotype, Logotype in bar, and Logotype with rule. The Honeywell logo should be reproduced in Honeywell Red and may be reproduced in black for one-color materials. A clear zone, equal to the height of the capital 'H' in any logo size, must surround the logotype.
- Logotype modifications are restricted in case of partnerships or joint ventures. The logotype may not be used to develop a partnership logo, combine with another logo, or create artwork unless specifically approved.
- The Honeywell logotype must not be incorporated into joint venture logos. It may appear on marketing materials produced by joint ventures if appropriate.

**Color Information:**

- Honeywell builds color equity using Primary, Secondary, and Tertiary palettes. Primary colors: Honeywell Red (Pantone 485C, CMYK: 0, 95, 100, 0; HEX: #DE0031), Total Black (CMYK: 0, 0, 0, 100; HEX: #191A00), Brilliant White (CMYK: 0, 0, 0, 0; HEX: #FFFFFF). Secondary colors: Dark Gray (Pantone 431C, CMYK: 11, 0, 0, 65; HEX: #666666), Light Gray (Pantone 428C, CMYK: 0, 0, 0, 23; HEX: #D6D7D6). Tertiary colors include Deep Blue, Dark Green, Light Green, Bright Blue, and Tan.

**Typography Information:**

- Helvetica is the chosen typeface for most applications due to its contemporary, simple, and readable style. Selected weights of Helvetica are used exclusively. Arial may be substituted for internal communications and presentations such as PowerPoint.

**Spacing Layout:**

- A clear zone or control field is required around the freestanding logotype. This space must be equal to the height of the capital 'H' in any logo size to avoid graphic elements intruding.

**Notes:**

- For more information on Honeywell branding, logo usage, and partnerships, visit http://www.honeywell.com/sites/brandM/masterband.htm.
- For joint ventures with approved unique names and logos, Honeywell must appear below the joint venture name or logo, connected by a defining line stating 'A joint venture of Honeywell and (partner name)'.

### 2014

**Logo Information:**

- The Honeywell logo is presented as a freestanding brandmark, brandmark with rule, and brandmark in bar. Freestanding logos should only be used with a white, black, or gray background. Backgrounds should not contain other colors or images unless the brandmark in bar is used.
- Do's and Don'ts for logo usage: Do not manipulate, condense, or change logo orientation. Do not reverse logos out of a background (exception: brandmark in bar). Do not stack letters vertically or use outlines or shadows.
- Control fields (clear space): For freestanding logos, a clear zone equal to the height of the capital 'H' in the Honeywell logo should be maintained around it with no graphic elements intruding.
- File quality for reproducing logos: Always use camera-ready artwork or authorized downloadable files.
- Minimum size rules and control zones for logos where necessary.
- Brandmark in bar usage: Must bleed on 3 sides (top, left, right) when used. Can only be reproduced in Honeywell Red with reversed white text or black in one-color materials.

**Color Information:**

- Honeywell primary color palette: Honeywell Red (Pantone 485C, HEX: #EE3124), Total Black (CMYK: 0 0 0 100, HEX #191A00), Brilliant White (CMYK: 0 0 0 0, HEX: #FFFFFF).
- Honeywell Enraf primary color palette: In addition to Honeywell colors, Enraf Green (Pantone 376C, HEX: #84BD00) and Enraf Logo Grey (Pantone 431C, HEX: #666666).
- RMG by Honeywell primary color palette: Includes RMG Logo Blue (Pantone 295C, HEX: #00417E) and RMG Indigo (Pantone 653C, HEX: #00639D).
- Color rules: For offset print, Pantone spot colors should be used. CMYK matching values are to be applied if Pantone is unavailable. RGB or Web values are specifically applied for digital media.

**Typography Information:**

- Standard typefaces: Helvetica (Light, Medium, Bold) is used exclusively. Arial font in matching weights may be substituted internally.
- Usage requirements: Text requiring special emphasis (e.g., Authorized Distributor or System Integrator) must use Helvetica Medium beneath the Honeywell logo.

**Imagery Photography:**

- The Global Arc: Represents a global perspective and proprietary cropping for imagery. Preferred use in most communications, placing images above the arc and text below.
- Photographic style: High-quality images cropped using the Global Arc to create unique, proprietary visuals aligned with Honeywell’s branding.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Global Arc placement: Clear rules about the vertical placement of the arc for advertisement and literature covers (lowest, preferred, and highest placements).
- Spacing rules for logo applications: H-height distance required above and below logos. The clear zone applies uniformly.

**Brand Voice:**

- Tone of communication: Consistent tone, theme, and graphical style in all advertisements, ensuring a unified Honeywell voice.
- Prohibited: Use of brandmark within sentences, headlines, or as part of phrases. Only 'Honeywell', 'Honeywell Enraf', or 'RMG by Honeywell' may be used as plain text.

**Notes:**

- Business cards: Templates shown with clear rules on placing Honeywell logos, titles, and distributor information.
- Promotional materials: Guidelines provided for posters, banners, collateral, and automobiles, emphasizing brand consistency.
