## Brand Guidelines

### 2014

**Logo Information:**

- All images contained in this document are available from Strategic Communications, Department of Premier and Cabinet. Images are available in EPS, JPG and PNG formats.
- The NSW NOW logo was introduced in March 2013 as part of an awareness building campaign targeting potential investors in NSW from interstate and overseas.
- The NSW Government logo is the primary corporate branding emblem for all NSW Government agencies and entities. Inspired by the floral emblem of NSW, it holds importance in Aboriginal history.
- Approved logo styles include Style 1 (Single horizontal line), Style 2 (Double horizontal line), Style 3 (Triple horizontal line), and Style 4 (Vertical Stack).
- Stylized graphic use of the logo includes cropping to the minimum recommended. Waratah may appear as a stylized watermark, with max 15% opacity on light backgrounds and 75% on dark.
- The minimum size for reproducing the NSW Government corporate logo is 10mm (28px) in height.

**Color Information:**

- Primary logo color palette consists of two shades of red (Red1: RGB R=198 G=12 B=48 | CMYK C=0 M=100 Y=75 K=4; Red2: RGB R=167 G=25 B=48 | CMYK C=5 M=100 Y=71 K=22). Monochrome versions also available.
- Complementary blue palette includes Mid Blue (RGB R=0 G=38 B=100) and Light Blue (RGB R=0 G=161 B=222).

**Typography Information:**

- The official font for NSW Government logos is Gotham. Arial is preferred for digital uses.
- Gotham Medium, 26pt font is used for agency names. Gotham Light, 19pt applies in grouped agency logos.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Image guidelines allow stylized cropping but exclude rotation or reversal.
- The background should use gradients from the logo color palette where appropriate, ensuring brand consistency.

**Notes:**

- NSW NOW branding applies to projects including Transport infrastructure (e.g., WestConnex), educational initiatives, and key Government responses.
- Statutory Bodies, Courts, and Tribunals exempt from logo guidelines include culturally significant institutions like the Sydney Opera Trust.
