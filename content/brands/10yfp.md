## Brand Guidelines

### 2013

**Logo Information:**

- The 10YFP logo consists of two elements — the 10YFP symbol and the tagline. The logo must maintain its integrity and should never be recreated, altered, or used in unauthorized forms.
- Clear space rules state a minimum distance around the 10YFP logo, using double the width of the '1' in the symbol. The insertion of logos from UNEP or partners follows stricter spacing rules as illustrated.
- Minimum size: The logo's width should not be less than 25.0 mm. Logo color variations include full color, solid black, or reversed in white. Use RGB color for online reproduction and CMYK or Pantone for print.
- Logo misuse guidelines outline several 'don'ts': Don't alter the proportions, orientation, or use non-approved colors. Keep the logo's integrity intact by avoiding crowded usage, watermarking, or adding extra elements.

**Color Information:**

- Color palette provides values for 10YFP branding. Primary colors: 10YFP Blue (#007BC1), 10YFP Green (#6BBD44), and Black (#535456). Includes Pantone references, CMYK ratios, and RGB equivalents.
- Secondary colors add thematic distinctions for backgrounds or headers. Includes colors like Pantone P106-7C (#76489C), Pantone P104-7C (#2C6BB4), and more.

**Typography Information:**

- The print typeface family is Interstate. Interstate Bold is recommended for headings, and Interstate Light for body text. Online use favors Arial Bold and Helvetica Bold for PC and Mac users respectively.
- Font sizes are specified for PowerPoint applications: Headers (32 points max), Subheaders (27 points), and body text sizes ranging from 16 to 20 points.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography style recommendations and rules focus on maintaining contrast with logo placement: Images behind the logo must be sufficiently light or dark for visibility.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Spacing around the logo follows strict rules based on the symbol size. Letterheads and banners must allocate half or double the 'X' distance values.
- PowerPoint layout guidance provides sizes for headers, subheaders, and body text while retaining proportionality.

**Brand Voice:**

- The tone of voice should always be positive and engaging, avoiding jargon or overly technical language. Messaging is results-oriented and inclusive.
- Brand values include terms like authoritative, inclusive, and responsive, emphasizing clear communication.
