## Brand Guidelines

### 2020

**Logo Information:**

- Logo elements are cropped to create graphic and pattern designs. Includes tile systems, decorative corners, paths (e.g., double elbow), and other graphical elements for use in branding applications.
- Social media profile and cover image guidelines require use of the provided logo template to ensure brand consistency (e.g., original logo as profile image).

**Color Information:**

- Electric green is used minimally as an accent color and should not dominate in designs, backgrounds, merchandise, or promotions.
- At the time of publishing, secondary colors are not defined. Agencies are advised to adhere strictly to the primary color palette for brand consistency.

**Typography Information:**

- Web-safe fonts include Trebuchet MS (Bold & All Caps for headers) and Georgia (Regular for body copy). These should be used for email and web communications where web fonts are unavailable.
- Fallback options for web platforms that do not support brand fonts are Trebuchet MS (headers) and Georgia (body copy). Links should use underlines.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Follow photography composition rules such as the rule of thirds, keeping the focal point on brand subjects, using negative space to reduce visual clutter, and ensuring inclusivity in representation.
- Preferred lighting for photographs is natural lighting or single-source lighting to create clean shadows. Avoid direct light to minimize harshness.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Designs should utilize a combination of white and black backgrounds for messaging clarity. White creates optimism, while black provides a dramatic tone.

**Brand Voice:**

- Guidelines emphasize tone consistency across social media. Posts should be professional, prompt, and align with the 'Defenders of Potential' ethos.
