## Brand Guidelines

### 2019

**Logo Information:**

- The University Mark has several configurations including Vertical, Stacked, Horizontal, and Wide configurations. Each serves specific design purposes based on layout needs.
- Logo color variations include Orange/Black (primary full color), Black/1-color, Reversed (for dark backgrounds), and White application (for busy backgrounds).
- Improper logo usage for University Mark specifies not changing logo colors, cropping, stretching, separating parts, adding strokes, outlines, drop shadows, or other effects.
- Improper Bengal logo usage includes not altering logo colors, adding elements, stretching, separating parts, using reversed logos improperly, adding strokes, outlines, or effects.
- Improper Spirit Mark usage includes not changing colors, cropping, stretching, separating parts, adding strokes, outlines, or effects.
- Clear space rules for the University Mark use the width of the uppercase 'I' in 'Idaho' as the base measurement, and for the Bengal logo, the space between the top teeth is used as the guide.
- Minimum size requirements and guidelines for the Bengal logo emphasize suitability for applications on wearables and campus branding.
- Logo formats include EPS (vector for high-res print), JPEG (raster for web/digital), and PNG (raster with transparent background). Color modes supported are CMYK, RGB, and spot colors (Pantone).

**Color Information:**

- Primary colors include Roarange (PMS 715, CMYK: 0, 46, 85, 0; RGB: 246, 146, 64; Web #f69240) and Bengal Black (Process Black: CMYK 0, 0, 0, 100; RGB: 0, 0, 0; Web: #000000).
- Secondary colors include Growling Gray (Light, Medium, and Dark variations) and White, with their respective CMYK, RGB, and HEX codes.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary font used is Museo Slab, a robust slab serif typeface, suitable for headlines and body copy larger than 10 points.
- Secondary font Museo Sans is a clean sans-serif font suitable for subheads/copy smaller than 10 points.
- Display font Veneer Clean is used sparingly for bold headlines and statements such as in the ROAR campaign.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Use creativity in photography design, balancing bold and restrained compositions. Examples demonstrate branding possibilities across advertisements and motion graphics.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Clear space for logos established based on the 'I' in 'Idaho' and Bengal top teeth visual guides. Maintain design simplicity and balance for wearables, banners, and campus applications.
- Typography hierarchy shown in brochures involves size relationships, type selections, and negative space to balance design.

**Brand Voice:**

- ISU’s brand voice involves maintaining strong identity through consistent application of guidelines. ROAR campaign embodies bold, strong messaging.

**Notes:**

- Examples of past marks that are disallowed include outdated versions of spirit, Bengal, and university marks.
