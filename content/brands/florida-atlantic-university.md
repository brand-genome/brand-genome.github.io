### 2020

**Logo Information:**

- The Florida Atlantic University logo features a depiction of a wave inside the letter 'A.' The wave is a tribute to the 'Atlantic' in FAU's name, symbolizing forward motion and energy.
- In addition to the logo, different wordmarks are available. Acceptable variations include centered, left justified, horizontal, inline, and stacked versions.
- Custom logos can be created for schools, colleges, or administrative units/departments, allowing them to align within FAU's visual identity.
- Color variations for the FAU logos include two-color, FAU Blue only, and black/gray or white. Logos must not appear in unauthorized colors.
- Clear zones are defined to maintain logo integrity, specifying spacing rules such as the height of the crossbar of the 'F.'
- Spirit logos, used as athletic marks, follow strict adherence to identity guidelines. Acceptable colors include full-color, FAU Blue, black, and white.

**Color Information:**

- Primary colors are FAU Blue (Pantone 295), FAU Red (Pantone 200), FAU Silver (Pantone 877), and FAU Gray (Pantone 428). FAU Silver is preferred; FAU Blue is dominant.
- Secondary colors include Teal (Pantone 320), Ocean Blue (Pantone 7689), and others used as accents. Primary identity colors should dominate communication pieces.

**Typography Information:**

- Restricted use includes stamps and institutional seals. The visual significance includes academic excellence and the Atlantic's importance.
- Seal-approved uses include diplomas, contracts, transcripts, banners, and pre-approved gifts. Non-approved uses include signage, business cards, websites, and ads.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Promotional items bearing University trademarks must be produced by approved licensees and adhere to visual guidelines.
- Retail items for resale must follow trademark licensing restrictions, including approved color combinations.

**Brand Voice:**

- Brand promises include transformative education, diversity in experiences, and a focus on innovative research and public service.
- FAU tackles advanced global challenges and embraces continuous academic improvement through access and excellence.
- Prominent words defining FAU's voice include ambition, diversity, opportunity, coastal, and exceptional.

**Notes:**

- School and department logos adhere to design standards and must be created by the Division of Public Affairs.

### 2014

**Logo Information:**

- The FAU logo features limited variations in color. Permissible options include: Two-color (FAU Blue and FAU Red or black), FAU Blue-only version, and all-black logo. FAU Silver/Gray is allowed for special backgrounds and the logo may appear in white for use on FAU Blue or dark backgrounds. All variations must include the line in the logo.
- FAU wordmarks are presented in variations such as Two-color Wordmark, FAU Blue Wordmark, Black Wordmark, White Wordmark, and FAU Gray/Silver Wordmark. These wordmarks are designed to complement the logo.
- Wordmarks with type variations are designed for compatibility across layouts and include color schemes such as Two-color Wordmark with Type, FAU Blue Wordmark with Type, Black Wordmark with Type, White Wordmark with Type, and FAU Gray/Silver Wordmark with Type. Additional type is set in Optima.
- On colored backgrounds, the logo and wordmarks must be adjusted for contrast. All type and rules should be white on dark backgrounds. Logos should not be used on medium value backgrounds due to lack of contrast.
- The layout of the University logo can be modified for schools or departments but must adhere strictly to proportions and color rules. Custom logos are created by University Communications.
- Logo clear zones are established to maintain visual integrity. The clear zone for logos equals one-third of the height of the logo itself or the height of the large cap in type (where applicable).
- Minimum allowable sizes are specified: Logos cannot be used smaller than their defined minimum sizes (e.g., 3/4 inches for major logo variations).
- For improper usage of FAU marks, examples include color alterations, rearranging logo components, unapproved fonts, sizing changes, or using FAU logos in unsupported colors like all FAU Red.
- School spirit-related logos and alternatives, particularly for athletic use, are available for certain contexts. Variations include full-color, two-color, and monochrome options such as FAU Blue and Black.
- Secondary logos for athletics are complementary marks intended for decorative or retail contexts where the university’s name is already established. Colors include FAU Blue, FAU Red, FAU Silver/Gray, and black.
- The winged monogram is a secondary mark intended for decoration but must adhere strictly to color and design rules. Permissible colors include FAU Blue, FAU Red, FAU Silver/Gray, and black.

**Color Information:**

- FAU color schemes include FAU Blue, FAU Red, Black, White, and FAU Silver/Gray, supported by process color equivalents depending on the background. Guidelines are provided for optimal contrast and clarity.

**Typography Information:**

- FAU visual identity includes three primary fonts: Palatino, Optima, and FAU Bold. Palatino is used in the seal and logo, Optima is used for University communications and typography, and FAU Bold is custom for athletics.
- Example characters for Optima font family and FAU Bold font family listed. FAU Bold aligns with athletic branding, while Optima and Palatino are classic serif options for corporate materials.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Spacing guidelines for clear zones around logos and marks: Clear zones are equal to one-third of the logo height or the large cap height used in typographic combinations.
- Layout examples for University wordmarks with text blocks: Spacing between tagline and text block is one-half the text block's leading. Preferred size is 10 on 11 points.

**Notes:**

- Contact details for University Communications at branding@fau.edu (phone: 561.297.0096) for questions related to branding or identity violations.
- Custom logos for colleges and departments should be created through University Communications, ensuring branding adherence.
