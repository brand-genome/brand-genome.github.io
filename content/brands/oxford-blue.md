## Brand Guidelines

### 2016

**Logo Information:**

- Correct use rules for the logo include using high-resolution artwork provided online and maintaining proportions while resizing. Incorrect uses highlighted include: modifying logos, distorting proportions, changing colors, creating outline versions, using low-quality logos, adding text within the exclusion zone, or extracting the word 'Oxford' from the logo.
- Exclusion zone requirements specify clear spacing, ensuring sufficient empty area around the logo for optimal display.

**Color Information:**

- Primary color: Oxford blue associated uniquely with the University. Color breakdown: Pantone 282; CMYK: C=100, M=80, Y=0, K=60; RGB: R=0, G=33, B=71.
- Complementary colors provide wide variety for design applications. Examples include Pantone 279 (CMYK: C=69 M=35 Y=0 K=0, RGB: R=72 G=145 B=220), Pantone 291 (CMYK: C=36 M=7 Y=2 K=0, RGB: R=158 G=206 B=235), and additional colors for typographic and background use.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary typeface: Foundry Sterling, chosen for clarity and readability. Available weights: light, book, book italic, medium, demi, bold, extra bold. Complementary serif typefaces include Minion (regular, italic, bold), Perpetua, and Sabon.
- Alternative sans-serif typeface Arial is recommended for internal communications. Web-friendly typeface PT Sans is used for digital applications and complements Foundry Sterling.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography guidance includes selecting high-quality images for print and digital applications. Resolution requirements: print images at 300dpi and web images at 72dpi.
- Access discounted rates for high-quality Oxford University photographs. Free selection available online via Oxford University Images.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Design templates ensure consistent layout rules for stationery such as letterheads and business cards. Letterheads are positioned top-right with clear margins, while business card layouts adhere to fixed spacing and font choices.
- PowerPoint presentation templates define slide margins, bullet point styles, and positioning for both title and secondary information.

**Brand Voice:**

- Oxford's Style Guide maintains grammar and formatting consistency, offering structured guidance for University communications but does not dictate creative writing.
- University maintains professional design standards and consistency through centralized resources and guidance provided by its Design Studio, encouraging adherence to branding rules in all materials.

**Notes:**

- Designers are encouraged to use the online toolkit for official resources, branding guidelines, typefaces, and templates per University standards.
- Feedback for guideline improvement can be submitted to publications@admin.ox.ac.uk.
