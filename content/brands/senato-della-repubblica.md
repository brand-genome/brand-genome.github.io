## Brand Guidelines

### 2010

**Logo Information:**

- Reference to the usage of the Senato logo in a variety of publication types, detailing its placement, alignment, and sizes (e.g., titles of various font sizes, base sizes, interlines). Text emphasizes the importance of maintaining logo proportionality (e.g., base sizes like 9mm, 12mm).
- Guidelines include examples of external publications carrying the Senato logo, emphasizing horizontal and vertical placements, maintaining clearance zones, and ensuring no elements overlap.
- Texture applications of the Senato logo for decorative purposes include enlarging specific elements of the logo for editorial or communicative aesthetics, reinforcing the institutional visual identity.
- Incorrect usage guidelines for the Senato logo include prohibition of rotation, deformation, special effects, or placing content or other logos over specific parts of the logo.

**Color Information:**

- Institutionally defined colors for internal services and public-facing roles, such as Pantone 644 C (CMYK 42/15/0/6), Pantone 647 C (100/56/0/23), and others specified for the archive, international affairs, etc.
- Red color (Pantone 193 C, CMYK 0/100/65/20, or Pantone 1955 C, CMYK 0/100/60/45), named 'rosso Aula,' symbolizes the institution’s identity and should be used for external communication.

**Typography Information:**

- RotisSerif Bold with varying font sizes is used as headline text across publications, such as 15pt-35pt sizes for titles (e.g., catalogues, publications, etc.). Body text employs RotisSerif in 9pt with 8pt interline spacing.
- Variation includes use of RotisSerif Bold font at smaller sizes as body text sections, supplemented with RotisSansSerif Bold for certain high-focus areas.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Defined grid layouts for dossiers, bulletins, and catalogues, measured spacing (e.g., 12.7mm base, 200mm by 205mm layout dimensions) and rules for consistent application across public-facing materials or their sections.
- Examples of horizontal and vertical formatted templates with specific margin and measurement standards, aligning with unified design output.

**Notes:**

- Significantly detailed guidelines provided for gifts (e.g., Senate-branded agendas, which include specific Pantone values for personalized embossing and restricted use for unique scenarios).
- Comprehensive templates suggest public releases must unify headers, insignias, proportions on front-facing yet flexible expanding contexts.
