## Brand Guidelines

### 2024

**Logo Information:**

- The Fashion Revolution logo must always use a black panel border with consistent font inside. It should not have its color, font, size distorted, stretched, rotated, or have other elements added inside the logo. Minimum reproduction width: 8 mm. Safe zone: margin equal to one-quarter of the black panel width surrounding it.
- The logo is intended for universal use across all communications, preferably positioned at the top right or center of documents.
- Incorrect usage examples include changing the color or font, stretching, rotating the logo, or adding additional text or graphic elements.

**Color Information:**

- Primary colors in the Fashion Revolution palette include Light Blue (PANTONE 310, RGB: 139, 205, 227), Neutral (PANTONE 7604, RGB: 241, 228, 221), Black (PANTONE Black, RGB: 0, 0, 0), Acid Green (PANTONE 382, RGB: 210, 219, 61), Pink (PANTONE 699, RGB: 243, 205, 213), Dark Blue (PANTONE 293, RGB: 36, 54, 205), Red (PANTONE Warm Red, RGB: 237, 42, 36), and Orange (PANTONE 1495, RGB: 241, 154, 19). Recommendations: RGB for digital assets, CMYK for print materials, PANTONE for special spot treatments.
- Gradients are offered as downloadable assets for backgrounds and textures.

**Typography Information:**

- Kelson Sans is the primary font, used for headlines and body text. Secondary fonts include Space Mono (available free on Google Fonts), Franklin Gothic in Heavy Italic, and Value Serif (available via a Typekit subscription). Zombie Checklist Alpha is used for handwritten-style posters ('Who made my clothes?' and 'I made your clothes').
- Example usage: Kelson Sans Bold for headlines, Kelson Sans Regular for body text, Space Mono for secondary text, Franklin Gothic Heavy Italic and Value Serif for alternative body text. Zombie Checklist Alpha must be outlined and adjusted for natural handwriting effects in posters.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography should capture bold, inclusive, and inquisitive themes. It must express diversity, equality, and inclusion in representing people, the planet, and fashion. All photography must align with the bold and activist mood of the campaign.
- Illustrations must be bold, inquisitive, positive, and unique in perspective. Artists familiar with the subject are preferred to enrich the final designs.
- Patterns: Stitch patterns can be used over colors or gradients. These patterns work best oversized and should maintain their direction (no rotation or flipping).

**Spacing Layout:**

- The stitch pattern can be scaled into different sizes and used across colors or gradients to create bold backgrounds. Direction of the pattern should not be rotated or flipped.

**Brand Voice:**

- Tone is bold, provocative, inquisitive, positive, accessible, and inclusive. Avoid being shaming, dull, full of jargon, overly technical, or victimizing.
- Messages should be action-oriented or solution-focused, avoiding guilt while empowering change. This ensures consistency and cohesiveness when representing the brand.

**Notes:**

- The guidelines serve to ensure the consistent identity and impact of Fashion Revolution worldwide, emphasizing the need for cohesive communication. All branding assets are downloadable from the official website.
- Fashion Revolution branding (logos and fonts like Zombie Checklist Alpha) is prohibited for commercial resale in any form, including apparel, merchandise, or other goods. Violators may face legal action to prevent misuse.
