## Brand Guidelines

### 2007

**Logo Information:**

- The CAN logotype exists in variations with a two-color logotype (Pantone 186C and Pantone 5275C) and one-color logotype variations. Clear space rules specify a minimum encroachment zone surrounding the logotype, defined as 'X=X'. The logo can also include or exclude the strapline depending on design needs.
- Logotype should always maintain nearest color values for specific media. The logotype is shown in a consistent style, using Pantone colors as indicated. Specific color ways are suggested for different applications.

**Color Information:**

- Primary colors: Pantone 186C (Red), Pantone 5275C (Blue). CMYK values for red: C=0, M=100, Y=81, K=18. CMYK values for blue: C=60, M=47, Y=8, K=81. RGB values for red: R=220, G=0, B=46. RGB values for blue: R=57, G=60, B=113.
- Complementary colors suggested as harmonious tones to CAN blue (Pantone 5275C). Bright colors discouraged to maintain prominence of CAN red (Pantone 186C). These colors can be visually matched for specific media applications.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary font is Helvetica Roman, used for headlines, text, and word processing needs. Helvetica Bold is also used. Additional font styles include Aura Regular. Fonts are aimed to provide a clean and contemporary look for printed publications.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography guidelines emphasize challenging stereotypes, e.g., avoiding depictions of vulnerability. Imagery should represent energy, purpose, and engagement. Suitable subjects include success stories and CAN involvement. Include people from diverse backgrounds and abilities.
- Photographs should be stored in high resolution, digitally on CD. Rules specify to always credit designers, photographers, and retain the rights details under which photographs were sold or commissioned.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Minimum encroachment zone for the CAN logo requires 'X=X' separation between the logo and neighboring elements. When placing the logo with the strapline, additional clear space rules may apply depending on the context or medium.

**Brand Voice:**

- Tone encourages representing the energy, purpose, and engagement. Communication aims to create positive impressions internally and externally, with consistent messaging.

**Notes:**

- Strapline alignment can vary based on layout and media, and red may be used to connect the logotype to strapline elements in certain cases.
- Specific attention paid to job titles and organizational names; consistent capitalizations for professional communications.
