## Brand Guidelines

### 2019

**Logo Information:**

- Four logo versions designed for light and dark backgrounds: one set for use above 30mm diameter and one set for below 30mm diameter. Includes a 'hop flower rule' for minimum distance around the logo. The elements of the logo must not be isolated, moved, or tampered with.
- Primary black logo for light surfaces and white logo for dark, saturated or black surfaces. If the circular logo does not perform optimally, use horizontal version. Primary logos must be used for reproductions with size bigger than 30mm diameter.
- Special small format logo versions for use below 30mm diameter or for digital reproductions. These versions keep sharpness and clarity in smaller sizes.
- Secondary logos: Vertical logo for vertical surfaces, horizontal for wide surfaces, and circular red logo for special visibility cases (e.g., social media). Color rules for circular and horizontal logos are shared.
- Dos and don'ts for logo usage: Top-to-bottom reading enforced; only specified colors can be used; elements must not be separated or removed; logo must not be rotated.

**Color Information:**

- Defined three primary colors: Pantone Black C (CMYK: 0-0-0-100, RGB: 44,42,41), Vintage White Pantone 9064-50% (CMYK: 2-2-8-0, RGB: 250,248,239), Passionate Red Pantone 185 C (CMYK: 0-93-79-0, RGB: 228,0,43).
- Color usage rules: Green conveys hops, grass, lime; dark blue conveys liquorice, nuts, bread; brown conveys caramel, chocolate, coffee; red conveys berries and tomato; black conveys intense flavors like chocolate, coffee; yellow conveys pineapple, passion fruit.
- Based on sensory analysis, beer varieties are divided into four clusters with distinct color coding to match aroma and taste.

**Typography Information:**

- Jacobsen Grotesk font family includes six variants: Regular, Regular Condensed, Bold Condensed, Bold, CAPS, and Display. Fonts designed for headlines (Display, CAPS) and body copy (Regular, Bold variants). Avoid long headlines in all caps and bold passages. Specific rules for variety names and default fonts.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Approved Jacobsen core images must use a monotone black/white effect and may be cropped into tall, wide, or square formats. No color filters or other effects allowed.
- Beer and food images must be in full color, ensuring appetizing visuals. Color saturation may be used but balance must avoid unnatural filters.
- Social media images: Videos are preferred (4:5 ratio for feeds, 9:16 ratio for stories). The first 3 seconds should deliver clear branding. Jacobsen logo can be used traditionally or as a watermark. Content must be sound-independent and match the brand tone.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Hop flower element used to break up long text blocks or add visual pause, mostly for print or digital media. J.C. Jacobsen’s signature is tactile on bottles and must follow specific guidelines.

**Brand Voice:**

- Jacobsen aims to brew beers that challenge beer enthusiasts or create trust for careful drinkers. Naming conventions vary based on beer categories like Contemporary, Seasonal, and Limited, with rules centered around beer type, character, and seasonality.

**Notes:**

- Guidelines for product presentations in Offtrade and Ontrade settings, including logo rules for glasses. Special attention given to pattern design that reflects beer flavor and aroma.
