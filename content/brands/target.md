## Brand Guidelines

### 2013

**Logo Information:**

- The Bullseye Design is a global and registered icon that may appear in Target Red, black, or white only. The logo consists of two rings of equal width, with a center circle twice the width of the outer ring. The Bullseye's inner ring is transparent and shows the background color or pattern.
- The Bullseye may appear in Target Red (PMS 186, CMYK 4/100/85/6), black, or white only. Other color usage is prohibited.
- The logo should not be distorted, modified, used as part of another object (like part of a flower, wheel, or eye), or connected to other items. It must always maintain its original design. Incorrect usages include using it as a number, a letter, part of a letter, or any other unintended context.
- Minimum clear space required around the Bullseye Design is not explicitly defined in detail but emphasizes maintaining the integrity of the logo.
- The Target wordmark can stand alone or be paired with the Bullseye Design. It must appear only in Target Red (PMS 186), black, or white.
- One approved lockup exists with the Bullseye Design and Target wordmark together. No additional taglines (e.g., 'Expect More. Pay Less.') should be included in that lockup.

**Color Information:**

- Target Red is specified as PMS 186 for spot color and CMYK 4/100/85/6 for 4-color process.
- The Bullseye logo's inner ring is transparent, and this transparency applies when placed on different colors or images.

**Typography Information:**

- Helvetica Neue is the corporate typeface, preferred for all branding and corporate communication. Weights specified include: 85 Heavy, 75 Bold, 55 Roman, and 45 Light.

**Imagery Photography:**

- The Bullseye Design may be applied to various items (e.g., clothing), and it can adapt to the shape of the object (such as a pattern on a shirt or an umbrella), as long as at least one complete, undisturbed Bullseye is visible. In 3D applications, the inner ring may appear white if the outer ring and center are Target Red.

**Spacing Layout:**

- When placing the Bullseye logo, maintain sufficient space between other elements and the logo. No explicit measurements are provided, but emphasis on the visual integrity and space of the logo.

**Brand Voice:**

- The brand promise 'Expect More. Pay Less.' emphasizes the brand's identity. It must be presented as a singular phrase with equal emphasis on 'Expect More' and 'Pay Less.' The two parts cannot appear separated or in mismatched type sizes or typefaces. The tagline should always maintain visual consistency.
- 'Expect More. Pay Less.' can appear as a lockup with the Bullseye logo or as a standalone tagline, depending on the context. It should stand out distinctly in all copy blocks where it's used.
- The brand promise 'Expect More. Pay Less.' remains consistent across all languages in translation and should be used appropriately in copy or with the logo lockup in print advertising.

**Notes:**

- Legal guidelines specify that the Bullseye Design, 'Target,' and 'Expect More. Pay Less.' are registered trademarks of Target Brands, Inc. The registered trademark (®) symbol is no longer needed in legal certifications. In international contexts, slight logo variations may be required for compliance with local regulations.
- For any uncertainties or requirements regarding the Bullseye Design usage, the document instructs contacting linguistic.services@target.com or Target.Brands@target.com for clarification.
