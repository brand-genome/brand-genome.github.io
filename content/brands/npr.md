## Brand Guidelines

### 2004

**Logo Information:**

- The NPR logo must always be used in one of the approved versions (positive or reverse). It can appear in color or black and white depending on the background.
- The NPR logo should appear against white or light-colored backgrounds for positive form and black or dark-colored backgrounds for reverse form.
- Black and white versions of the logo are acceptable for single-color printing when full color is impractical.
- Incorrect logo usages include spacing out the tiles, including 'National Public Radio' within the logo, or stacking the tiles vertically unless for specific graphic applications like banners or signage.
- Do not alter, modify, add to, or change the original approved versions of the NPR logo. Examples of incorrect usage include adding promotional copy, competing slogans, or other visual elements in close proximity to the logo.
- Safe zone: The logo should have generous background space around it. Avoid encroaching on the clear space.
- Improper combinations include using the NPR logo as part of station branding, improper positioning tags, or distorting the logo.

**Color Information:**

- Use Pantone color matching systems for accurate reproduction of the NPR logo colors. Visual color matching is recommended to ensure fidelity.
- When producing the logo with fewer than two colors, use only black or white and match the darkest ink with light-colored backgrounds.

**Typography Information:**

- The primary corporate font is Bell Gothic, available in Light and Black weights for PC and Mac. Times (secondary font) is standard and part of system fonts.
- Font usage rules: Use Bell Gothic Light or Times for body text; Bell Gothic Black for headlines/subheads. Default font size is 10pt with 12pt line spacing.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Clear space rule: Logo must have adequate background space, free from any conflicting design elements or text.
- Separator lines between co-branding logos cannot exceed 0.5pt in thickness and must maintain balanced spacing according to guidelines.

**Brand Voice:**

- The NPR brand emphasizes national media quality. Member stations complement the NPR brand with their local strengths.
- Improper use of the NPR name, typeface, or logo creates confusion and diminishes its value.

**Notes:**

- NPR member stations require proper licensing agreements to use the NPR logo.
- When reproducing the logo, only use first-generation images from approved sheets, as photocopies may degrade quality.
- Digital outputs for the NPR logo must achieve high resolutions (1270 dpi). Low-resolution outputs are inadmissible for final printing.
