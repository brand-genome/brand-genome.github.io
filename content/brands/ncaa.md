### 2015

**Logo Information:**

- The NCAA Championships Logo System includes designs for regular-season logos, championship logos, and foundation graphics. The system utilizes the NCAA blue (Pantone 300C) and NCAA Legacy blue (Pantone 295C). Logos cannot be altered or rearranged. NCAA Legacy and United typography must be used.
- Logos are available for download in EPS and JPEG formats through the NCAA Digital Library (NCAAlogos.com).
- No logo elements should be adjusted, including alternate fonts or colors. Guidelines exist for logo placement, sizing, and general application.
- Specific guidelines for logo use on signage or décor are provided, including layout adjustments for horizontal and vertical applications.

**Color Information:**

- NCAA Championship's primary colors include NCAA brand blue (Pantone 300C) and NCAA Legacy blue (Pantone 295C).
- Accent color Pantone 428C is used for the NCAA Championship graphics supporting elements.
- Photography and gradients use monotone treatments with Pantone 300C. Guidelines detail gradient design for various orientations.

**Typography Information:**

- The official NCAA typography for championships is NCAA Legacy and NCAA United, exclusively designed by Joe Bosack & Co. No alternative fonts are allowed.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography styles for branding include monotone treatments using Pantone 300C. Preliminary rounds use trophy images with no player likeness, while championship rounds use images from previous championships.
- Detailed steps for manipulating photography via Photoshop include converting full-color images to grayscale, followed by monotone treatment using Pantone 300C.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Guidelines for vertical applications include layout details such as gradients extending 8% of overall width, and accent lines must occupy 2% of the width.
- Horizontal application layout guidelines involve the accent lines at 2% of the overall height and gradients extending 8% vertically.
- Traditional applications involve proportionate adjustments to accent lines and gradient layers depending on height or width.

**Brand Voice:**

- The brand emphasizes unity and consistency in representing NCAA values of protecting college athletes, quality education, fair competition, and showcasing student-athletes' excellence in academics and sports.

**Notes:**

- Trademarks must always use the ® and ™ symbols with the first and most prominent usage. Logos and marks cannot be modified or used without explicit approval.
- The NCAA Championships Logo System must always align with branding elements such as approved colors, typography, and layout rules.
