## Brand Guidelines

### 2012

**Logo Information:**

- The logo has a mandatory protection zone labeled 'E' which corresponds to the height of the typography of 'LORRAINE'. No graphic elements should appear in this zone. The space 'X' between elements is set equal to the width of the yellow 'L'. Logo must align according to specific construction lines (A, B, C, D), ensuring it does not exceed maximum height (A-D).
- Examples of logo co-signature with associated entities such as collegiums, poles, or components provided.
- Incorrect usage: logo elements cannot exceed specified sizes, spacing between elements must follow rules, logo co-signatures must align with construction lines (as described on page 58).
- Logo height is specified for various use cases, e.g., PowerPoint top left logo height is 30mm, logos at the bottom are 10mm height.

**Color Information:**

- Primary colors are yellow and light gray, with panels able to incorporate accompanying colors for signage.
- Cover designs stipulate that logos with text can use black on photo backgrounds or colored backgrounds.

**Typography Information:**

- Typography used: Trade Gothic LT, Condensed N°18. Font is styled in uppercase for co-signature elements.
- Font used in titles: Trade Gothic LT Bold Condensed N°20. It is applied across covers, posters, and signage.
- For co-signatures placed on signage or additional documents, Arial is used for accompanying text.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Logos in the image background can go in black font. White space must surround the logo.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Spacing between typographic elements always equals 'X', which corresponds to the width of the yellow segment of the logo.
- For signage, white margins surround panels with dimensions such as 10mm or 20mm white space.
- Digital presentations mandate logo placement on the top left of the first page and bottom left on subsequent pages within specific layouts.

**Brand Voice:**

- Strict rules apply to the use of the Université de Lorraine logo to ensure consistency across communication supports. The tone for all associated branding materials should carry professionalism with highly structured alignment setups.

**Notes:**

- Various templates for professional communication items (e.g., letterhead, business cards, press releases) are explicitly mentioned within digital production files, such as 'UL_CO_PAPIER_A_LETTRE.INDD'. Items like signage support specific formats like IDML.
