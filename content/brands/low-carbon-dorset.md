## Brand Guidelines

### 2018

**Logo Information:**

- The logo uses colors linked to the Dorset County Council brand. The circular motif reflects dynamism and the progressive outlook of Low Carbon Dorset.
- Key logo usage rules: 1. Always use correct colors. 2. Never rotate or distort the logo. 3. Do not recreate the logo with different typefaces. 4. Ensure sufficient contrast with the background. 5. Always respect the correct isolation area.
- Color variations for the logo include black-only and whiteout versions.
- The logo must always maintain an isolation area equivalent to the height of two 'D's in 'DORSET'.
- The motif (colored swirls within the logo) can be used separately as a graphic. It must not be distorted but can be rotated. The motif can also bleed off-page or be used in a white-only version.
- The isolation area also applies to dual branding. The Low Carbon Dorset logo must be on the outside of dual branding arrangements, either on the left or right side.
- The logo should ideally not be used smaller than 15mm in height.

**Color Information:**

- Darker Blue: CMYK 85 24 0 0, RGB 0 150 214, HEX #0096d6.
- Light Blue: CMYK 40 0 20 0, RGB 150 213 210, HEX #96d5d2.
- Grey (text): CMYK 5 0 5 50, RGB 139 146 144, HEX #8b9290.
- Main (Darker) Green: CMYK 69 0 100 0, RGB 84 185 72, HEX #54b948.
- Lighter Green: CMYK 46 0 100 0, RGB 151 201 61, HEX #97c93d.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary typeface: Avenir. All weights are available, including: Avenir Light (35), Medium (65), Heavy (85).
- Alternative typeface: Arial. Arial Regular and Arial Bold can be used when Avenir is not available.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Clear space (isolation area) is required around the logo and equals the height of two 'D's in 'DORSET'. This applies even when dual branding is in use.

**Notes:**

- The short guide helps to promote and protect the Low Carbon Dorset brand. It ensures consistent style and quality across all materials.
