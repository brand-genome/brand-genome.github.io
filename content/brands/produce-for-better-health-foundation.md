## Brand Guidelines

### 2020

**Logo Information:**

- The Have A Plant® logo should never be used at a size less than 1 inch in width with URL tagline lockup, and 0.75-inch width without the URL tagline. Clear space requirements prohibit placement of text, photos, artwork, or edge encroachment near the logo.
- Unacceptable logo usage includes recoloring, recreating the logo in presentation software, adding shapes, inverting colors, altering proportions, or using unapproved artwork files.
- The logo can include photography or motion video in the word 'plant' but must feature single-category produce images authorized and produced by PBH Marketing.

**Color Information:**

- Core brand colors are PBH Green (CMYK: c66/m6/y100/k0, RGB: 100/178/70, PMS: 369) and PBH Purple (CMYK: c50/m94/y6/k0, RGB: 157/53/141, PMS: 513). Accent colors span the full color spectrum but restraint is advised in their use.
- Logo color guidelines include preference for full color versions on light or white backgrounds, black versions for no-color contexts, and reversed (white) versions for uniform image texture backgrounds. CMYK and RGB formulas must be used consistently to ensure color reproduction.

**Typography Information:**

- GT Walsheim is the primary font family for the brand. GT Walsheim Bold is used for headlines/subheads, and Regular weight is recommended for body text. DIN Pro Black is used for the tagline. Arial is recommended for presentations and electronic documents.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography should reflect brand attributes such as boldness, passion, joy, optimism, straight-talking, and freshness. It should avoid industry-typical stock images and focus on being bright, clever, relatable, and celebratory of healthy living.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Logo clear space rules require adequate margins. Text, photos, or borders cannot encroach on the designated clear space.

**Brand Voice:**

- The tone of the brand is joyful, optimistic, bold, passionate, and straight-talking. Messaging incorporates modern, fresh language while avoiding dated or complex communication styles.
- The written brand guidelines specify that 'Have A Plant®' should always be referred to by its full name. Acronyms like 'HAP' are discouraged. URL capitalization prefers lowercase, and hashtags such as #haveaplant should use only lowercase.

**Notes:**

- PBH's logo use guidelines ensure inclusive messaging—no promotion of one commodity form (e.g., fresh, canned, frozen) over another. Logo applications across digital and print materials require review for messaging accuracy and adherence to branding/nutritional standards.
- Products displaying the logo must meet nutrient criteria based on FDA standards (e.g., maximum levels for saturated fat, added sugars, sodium, and cholesterol). Fortification must comply with FDA policies.
