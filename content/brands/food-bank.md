## Brand Guidelines

### 2014

**Logo Information:**

- Our logo consists of an organic, hand-drawn word mark positioned in the center of a graphic device that symbolizes the process of reinvention. The visual integrity and clarity of the logo must be upheld at all times. Distorting, scaling, changing colors of the logo in part or in full are not allowed. Special imaging effects such as embossing, blurring, and shadows are not to be used.
- To protect the integrity of our logo, a minimum area around our logo should be kept clear of text or graphic devices. The minimum clear space is defined by the width of the 'O'. To maintain the legibility of our logo, a minimum size of 15mm has been determined. There are also mono and reversed versions of the logo which can be used when there are color limitations.

**Color Information:**

- Purple has been chosen as the Primary Brand color for an uplifting, positive, and contemporary feeling. Secondary and neutral color palettes have been selected to work in harmony with the primary brand color.
- Pantone 312 C (HEX: #00ADD0, CMYK: C94 M0 Y11 K0) and Pantone 151 C (HEX: #DF7400, CMYK: C0 M55 Y100 K0) serve as secondary brand colors.
- Neutral colors include Black (Pantone Lƒ, HEX: #000000, CMYK: C0 M0 Y0 K100), Pantone 416 C (HEX: #83847A, CMYK: C22 M14 Y24 K45), and Pantone 420 C (HEX: #CECFCB, CMYK: C6 M4 Y7 K11).

**Typography Information:**

- Abraham is used for top-level titles, large statements, and pull-out quotes. Bliss is a clean, approachable typeface complementing Abraham with multiple weights to create heading hierarchies throughout communications. Trebuchet MS is readily available and used internally for documents.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Images should focus on people and communities that Foodbank works with, reflecting a warm, vibrant, and optimistic feel. They should portray people with respect and dignity, whether subjects directly face the camera or are candid in their setting.

**Brand Voice:**

- Tone of voice should be consistent, creating exceptional experiences at every touch-point, whether through the website, phone calls, or volunteering interactions. The brand should communicate with fun in mind. Utilizing food puns and imagery as creative entry points helps achieve this goal.

**Notes:**

- The guidelines emphasize consistent artwork usage and approval by the Marketing Manager before production. Contact Anubha Rawat (Marketing Manager, Foodbank Australia) for queries on guidelines.
- Partner collaboration should align with Foodbank’s values. A database of partner assets is being developed to assist strategy.
- Images of warehouses should showcase Foodbank as the largest food relief organization in Australia.
