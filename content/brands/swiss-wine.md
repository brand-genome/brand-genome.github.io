## Brand Guidelines

### 2014

**Logo Information:**

- The logo is composed of a red Pantone 1805 square with a white layer underneath, and typography aligned to both sides.
- Logo safe space is defined to prevent encroachment and ensure it always appears on a plain background.
- Two logo sizes are noted: Logo version 1 should be at least 15mm (56.5px) and Logo version 2 should be 8mm (30.5px).
- The logo must be used with its primary colours as much as possible, preferably on a light background. Below are the possible colour usages of the logo: coloured logo on light background, whiteout logo on dark background, and black and white logo.
- The logo must never be altered. Misuse examples include: do not invert colours, do not rotate symbol, do not use type above symbol, do not use coloured logo without white background, do not dissociate type from symbol colour unless stated otherwise (e.g., co-branding), do not change colours, do not stretch, do not increase elements independently.

**Color Information:**

- Primary color palette includes Pantone 1805 (C0 M98 Y75 K22), White (C0 M0 Y0 K0). Secondary palette includes Pantone 411 (C0 M30 Y30 K80) and Pantone Warm Grey 1 (C0 M2 Y7 K8).

**Typography Information:**

- Primary typeface is Feijoa Medium (with variations including italic). A secondary typeface complements the applications. Font sizes typically noted: Feijoa Medium 10.5pt, Brauer Neue Bold 8.5pt.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Patterns add texture and interest to brand communications. Designed primarily for physical applications such as bags or fabrics, not online communications. Patterns follow strict ratios of Swiss Wine logotype stripes.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Spacing guide for logo safe space ensures integrity. Co-branding lock-ups show consistent alignment and spacing: e.g., X, 2.5X, 7X ratios.
- Spacing guide for co-branding and canton alignment layouts detailed with X, 2.5X, and 7X spacings.

**Brand Voice:**

- Legal copy is a compulsory asset and must always adhere to predefined layouts and rules.

**Notes:**

- Detailed rules for co-branding and packaging styles, including cork foil designs, adhesive band usage (not diagonally or altered), tissue paper branding (covering the entire surface), paper bag variations with patterns or logo emphasis, and engraved barrels for trade fairs.
