## Brand Guidelines

### 2003

**Brand Voice:**

- The Royal Parks tone of voice combines several attributes to align communication delivery with its personality and values. The approach is described as personal and informal, informed, expert and confident while grounded in fact and reality, philosophically passionate, enthusiastic, respectful, and with strong Anglo-Saxon vocabulary preference avoiding Latin roots except for specific cases like plant names or scientific terms. The Parks voice is analogous to a warm, encouraging coach with traits such as restraint, foresight, patience, pride in British heritage, and a consistent focus on sustainability. A sense of freedom, diversity, and occasional humor is important, avoiding redundant or pompous language while fostering hospitality and respect.
- Key verbal messages defined include the primary message: 'London’s personal space', acting as a statement of positioning. Secondary messages include emphasizing diverse park offerings, history, organizational expertise, and commitment to continuous improvement.
- The strapline 'London’s personal space' is introduced as an extension of the logo to reinforce brand positioning during mid-term communications. Usage includes posters, reports, event catalogues, and other external material. It serves as a concise verbal carrier tied to strategic priorities.
- Boilerplate introductions emphasize the uniqueness of The Royal Parks with specific metrics: 5,000 acres, 220 monuments, 134 miles of walkways, and 100,000 roses among other attributes. Repeated reference to 'London's personal space' serves as a conceptual anchor.
- Verbal language guidelines emphasize friendly, concise, active voice writing, meaningful specific examples, avoidance of passive constructions, redundant phrasing, and formal institutional vocabulary.

**Notes:**

- The document heavily emphasizes principles underlining The Royal Parks’ identity, like respect, diversity, sustainability, beauty, and hospitality. These values influence all branding and communication efforts.
- Values are foundational and tie directly to corporate objectives. Five core values (respect, sustainability, diversity, beauty, hospitality) are central to how the parks function and communicate with guests and employees globally.
- Examples provided contrast effective vs ineffective verbal messaging strategies, offering concrete Do's and Don'ts. Detailed attention goes into maintaining the park’s welcoming tone while ensuring accessible, enjoyable communication.
