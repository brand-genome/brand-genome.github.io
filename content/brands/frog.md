## Brand Guidelines

### 2017

**Logo Information:**

- Main brand logo captures the spirit of Frog: simple, bold, fun, and impactful. Used to create a positive emotional connection to the brand.
- Corporate logo includes the Frog head with the name tag. This version is intended for corporate communications (e.g., brochures, event stands, websites for new audiences).
- Campaign logo features the Frog head without the name tag. This version is suitable when the audience recognizes the Frog brand, particularly for informal or playful contexts.
- Do's for logo usage: keep logo centered, equal spacing on either side, use blue Frog logo on white background, or white Frog logo on any approved background color, minimum size requirements of Frog head is 15mm.
- Don'ts for logo usage: do not alter logo outline, silhouette, or proportions of head/name, do not place logo on unapproved background colors.
- Logo clear space rule: logo should remain centered with equal space on either side.
- File format references include EPS, PNG outlined implicitly for designers; not directly stated but contextual visual examples provide guidance.

**Color Information:**

- Primary brand color is Frog Blue (Pantone 300 C, CMYK: C:100 M:44 Y:0 K:0, RGB: R:0 G:121 B:194, HEX: #0071b9). Always used as the lead color for corporate communications on a solid Frog Blue background.
- Accent colors include Pale Blue (Pantone 801 C), Deep Blue (Pantone 2768 C), Purple (Pantone 2597 C), and others. HEX and CMYK values are provided for all colors.
- Color usage rules: avoid pastel colors to maintain impact; use color opacity adjustments and blending for design variations.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary typeface is Raleway. Suggested uses: Feature text, headers, body text across print, digital, and campaigns.
- Fallback typeface for emails is Verdana for consistency in cases where custom fonts fail.
- Raleway font weights available include Extra Bold, SemiBold, Medium, Regular, Light.
- Campaign special fonts: Marketing and design teams are allowed to use custom fonts (preferably Google Fonts) for campaigns.
- Handwritten font (e.g., Caveat Bold) is used for creating quotes.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Preferred imagery includes children and teachers in school settings interacting with technology, conveying authentic, diverse, and relatable stories.
- Photos must have permissions from schools or parents before usage in official materials.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Illustration style relies heavily on visible lines with solid, dotted, or dashed treatment. Illustrations should retain a line-based aesthetic.
- Icons and glyphs use line-based styles to indicate product features, points, or solutions. Each solution is assigned a specific color tone.

**Brand Voice:**

- Tone of voice descriptor: Simple, clear, authoritative, and jargon-free, offering an understandable technological message for educational audiences.
- Friendly tone: Informal, conversational, and warm tone to establish trust with schools.

**Notes:**

- Corporate communication emphasizes a white Frog logo on a solid Frog Blue background for maximum impact and brand consistency.
- The design team retains flexibility for introducing decorative elements in specific campaign scenarios, following brand approvals.
