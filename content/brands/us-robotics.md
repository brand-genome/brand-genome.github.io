## Brand Guidelines

### 2005

**Logo Information:**

- Minimum size of the logo must be at least 1 inch (25mm) in width.
- Clear space around the logo is proportional to the x-height of the logotype's letter 'o'. This space increases or decreases proportionally to the logo size.
- Do not alter, recolor, re-proportion, or change the logotype in any way. Use only the approved logos displayed in the guidelines.

**Color Information:**

- Primary corporate color: USR Red (Pantone 485). White is also a primary color, providing balance and clarity.
- Secondary colors: USR Gray (Pantone Cool Gray 8) and USR Orange (Pantone 1505). Tertiary colors are complementary and used sparingly as accents.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary font: Meta Plus family, set at 110% width, used for major headings and prominent text elements.
- Complementary serif font: Adobe Garamond family, suitable for large portions of text for a classic, legible presentation.
- Text style: Flush left, ragged right alignment. Leading (line spacing) should be 1.2-1.5x the font size. No paragraph indentation; single- or half-line spacing between paragraphs.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Brand photography primarily uses full-color, custom images. Stock images, when used, should show authenticity with real people in real situations and avoid overly posed or artificial imagery.
- Product photography uses plain white backgrounds with minimal shadowing. Clipping paths are recommended to enable images to be placed on colored backgrounds.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Red bar is a key visual element. It runs the full width of the page and can include text or product images.
- A structured and tight grid system is preferred in layouts to ensure visual consistency and proper alignment of design elements.

**Brand Voice:**

- Brand communication should be simple, direct, and clear, emphasizing a consistent visual and verbal identity. Prominent themes include 'USRobotics means...', 'US eR Friendly', and 'Business unUSual'.
- Do not use selectively tinted or ghosted images as watermarks or backgrounds. Maintain white space for clarity and professionalism.

**Notes:**

- Stationery system, including letterheads, envelopes, and business cards, must utilize brand templates to maintain visual identity consistency.
- Microsoft PowerPoint templates are available with the brand components, including the color palette, typography, and photography, for all corporate presentations.
- Data sheets should be consistent with the brand identity, utilizing red bars, icons, and other visual elements cohesively.
