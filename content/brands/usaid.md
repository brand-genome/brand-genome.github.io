## Brand Guidelines

### 2020

**Logo Information:**

- The USAID logo must maintain clear space and minimum print size. Clear space guidelines indicate maintaining proportionality.
- Logo usage in co-branded materials requires equal prominence and may include host government or ministry logos.
- New logos for USAID campaigns and initiatives are prohibited unless approved explicitly by the Senior Advisor for Brand Management.

**Color Information:**

- USAID mentions using consistent colors across branded materials, ensuring alignment with brand identity. Detailed color codes not included in visible text.

**Typography Information:**

- Presidential initiatives and USAID campaigns require use of the Gill Sans font family for text in all its variants.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photographs are integral to communicating the impact and scope of USAID's work, with emphasis placed on using high-quality images that convey a positive and impactful message.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Maintaining clear space around the USAID logo ensures readability and brand visibility.

**Brand Voice:**

- USAID's tone of voice emphasizes amplifying and protecting the brand as a symbol of American goodwill. Messaging should be clear, consistent, and impactful.

**Notes:**

- USAID branding applies across all communications channels and must follow the standards provided in this manual, as mandated by Congress.
- The branding guidelines focus on partnerships with other governments and organizations to amplify USAID's efforts and to reach more people effectively.
