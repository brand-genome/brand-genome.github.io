## Brand Guidelines

### 2014

**Logo Information:**

- The UK aid logo is comprised of five elements: 1) the Union Flag, 2) 'UK' text, 3) 'aid' text, 4) 'from the British people' strapline, and 5) its specified colors (Royal Blue, Red, White as per Pantone/CMYK/RGB/HEX).
- Version details: Full-color UK aid logo, Black logo format (when color not possible), and White reversed logo format (used with dark backgrounds). Logo formats: Spot color (Pantone), CMYK, White reversal, Black-and-white documents.
- Logo must maintain its exclusion zone (equal to width of the 'U') and minimum size must be 17mm width of the flag.
- Logo file formats: JPEG (for general usage like Microsoft Office and web) and AI (vector files for professional print and design). Designers advised to use AI format for scalability.
- Do's and Don'ts: Do not rotate, resize, stretch, change colors, change fonts, rearrange elements, remove/add straplines, or alter text in any way. Full-color logo only displayed on white background. Sign painters must ensure fidelity to the logo.
- Examples of incorrect usage include resizing, altering text or font, removing strapline, changing colors, stretching out elements, or rotating the logo.
- Use cases of logo: Acknowledging UK as the donor in shelters for gender violence victims, humanitarian response works, distributed aid materials such as sacks of maize, banners, media events, reports, websites, video content, speeches, uniforms, and distribution points.

**Color Information:**

- Royal Blue: Pantone 280 C, CMYK (C:100 M:72 Y:0 K:19), RGB (R:0 G:36 B:125), HEX (#003399).
- Red: Pantone 186 C, CMYK (C:0 M:91 Y:76 K:6), RGB (R:207 G:20 B:43), HEX (#CC0000).
- White: Pantone Safe, CMYK (C:0 M:0 Y:0 K:0), RGB (R:255 G:255 B:255), HEX (#FFFFFF).
- Usage rules for colors: Full-color logo is preferred and must be displayed against a white background. Black and White formats only for monochrome scenarios or solid dark backgrounds.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photos should illustrate how DFID's partners use the UK aid logo on programme assets like shelters (e.g., Uganda gender violence shelter by ActionAid), humanitarian response works (e.g., Typhoon Haiyan response led by International Organisation for Migration), and distributed items (e.g., maize sacks from World Food Programme, Malawi).

**Spacing Layout:**

- Logo exclusion zone must equal the width of the 'U' in 'UK aid'. Minimum width of the logo is 17mm as measured by the width of the flag.

**Brand Voice:**

- Brand messaging must reinforce UK aid as funding by British taxpayers and promote transparency and credit to the UK government. Statements like, 'This project was funded with UK aid from the UK government' should be included in communications.
- Exceptions to branding may apply for aspects that risk safety, dignity, operational delays, or legal misrepresentation. Branding is not necessary for small personal goods, sub-contractors, and other assets not directly supporting program outputs.

**Notes:**

- UK aid must not fund promotional items (e.g., pens, mugs, bags, hats) unless directly delivering program outcomes (e.g., uniforms necessary for program delivery).
- DFID programme managers and funding recipients are responsible for ensuring compliance with branding guidelines, including submitting a visibility statement regarding all assets and supplies carrying/not carrying the UK aid logo.
- UK aid branding files are available for download and usage through official DFID channels. Recipients must provide evidence (e.g., photographs, materials) of branding in use.
- Annex C provides visual examples of how to use the UK aid logo on distributed goods, shelters, program banners, and more.
