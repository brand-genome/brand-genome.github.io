### 2019

**Logo Information:**

- Embroidery logo specifications: Full-color (preferred), full-color reversed (optional), tone-on-tone or white logo treatment is available but strongly preferred use of the full-color logo is advised to build recognition. Madeira thread specifications are matched to Pantone solid-coated swatches. Pantone codes specified: PMS 485C, PMS Cool Gray 10 C, PMS 2299 C, PMS 306 C, PMS 7461 C.
- Clear space for embroidery logos: Minimum clear space equals 65% of the height of the symbol on all sides. The clear space within the symbol should always appear as negative space rather than filled with white.
- Minimum size specifications for embroidery logos: Horizontal lockup, vertical lockup, symbol only height defined as 0.4 inches or 10mm, except for symbol only, which requires a minimum height of 0.55 inches or 14mm.

**Color Information:**

- Preferred use of primary colors on promotional items to build equity and brand recognition. Red, designated as the legacy color, is emphasized for bold impact and differentiation.
- Illustrated guidance on using bright and neutral colors in presentations—neutral grays for secondary background information and vibrant colors for key content understanding.

**Typography Information:**

- For letterhead correspondence, body copy uses Arial Regular 10-point font, upper-and-lower case, with a leading value of 14 points. Document margins conform to 1.125" left and 0.75" right, ensuring clarity and professionalism.
- Stationery guidelines specify the respect for clean layouts, incorporating the horizontal 1-line logo alongside the brand's color palette and typefaces. Personal signatures are allowed within certain parameters.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Examples of inappropriate imagery in brand visuals include compositions that are blurry, excessively staged, visually dark, appear flat, or depict clichéd concepts. Optimal imagery conveys forward-looking optimism.
- Guidelines for integrating large, high-quality imagery include their use as dynamic backdrops to emphasize content pacing and energize visual impact. Imagery is also used for supportive high-visibility presentations.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Embroidery-specific layouts require adherence to negative space guidelines that equal 65% of the symbol height. Clear space enhances logo visibility and maintains design standards.
- Stationery templates adhere to predefined margin conventions, preserving hierarchical structuring, logo prominence, and uniformity across the document.

**Brand Voice:**

- The brand tone is described as human, optimistic, dimensional, confident, and engaging. Incorporates vibrant supportive visuals and imagery alongside a warmhearted tone of voice.
- Incorporation of supergraphics with tone-on-tone patterns to emphasize key messages, these visual elements enhance the brand presence on platforms like fleets, promotional items, and digital presentations.

**Notes:**
