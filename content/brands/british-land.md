## Brand Guidelines

### 2016

**Logo Information:**

- The British Land logo allows the icon (head) to be used without the wordmark for social media applications.
- The British Land logo should prominently adhere to its clear-space guidelines (see p16) when used in promotional materials.
- In joint ventures, the British Land logo appears first and is followed by a dividing rule whose clear space is defined by half the height of the Britannia logo.

**Color Information:**

- Primary colors include blue tones consistently used for digital applications, such as call-to-action areas. Red serves as an accent color and must not be overused, especially in large panels.
- Social media channels should use the British Land palette where specified, aligning color tone standards detailed on page 21.

**Typography Information:**

- Recommended fonts are DIN Pro and ITC Caslon 224 for digital use; alternatively, Georgia and Arial default fonts can be used. Minimum font size for digital use is 10px.
- Accessible digital typography considers color contrast (checked via tools like Colour Contrast Analyzer) with prioritized legibility standards.
- Typography specifications include example styles: Heading Two (Din Pro Medium, 24px), Body Copy (Arial, Regular, 16/24px #646363).
- Print advertisements feature styles such as supporting text (Din Pro Regular), main head text (ITC Caslon Black Italic), body text (DIN Pro Regular, and call-to-action in Din Pro Bold).

**Imagery Photography:**

- Video style emphasizes natural human interactions and the character of settings (e.g., shallow depth-of-field background blur is favored).
- Photography in social media and other promotions should adhere to specific recommendations (see page 29) ensuring personality alignment.
- Cutaways in video should evoke humor, quirky details, and architectural or artistic elements where suitable.
- Superimposition of text panels on video should prioritize legibility, avoiding large colored blocks unless necessary.

**Spacing Layout:**

- For co-branded visuals, logo positioning, alignment, and proportional scaling (height, width) must adhere to defined rules (e.g., ruled by Britannia height proportions).
- Promotional material like images and logos strictly follow precise guidelines for space (clear space rules specified).

**Brand Voice:**

- The personality traits are Approachable, Stylish, Smart, and Contemporary British—guiding all communications and projects consistently in tone and creative execution.

**Notes:**

- Approval processes require adherence to review cycles detailed (brief creation through corporate sign-off). For detailed inquiries, contacts at page 73 give specific guidance.
- All collateral should be approved according to predetermined workflow, also ensuring creative fulfillment mirrors brand principles.
