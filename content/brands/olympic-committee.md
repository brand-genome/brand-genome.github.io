## Brand Guidelines

### 2013

**Logo Information:**

- The Olympic symbol, seen worldwide, represents the Olympic values of striving for excellence, demonstrating respect, and celebrating friendship. It must not be distorted, altered, or compromised.
- The use of the Olympic symbol is both a privilege and a responsibility. It requires strict adherence to the designated guidelines for each use.
- Full-color artwork information: The preferred Olympic rings version uses the colors of blue, yellow, black, green, and red on a white background.
- Monochrome versions of the Olympic rings are available in colors such as blue, green, yellow, red, black, and white. PANTONE color identification and proper usage guidelines for black and white backgrounds are provided.
- Always use the artwork provided by the IOC and ensure the Olympic rings' integrity.

**Color Information:**

- PANTONE color reproduction for the Olympic rings is specified for both full-color and monochrome versions. Full color uses PANTONE designated colors: blue, yellow, black, green, and red.
- Monochrome Pantone color references include specific shades for blue, green, yellow, red, black, and white.

**Typography Information:**

- Typography not explicitly described in this document.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Various graphic imagery and photography incorporated into partner activations must reflect the integration of sport with cultural and educational messaging, maintaining the integrity of the Olympic properties.

**Spacing Layout:**

- The layout and spacing rules primarily apply to surrounding elements associated with Olympic properties like text and typography accompanying the logos.

**Brand Voice:**

- The Olympic brand voice promotes excellence, respect, and friendship with a focus on unity displayed graphically and verbally in all activational content.

**Notes:**

- The guidelines outline the historical and cultural significance of the Olympic symbol. They were established to ensure proper respect and adherence to use.
- Partner activations and designs using Olympic properties should align with the overarching Olympic principles and mission of encouraging social responsibility.
