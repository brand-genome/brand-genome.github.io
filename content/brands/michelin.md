**Logo Information:**

- The MICHELIN full-colour logo should be used. The horizontal MICHELIN logo is the preference, but as an exception, the stacked version can also be used. On black and white adverts, the monochrome logo version may be used. The protection zone and background rules must be observed. Minimum reproduction size: 30 mm wide (print), 85 pixels wide (screens). The MICHELIN logo must not be tampered with or altered in any way. The Michelin Man and prior campaigns are prohibited. Logo integration must be validated by Michelin licensee and team.
- The MICHELIN logo can live on different backgrounds: light, yellow, or dark. On dark backgrounds, the MICHELIN wordmark is white. On yellow backgrounds, the support line is blue. Guidelines specify colour contrasts for logo placement and variants for different backgrounds.
- When using the MICHELIN logo at exhibitions/fairs, its placement is limited next to its products, with the distributor’s branding style prevailing over MICHELIN branding.
- The MICHELIN logo can be integrated digitally but must always remain secondary to the distributor/retailer’s visual identity (color, typeface, etc.) and branding. It must be smaller than the primary branding logos.
- Strict key principles dictate maintaining MICHELIN visual integrity. This restricts competing products’ promotions, tampered logos, and the use of low-quality layouts.
- Use of the MICHELIN trademark/logo is strictly regulated to protect legal rights. All use must align with approvals by Michelin.

**Color Information:**

- Variations of the MICHELIN logo involve colour adaptations, like full-colour (blue), monochromatic logos for B&W media, and adaptations for light and dark backgrounds. Wordmark/logo visibility on contrasting layouts (background-specific) is crucial per guidelines.
