## Brand Guidelines

### 2013

**Logo Information:**

- The NANTON logo comprises three elements: the wordmark 'NANTON,' the established date, and an iconic image framed in an ellipse. This design depicts the rich history and uniqueness of the town.
- Clear space guidelines: A minimum clear space equivalent to the height of 'e' within the wordmark must be maintained on all sides to ensure visibility and integrity.
- Minimum size: The logo must not be reproduced smaller than 1 inch wide for the wordmark to maintain legibility.
- Logo color variations: The logo can be used in three variations—full color (spot/Pantone and 4-color process), solid black, or full-reverse white—for various contexts and backgrounds.
- Logo misuse guidelines: Examples of misuse include altering logo colors, rearranging elements, using grayscale, stretching or squeezing, tilting, removing elements, or adding unapproved text.
- Usage guidelines include: logos must remain upright, elements must not be deleted or recreated, and scaling should maintain proportions. Backgrounds must contrast appropriately with the logo.

**Color Information:**

- Primary color—Pantone 1815: CMYK [0, 73, 75, 51], RGB [124, 33, 30], HEX: #7C211E.
- Accent colors include: Pantone 464 (Brown), Pantone 467 (Beige), with corresponding HEX, CMYK, and RGB values defined for design consistency.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary typefaces include Veneer (hand-crafted with vintage appeal), Museo Slab (formal), and Avenir LT Std. These are used for headings and general text.
- Fallback typeface: Arial is recommended for use in digital tools like Microsoft Office.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Imagery should embrace 'Authentic Alberta,' reflecting the region's western spirit, personality, and unique textures associated with the Nanton brand.

**Brand Voice:**

- The brand tone prioritizes heritage, reliability, and western authenticity, aligning with the tagline 'Authentic Alberta.'

**Notes:**

- All uses of the logo and associated visual elements must be approved by Erin Dancey (Marketing & Communications Coordinator), and contact details for questions are provided.
