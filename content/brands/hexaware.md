## Brand Guidelines

### 2024

**Logo Information:**

- The Hexaware logo was modernized to a clean, streamlined look, removing the previous blue, red, and yellow 'H' monogram. The updated design uses the Hexaware blue color for clarity and consistency.
- Modernized logo examples demonstrate suitable applications for light and dark backgrounds. Hexaware's logo colors are limited to blue, white, or black to ensure brand consistency.
- Incorrect usage examples include: rotating the logo, adding gradients/drop shadows, stretching it, using unapproved colors, or placing it on a non-contrasting background.
- Sub-brand lock-ups include module taglines, displayed in both horizontal and vertical layouts. Tagline font: Manrope, 12 pt, regular weight, color #535983.
- When displaying multiple sub-brands on the same page, the Hexaware logo should not appear to avoid redundancy.

**Color Information:**

- The Hexaware color palette includes a vibrant, impactful, digital Hexaware blue to convey clarity and modernity.
- The primary colors are Hexaware blue, white, and black. Other colors in the palette are supplementary but not used for logos to maintain consistency.

**Typography Information:**

- Tagline font for sub-brand lock-ups is Manrope, size 12 pt, in regular weight. The color for the font is #535983.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Imagery guidelines emphasize simplicity and global sophistication. Photography should align with Hexaware's updated digital-first brand narrative.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Spacing and alignment guidelines are designed to maintain clarity and orderliness across all applications of the brand. Exact grid specifications were not explicitly detailed in the reviewed content.

**Brand Voice:**

- The Hexaware brand voice emphasizes simplicity, elegance, and a global-sophisticated narrative. It aligns with their 'less-is-more' approach to branding.
- Messaging focuses on clarity and avoiding excessive noise or confusion. The goal is to offer a streamlined message with coherent storytelling.
