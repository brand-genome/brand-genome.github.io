## Brand Guidelines

### 2018

**Logo Information:**

- The 2N corporate logo comprises two elements: the logo symbol and logotype. The tagline 'An Axis company' is added under the logotype using the Rotis Sans Serif Pro Bold font.
- Logo color versions include: General blue logo, grey version, white version, black version. The black version is used in product packaging.
- Clear space around the logo is defined as the length of the lower stroke of the character 2. No graphic elements can invade this space.
- Minimum logo sizes: Full Logo - 14mm x 11mm; Logo Symbol - 5mm x 2.9mm.
- Do not alter the logo by repositioning, rotating, changing proportions, or styling. Incorrect applications include breaking into multiple lines and changing type style or proportions.

**Color Information:**

- The primary corporate color is Blue with codes: CMYK (C100 M54 Y0 K18), Pantone 294C, RGB (000, 085, 151), HEX (#005597). Gradients are defined from 100% to 20%.
- Secondary color system includes colors assigned to product groups such as: Access Control (CMYK C80 M0 Y80 K0, HEX #00a75d), Audio Systems (CMYK C100 M10 Y0 K0, HEX #0095db), Lift Systems (CMYK C0 M20 Y80 K15, HEX #e1b83d), and others. Used to accent and support the primary palette.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary font is Gotham SSM with weights Bold and Book, used for corporate materials like the website and marketing.
- Verdana is defined as the primary font for presentations.
- Roboto is selected for Android applications, while San Francisco is selected for iOS applications. Both fonts bring energy and enthusiasm to corporate communication.

**Brand Voice:**

- Consistency and controlled messaging are emphasized as core to maintaining a unified image. Unauthorized or incorrect use of brand elements is prohibited.

**Notes:**

- The guidelines emphasize commitment to quality and consistency while maintaining brand control. Partners are restricted from using 2N domain names or websites.
- The company summary highlights 2N's status as a leader in access control systems and its integration into the Axis group in 2016.
