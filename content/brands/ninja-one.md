## Brand Guidelines

### 2024

**Logo Information:**

- The NinjaOne logotype is based on the Montserrat font, with 'i', 'j', 'a', and 'e' stylized to create a ninja-like aesthetic. Recommended to include the 'circle R' symbol when the logo stands alone, such as on brochures, trade show booths, and website headers. Not necessary on swag items.
- Clear space is determined by the height of the lowercase 'n' in the logo. Minimum size is set at 90px or 1.25 inches wide.
- The logotype should always be used in a solid color. Blue (#053856), black, and reversed white or vivid green (#04FF88) are acceptable. Contrast ratio must comply with WCAG Accessibility Standards (AA).
- Do not change the font, colors, outline the logo, or add style effects like drop shadows or glow. Always maintain proportions and use only approved colors.
- Co-branding guidelines: Pair the NinjaOne logo with partner logos by placing a thin vertical line (40% black stroke) between them. Logos should be visually equal in scale. Prefer using a one-color lockup.

**Color Information:**

- Primary palette features a deep blue (#004668, Pantone 302) and vivid green (#17E685, Pantone 7479). Additional colors: #04FF88 (Pantone 802), #007DA5 (Pantone 2185).
- Secondary colors include gray hues: #F4F8F8 (Pantone 656), #E9EDED (Pantone 7541), and #DFE3E3 (Pantone Cool Gray 3). Accent colors include #F39500 (Pantone 715), #FF6027 (Pantone 165), and #053856 (Pantone 7463).
- Gradients: #04FF88 0%, #55EBFF 100% and #F2F9FA 0%, #F2FAF6 100%.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary font: Geologica, described as a humanist yet geometric, assertive typeface with a wide stance, large apertures, and a clean color. Use Montserrat interchangeably if Geologica is unavailable. For code snippets, use Fira Code.
- Font styles mentioned: Geologica and Montserrat demonstrated as uppercase, lowercase, numerals, and symbols. Fira Code specified for technical or code-related use cases.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Prefers custom photos of employees and customers over stock photography. Acceptable stock photos must show diverse people in casual attire, working in real-world settings, not looking at the camera. Avoid suits and ties.
- Images of NinjaOne employees and customers may look directly at the camera to create personal connections. Ensure diversity in representation across photos.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Background incorporates subtle vector patterns and textures representing automation and abstract endpoints.

**Notes:**

- The NinjaOne favicon ('N' icon) is used as a simple, identifiable element in web browser tabs, bookmarks, and address bars, increasing online visibility and user recognition.
