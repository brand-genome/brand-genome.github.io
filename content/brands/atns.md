## Brand Guidelines

### 2015

**Logo Information:**

- The ATNS logo is the central element of brand identity. It must have clear space using the x-height of the logotype around all sides to protect its integrity.
- Logo minimum size for printing techniques are provided for various methods: For instance, silkscreening requires 39mm for logotype with descriptor, 37mm without; pad printing requires 2mm for logotype with descriptor, 27mm without.
- Logo color options: Full color (blue and grey), one color grey, one color blue, black, or reversed.
- Logo must not be placed on busy or overpowering backgrounds; colors cannot be altered, and it must not be distorted, skewed, rotated, or used in a keyline.
- The strapline must always be displayed in Dax Medium font. It may be placed below, above, or to the left of the logo, with all strapline-positioned instances following the clear space rule.
- Divisional sub-brands can be displayed in horizontal or stacked versions distinguished by a keyline aligned with the x-height or height of the logo.
- Product names must be positioned following 3x the x-height below the logo or below divisional sub-brands.

**Color Information:**

- Primary external color palette includes Pantone 2945 C (C:100 M:50 Y:0 K:0, R:0), Pantone 294 C (C:100 M:72 Y:0 K:26, R:0), Pantone 307 C (C:100 M:12 Y:0 K:27, R:0), among others.
- Primary logo color palette uses Pantone 2945 C (C:100 M:50 Y:0 K:0, R:0) and Pantone Cool Gray 5 C (C:0 M:0 Y:0 K:40).
- Secondary external palette features colors like Pantone 7546 C (C:33 M:7 Y:0 K:85, R:45), Pantone 430 C (C:20 M:9 Y:10 K:45), among others.
- The secondary internal palette includes Pantone 200 C (C:0 M:100 Y:70 K:10, R:0 G:32 B:91), Pantone 136 C (C:0 M:30 Y:90 K:0), Pantone Black (C:60 M:0 Y:0 K:100).

**Typography Information:**

- Primary font for print and digital applications: Din, which can also be used for headers and subheaders in all applications. Din has a simple and lighter appearance, making it suitable for body text.
- For electronic use, the Arial font is recommended for basic accessibility and compatibility.

**Imagery Photography:**

- When placing the logo over an image, ensure clear space and that the background is not busy or overpowering, as it would compromise legibility and brand integrity.

**Spacing Layout:**

- The grid layout provides consistent design for portrait formats and landscape formats, ensuring appropriate placement of logos and other elements on stationery and communications. Provisions for punch holes on stationery are specified in layouts.
- Logo placement can vary between left- and right-aligned on communications depending on application, ensuring compliance with the ATNS grid.

**Brand Voice:**

- The brand messaging is structured around concepts of collaboration and safety in aviation, with a focus on environmental impact wherever possible.
- The strapline for the brand emphasizes innovation, care for people and the planet, and achieving excellence in aviation safety.

**Notes:**

- Brand elements must always be used consistently across all marketing and communications materials. Misuse such as alteration, distortion, or neglecting clear space visibly undermines brand integrity.
- Examples of improper logo usage and strapline misplacement are explicitly provided for clarity.
