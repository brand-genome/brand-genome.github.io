## Brand Guidelines

### 2015

**Logo Information:**

- TxDOT uses designated logos for television and video, such as the 'Solid-bar Logo,' which is a derivative of the 'Flying T' logo and includes a mark (Ž) instead of ®. Solid-color logo variations require prior approval from Creative Services.
- Placement of the logo must maintain clear space equivalent to half the star width and avoid patterned backgrounds. Clear space requirements apply to all versions and formats.
- A text treatment option is available if the logo cannot be used due to patterned or dark collateral designs. Approval from Creative Services is required for text treatments, which must adhere to color, size, and shape regulations.

**Color Information:**

- Color palette guidelines include specific restrictions, such as avoiding desaturated/altered palettes or using unauthorized images. Always align colors with TxDOT goals and brand guidelines.

**Typography Information:**

- Typography specifications require using Franklin Gothic Book for standard body copy and Franklin Gothic Demi for headlines, titles, and callout text. Other typography examples include Italics variations.
- Additional typography rules specify leading (line spacing): 15% larger than type size for headlines and 25% larger for body copy. Font suggestions for size include Franklin Gothic guidelines.
- The body copy for News Releases is Franklin Gothic, with Arial allowed as a substitute. Font alignment ensures uniform readability.
- Letterhead uses Franklin Gothic 11pt font, with templates downloadable from the Communications Division web page.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Brand-compliant imagery emphasizes candid depictions of people, TxDOT logos visible in action, natural road condition focal points, proper safety precautions, and dynamic compositions.
- Non-compliant photographs include those with poor lighting, lack of interaction, obscured TxDOT equipment, unsightly roads, and cluttered compositions.
- Photos to avoid include desaturated colors, inappropriate or staged settings, excessive special effects distracting from the brand story, and unlicensed clip art.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Survey designs and layouts should adhere to concise, accessible spacing rules in documents for public interaction.
- Proper layout for brochures maintains uniform spacing and inclusion of headers/subheaders for clarity.
- Memo templates should follow strict layout and padding guidelines, complying with standardized branding structure.

**Brand Voice:**

- Voice guidelines emphasize respectful, clear, and audience-friendly language to maintain professionalism across audiences such as citizens, employees, business stakeholders, and legislators.
- TxDOT communications adopt Associated Press Stylebook rules, complemented by an internal TxDOT Publications Styleguide for consistent messaging.

**Notes:**

- Creative Services provides brand-compliant business card templates and changes must be approved internally.
- Approved poster design formats emphasize high-traffic reach and visual engagement, requiring approval for any external/third-party design products.
- Templates and downloadable resources are updated regularly on the Communications Division web page.
