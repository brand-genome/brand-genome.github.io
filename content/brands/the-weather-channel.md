## Brand Guidelines

### 2007

**Logo Information:**

- The Weather Channel logotype serves as the most critical visual identity element. Explicit rules state it must not be altered, distorted, or adjusted. Available formats include Illustrator EPS, TIF, and JPG for Mac and PC.
- Clear space rules: A minimum exclusion zone around the logo should always be preserved (measuring twice the height of the logo's 'W').
- Minimum size requirements: 1/2 inch in print, 1 1/2 inch on clothing and stitching, and 40 pixels online (based on a 1024x768 screen).
- Positioning: The logotype should be centered or aligned to the right-hand axis (top or bottom), except in co-branded instances.
- Acceptable logotype color treatments include The Weather Channel Blue (primary), grayscale, black and white, and extreme use cases for reverse black-and-white images.
- Do's and don'ts for logo usage: Examples provided include no color changes, distortion, perspective alterations, drop shadows, outlines, 3D effects, or addition of text/backgrounds.

**Color Information:**

- The primary colors of The Weather Channel brand are The Weather Channel Blue in addition to complementary weather colors (Weather Blue 1–4, Weather Yellow 1–4) with separate Pantone and CMYK values specified.
- On-screen color specifications are provided for Hex and RGB, accounting for variation across monitors and calibration. Specific Hex/RGB codes are detailed for TWC Blue, Weather Blues (1–4), and Weather Yellows (1–4).

**Typography Information:**

- Corporate typeface: Helvetica Neue (fonts include Roman, Medium, Bold, Heavy styles). Substitutions include Arial for documents such as emails and PowerPoint, and Verdana for online HTML as it improves readability.
- Typography style guidelines emphasize simplicity, clarity, and consistency. Guidelines: use upper & lower case, emphasize with weight or color, avoid type effects like shadows or outlines.

**Imagery Photography:**

- The Weather Channel uses photographic styles that emphasize beauty, nature, and realism. Imagery is divided into two categories, people and nature, aligning with the tagline 'Bringing weather to life'.
- Nature photography represents real weather and showcases textures (raindrops, frost) and landscapes (cityscapes, nature, etc.). Light and composition are emphasized for depth and mood.
- Photo selection guidelines include relevance to weather, natural (non-staged), simple and clear images representing real life, and alignment with the brand color palette.

**Spacing Layout:**

- The 'Window Device' is a perfect square aligning with the logotype. It visually portrays forecasts, weather events, and human-related weather narratives. It should be used sparingly, with one device per layout.
- The 'Window Device' can include photographic or graphic elements depicting different weather or time scenarios. Restrictions exist for enhancing its impact.

**Brand Voice:**

- Tagline: 'Bringing weather to life.' The tagline should function solely as a sign-off, not as a headline. Preferred typographic treatment includes Interstate Regular in brand colors.

**Notes:**

- Trademark and copyright rules specify correct registration notices for The Weather Channel logo and name, including the registered trademark symbol® placement. The use of the possessive form of 'The Weather Channel' or 'weather.com' is prohibited.
- Co-branding rules prioritize equal visibility and proper placement of The Weather Channel logo. Partner logos must not encroach on the safe zone or diminish brand presence. Sponsorship formats should always conform to collaboration agreements.
