## Brand Guidelines

### 2020

**Logo Information:**

- The primary logo for the University of Alabama includes the Script A wordmark, along with specific guidelines around isolation area and minimum size (half the height of the tallest letter).
- Script A logo usage must include a ¼ inch clear space around all sides and cannot be used within or on top of other designs, words, or verbiage. Also, it cannot be modified (e.g., beveling, shadowing).
- Only specified wordmarks and identifiers like The University of Alabama, the Capstone A, or division-specific versions are approved for use on items like T-shirts and promotional items.
- Incorrect logo usage includes: modifying the logo, combining it with other logos, or using unapproved colors. Any usage within or on top of other imagery is not allowed.

**Color Information:**

- Official UA colors include Crimson (Pantone 201), Cool Gray (Pantone 430), White, and Black. Accent colors may not dominate UA’s official colors.
- The Script A logo's accepted colors for internal and outlines are Crimson (PMS 201C), Silver (PMS 428 or PMS 430), White, and Black. Other colors require prior approval.

**Typography Information:**

- Minion Pro is an official serif typeface allowing flexibility through numerous typestyles, offered in OpenType and Web font formats.
- Trade Gothic is the official sans-serif typeface with flexibility across typestyles, also available in OpenType and Web font formats.
- Typography guides for UA emphasize consistent use of serif and sans-serif pairings across all channels to reinforce identity and elevate trust.

**Spacing Layout:**

- There must be a ¼ inch clear space surrounding all sides of the Script A logo in any application. The logo cannot be placed right or below any verbiage.
- For the wordmark logo, the isolation area must be no less than half the height of the tallest letter in the logo.

**Notes:**

- All logo and brand usage guidelines must adhere to approvals provided by Strategic Communications or other relevant authorities. Incorrect usage may result in rejection.
