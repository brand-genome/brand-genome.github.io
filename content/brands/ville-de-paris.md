## Brand Guidelines

### 2020

**Logo Information:**

- The logo is recommended to be used primarily in its vertical version for more elegance and stature.
- Two configurations exist for the vertical logo: one with the mention 'Ville de,' and the other without, which is preferred for digital use.
- Horizontal logos also come in two configurations: with and without the mention 'Ville de.' All logo configurations can be used in full color on white backgrounds or reversed white on blue backgrounds.
- Clear space rules for the logo are presented, requiring a protection zone to let the brand breathe.
- Minimum size requirements for the logo in print and digital formats: 10 mm (28 px) or smaller sizes of 5.6 mm (16 px).

**Color Information:**

- The primary color is called Deep Anthracite Blue. CMYK: C100 M40 Y0 K80; RGB: R7 G31 B50; HEX: #071F32.
- The primary color is used across all print and digital materials.
- The logo is used in positive (full color on white background) or reversed (white logo on blue background) versions depending on the medium.

**Typography Information:**

- The main typography is Montserrat, used across both print and digital applications. The font includes 9 weights, all of which can be used. Three principal weights—Regular, Bold, and ExtraBold—are highlighted.
- It is recommended not to use more than two or three font weights within the same document.

**Spacing Layout:**

- The 'marie-louise' layout structure is to be used in communication materials, emphasizing the branding and providing space for messages.
- Various format layouts (e.g., vertical, horizontal, square) include specific spacing measurements for the 'marie-louise' and other elements, such as using X=1/14th H or 1/11th H for spacing.

**Brand Voice:**

- The tone emphasizes proximity, services to Parisians, and reflects care, commitment, and seriousness in city governance.

**Notes:**

- The guideline highlights that the 'Marie-Louise' layout is obligatory and logos must be positioned carefully within it (e.g., top alignment preferred).
- Further elements such as email signatures and co-branding may be detailed in future updates to the brand guidelines.
- No modification to the logo is allowed, including its colors.
