## Brand Guidelines

### 2017

**Logo Information:**

- HCT logo includes three primary alignments: center-aligned stacked formal, left-aligned for English language literature, and right-aligned for Arabic literature.
- Variations include stacked center-aligned logos with campus names in English or Arabic, left-aligned logos, and right-aligned logos for dual and single languages.
- Logo construction specifies proportions: the length of 'Higher Colleges of Technology' is four times the width of the HCT shield, space between campus names is the height of English letter 'H', shield aligned above 3x height of 'H'.
- Construction rules for secondary logos specify left-alignment and spacing measurements, including height considerations.
- Positive black and white logo usage includes without and with half-tone dots, reversed logo allowed for higher than 30% tonal background. Single-color logos preferentially used on primary brand colors.

**Color Information:**

- Primary colors and their codes: Pantone 2747C, Pantone Solid Coated 347 C (CMYK specifications: C100 M0 Y100 K0, RGB equivalents provided). Example HEX codes: #009641, #1D337C.
- Academic divisions have unique colors with corresponding Pantone, CMYK, HEX, and RGB specifications (e.g., Pantone 7598C, CMYK: 0,80,82,21, RGB: 196,69,41).

**Typography Information:**

- Primary typeface for English: Helvetica Neue LT Std. Can be used for all body copy, headings, and captions. Arabic font: AXt Manal in Regular, Bold, Black weights.
- Digital typefaces for office applications: Arial and Calibri for English and Arabic. Applied to documents, correspondence, and reports.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Logo construction specifies clear spacing and safe zones: distance between elements is based on the height of the English letter 'H'. Additional alignment rules applied for shields and names.

**Notes:**

- Guidelines stress consistency in logo usage and approval for variations from CS Communication Department. Alignments and dimensions must adhere to the construction standards shown.
- Formal applications reproduce logos in four colors using the Pantone Matching System. One-color grayscale version acceptable for less formal uses.
