## Brand Guidelines

### 2015

**Logo Information:**

- The logo consists of three parts: The Great Seal of the United States, text treatment, and tagline. These elements must be consistently used in horizontal or vertical formats. Do not alter proportions or use alternative colors.
- Blue is the primary Feed the Future color used for logos. Use blue for logos on white backgrounds or white on blue backgrounds. The tagline must appear in black when the logo uses a white background.
- Guidelines for secondary green color use: The horizontal logo is preferred. Use green on serial publications when permitted; however, the primary logo color is blue.
- Black & white logo usage: Use when color options are unavailable. Combination guidelines for 'positive' (black text, white backgrounds) or reverse setup are outlined.
- Minimum sizing rules for logos: Horizontal logos must be at least 2 inches wide and 0.375 inches tall, and vertical logos must be at least 1.625 inches wide and 0.75 inches tall.
- Clear spacing rules: Maintain a clear zone around the Feed the Future logo free of surrounding text, imagery, or graphic elements.
- Correct logo usage examples: Include adequate spacing around logos, correct color combinations, and observe minimum sizing rules. The logo must maintain visibility and integrity on assorted colored backgrounds.
- Incorrect logo usage examples include modifying proportions, colors, alignment, combining unwanted graphics, or distorting provided format visuals.

**Color Information:**

- Primary Color: Blue (Pantone 7459 C / CMYK 71/26/21/0 / RGB 71/153/181 / HEX #4799B5). Represents optimism for eradicating poverty.
- Secondary Colors: Green (Pantone 7495 C / CMYK 25/0/80/30 / RGB 148/165/69 / HEX #94A545) and Orange (Pantone 7413 C / CMYK 14/58/100/2 / RGB 211/125/40 / HEX #D37D28) for growth and vitality.
- Accent Colors: Black (Pantone Black 7 C / CMYK 62/60/68/54 / RGB 64/59/55 / HEX #403B37) and other muted grays for neutral applications.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary Typography: Gill Sans (Bold for headlines; Regular and Light for body text). Arial can be used as a substitute when unavailable.
- Serif Fonts: Adobe Garamond used for longer documents (e.g., reports) with fallback options of Times New Roman. Captions use Garamond Italic.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Imagery uses appropriately layered photographs without obstructing the logo for readability.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Follow a margin of 0.5 inches for content with formatted spaces and clear alignment when including logos and text.

**Brand Voice:**

- Brand tone emphasizes clarity, professionalism, and consistency across documents (e.g., reports, videos).

**Notes:**

- Guidelines include specific rules for materials such as fact sheets, website banners, social media, and press releases.
