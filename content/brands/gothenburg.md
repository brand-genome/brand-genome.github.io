## Brand Guidelines

### 2017

**Logo Information:**

- The international logotype for Gothenburg is used outside Nordic and German markets. Additionally, an inverted letter 'ö' design is notable, representing 'o' and signifying 'go' in English as a way to symbolize friendliness and likability.
- Logo colors include blue as primary. White is used for colored backgrounds or pictures, black for printing conditions that require it.
- Clear space or 'free space' surrounding the logo equals the size of the letter 'o' in the logo, ensuring no other elements reside within this zone.
- Minimum size requirements specify: the color logo must be no smaller than 18 mm, while black and white versions are allowed down to 13 mm in size.
- Prohibited usage includes recreating or modifying the logo's design, disassembling its parts, and incorporating it into body text or text imitation.
- Collaborative use guidelines specify logo placement and size for joint projects. For example, the project logo is placed at the bottom left and must align size-wise with Gothenburg's logo, which is stationed at the bottom right.

**Color Information:**

- Primary colors: Pantone Process Blue C (CMYK: 100/10/0/0, RGB: 0/148/216, HEX: #0094D8), complemented by other secondary colors including Pantone 302 C, Pantone 7472 C, Pantone 376 C, Pantone 7405 C, and many more. Variants include 20% and 40% lighter shades.

**Typography Information:**

- Identifying typography: DIN Pro Regular and Light for short texts and intermediate headings; DIN Bold only for headings in uppercase; Lutz Headline exclusively used with the 'go' logo in uppercase; Arial Regular for headlines and information texts; Georgia Regular for body copy in communication material and web publications.
- Everyday typography: Georgia used for general text (letters and presentations), Arial preferred for charts and tables (e.g., Excel documents).

**Imagery Photography:**

- Images must reflect core values (pleasant, pluralistic, inspiring) with guidelines emphasizing views showing more than landmarks, detailed close-ups for destination features, portraits that feel spontaneous, and dynamic variation of perspectives and angles. Style emphasizes mild tones and less saturation for a modern, creative feeling.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Placement and size guidelines specify that logos appearing together should be of equal width and appropriately spaced. 'Go' is typically in the upper left corner, and 'Göteborg' in the lower-right corner.

**Brand Voice:**

- Core values define the brand voice: Pleasant (friendly and welcoming), Pluralistic (diverse and open), and Inspiring (creativity and innovation). Messaging supports tourism, events, and knowledge association with Gothenburg.
- Specific approved 'go' messaging words are detailed, including Explore, Enjoy, Discover, Together, Sustainable, Feel the Vibe, Party, Make Memories, etc., limited to approved usage as dictated by the communications department.

**Notes:**

- Guidelines for paper selection suggest uncoated paper for elegant impressions and text suitability, while coated papers are recommended for glossy finishes suitable for photographs and covers.
- Graphic identity guidelines updated periodically as new applications emerge.
- Examples of collaborative use are showcased visually across specific pages.
