## Brand Guidelines

### 2022

**Logo Information:**

- The Orange Middle School brand includes multiple logo configurations: Primary School Logo, Secondary Mascot Logo, Full Mascot, Full Single Color Mascot, Alternate Mark, Alternate Single Color Mark, Orange Script, and Trailblazers Script.
- Logo elements can be customized for specific departments, clubs, or sports by replacing wording in the activity logo and text within the banner. Customization requires vector-based programs such as Adobe Illustrator or Corel Draw.
- Incorrect logo usage guidelines: Do not stretch, distort, rotate, alter colors, invert, or reverse logo colors. Avoid drop shadow effects, adding graphical elements, busy backgrounds, changing opacity, or recreating logo elements.

**Color Information:**

- The primary brand colors are Royal Blue and Orange. Royal Blue: Pantone 287 C, CMYK: 100 75 2 18, RGB: 0 48 135, HEX: #003087. Orange: Pantone 021 C, CMYK: 0 65 100 0, RGB: 255 105 0, HEX: #F47920. CMYK is recommended for print, while RGB is for digital applications.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary logo fonts: Yearbook Solid and Nissan. Yearbook Solid is used for the school name and for activities like sports, clubs, or departments. Heather Oliver, a script font, is used for informal or energetic branding such as athletic uniforms or general apparel.
- Olentangy School District Fonts: DIN OT and Proxima Nova. These fonts are used for secondary branding like headlines, body text, and large text. DIN OT and Proxima Nova come in various weights and styles, ensuring legibility across different applications.

**Brand Voice:**

- The Orange Trailblazer Creed emphasizes independence, boldness, friendliness, acceptance, and finding one's true path. It highlights the sense of family and school pride that forms the brand identity.

**Notes:**

- Requests for logo files or customized logos require contacting the Orange Middle School Principal or the Olentangy Communications Department.
- Legal and mandatory requirements specify that brand elements must conform to approved standards across all applications (e.g., merchandise, materials, signage) and must undergo visual approval by the Principal or the Olentangy Communications Department.
- Guidelines specify the role of the licensing program. Approved vendors and licensing ensure consistency and quality in branded materials.
- Typography recommendations include external resources for font acquisition such as Google Fonts, Font Squirrel, and Adobe Fonts (for users with a Creative Cloud subscription).
