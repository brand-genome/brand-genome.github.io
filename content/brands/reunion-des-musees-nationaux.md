## Brand Guidelines

### 2006

**Logo Information:**

- Logo retains the Frutiger 1969 seal. It includes a dynamic three-element geometric framework derived from the original 'm'. Geometric layout includes a left 'r' and right 'n' revealed through a 66-degree cut of 'm' within a circle.
- Placement rules for various stationery items: Business cards (32mm logo, 5mm offset); Small envelopes (36mm logo, 7mm offset); Large envelopes and A5+ sizes (38mm logo, 10mm offset); Letterhead (38mm logo, 15mm offset).
- Minimum logo size is 10mm. A clear space of 2mm around the logo is required.
- Restrictions on logo alterations: Do not distort, stretch, or separate elements. Size changes must maintain proportions.
- A vertical logo variation is available for book spines, and a 'tilted' version with a 24-degree angle adjustment is permissible in special use cases.

**Color Information:**

- Recommended logo colors are black and white. If other colors are chosen, they must use 100% Pantone without transparency.

**Typography Information:**

- The Univers typeface by Adrian Frutiger (1956) is required for all logo applications. Styles used: Univers 45 Light (for sub-brands/titles), Univers 55 Roman (general use), and Univers 65 Bold (general use).
- Courier New is required for text-based documents and contrast with Univers in RMN communications.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Text in Univers (institutional information) must be placed at a 66-degree angle to align with the diagonal portion of the 'n' in the logo.
- Spacing between text and logo: 2mm on small stationery (business cards), 2.5mm on medium documents (envelopes), and 3mm on larger formats.

**Notes:**

- The new RMN logo and identity adhere to visual coherence rules suggested by Adrian Frutiger's original seal design from 1969, now adapted using a geometric transformation.
- A unique drafting style for using Courier New in text documents enhances dual voices of the brand: objective (commission) vs. subjective (user).
- The new logo was developed by Experimental Jetset in 2006. Their client list includes prestigious institutions like Musée Stedelijk and Centre Pompidou.
