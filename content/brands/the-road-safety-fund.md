## Brand Guidelines

### 2020

**Logo Information:**

- Clear space around logo should be equal to at least 50% of the UN emblem height and width.
- Do not distort, tilt, or change the colors of the logo. Do not use the logo on a busy background, nor only a part of the logo.
- Logo versions include a standard UNRSF logo and Contributor Logos (Bronze, Silver, Gold, and Platinum categories).
- Contributor Logos must not be used in reverse. Elements of Contributor Logos must not be used separately, altered, or have new versions invented.
- Logos should not be crowded by text or other design elements and must maintain appropriate clear space. Logos must not incorporate or imply integration into other symbols, designs, or entities.
- Logo usage includes a reversed white version on a UN-blue background.

**Color Information:**

- Primary colors include UN Blue, Dark Blue, Bright Orange, Dark Gray, Ochre, and White.
- Dark Blue is predominantly used for communication materials rather than UN Blue.
- Bright Orange is used mainly for highlighting elements, and dark gray is not used for blocks outside of the logo.
- Approximate proportions of color usage: detailed via a pie chart diagram in brand materials.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary font is 'Cera Pro', available in six weights: Thin, Light, Regular, Medium, Bold, and Black.
- Cera Pro Regular and Cera Pro Bold are primarily used in communication materials.
- Examples of typeface and usage include 'Cera Pro Regular' and 'Cera Pro Bold Italic' for text purposes.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography should show real street scenes, preferring those in developing countries. Images may depict stress but avoid overly miserable messaging.
- Preference for unique images rather than mosaics. Logos should appear in a corner, preferably top-left or bottom-right.
- Use carefully selected impactful images, colorful infographics, and bright designs to engage stakeholders.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Clear space around Contributor Logos should equal at least 50% of the UN emblem height and width.
- Align logos to avoid crowding or truncating at edges of pages. Maintain proportional replication.

**Brand Voice:**

- Messaging and brand voice reflect saving lives, collaborative impacts, and meeting Sustainable Development Goals through innovative partnerships.
- The tagline emphasizes a world safe for every road user, with key focus on safety and trust.

**Notes:**

- The Contributor Logos (Bronze to Platinum) correspond to donation tiers and are used with strict guidelines. All logo usage language reinforces the UN's authority and visual representation.
- All communication materials must include pre-approved text that reflects the UNRSF's vision and brand messaging standards.
- Contact information for usage approval or technical inquiries is provided in the guidelines.
