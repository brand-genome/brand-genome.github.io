### 2009

**Logo Information:**

- The NASA insignia (logo), also referred to as the 'meatball,' is a unifying element. It must not be modified or distorted in any way.
- The insignia may consist of a one-color version with specified color percentages (100%, 35%, gradient).
- Incorrect usage of the NASA insignia includes adding elements, changing colors, distorting the shape, or deleting any part of the design.

**Color Information:**

- The NASA insignia uses specific colors: NASA Blue (Pantone 286C, HEX #0033A0, RGB 0,51,160) and NASA Red (Pantone 186C, HEX #BA0C2F, RGB 186,12,47).
- For a gradient background, the NASA insignia uses predefined color percentages: vector top (35%), vector bottom (100%-35% gradient), and sphere (100%).

**Typography Information:**

- The official NASA typeface is Helvetica, used in various weights: Light, Regular, and Bold.
- Helvetica is utilized for headlines, body text, and other formal applications. Consistency is emphasized.

**Imagery Photography:**

- NASA imagery must adhere to clean, professional guidelines. Backgrounds should not interfere with the clarity of insignia elements.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Clear space around the NASA insignia must equal twice the diameter of the sphere portion of the insignia.
- Insignia and text margins must maintain appropriate padding equivalent to standard graphic design proportions.

**Brand Voice:**

- NASA's tone of voice is professional, authoritative, and consistent with its role as a leading space exploration agency.

**Notes:**

- All materials using NASA insignia require prior approval from the Assistant Administrator for Public Affairs or a designee.
- The retired ‘worm’ logo is not to be used in official communications unless expressly approved.
