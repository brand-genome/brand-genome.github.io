## Brand Guidelines

### 2014

**Logo Information:**

- The FSSA logo is the primary graphic element. The official logo color is Pantone 348C. Alternate versions include black and reversed (white). Minimum size: 0.75 inches in print or 80 pixels for screen applications. Clear space rule mandates a margin equal to the height of the letter 'F' in 'FSSA' around the logo.
- Examples of incorrect usage include altering the color, removing elements, adding outlines, distorting proportions, using low-resolution images, and placing the logo too close to other visuals. Outdated versions of the logo must not be used.
- The FSSA logo can be used with divisional or departmental identifiers and displayed alongside other logos. The FSSA logo must maintain visual weight and prominence compared to neighboring logos.
- File format conventions for logos: EPS (vector for high-quality printing), JPG/PNG (screen use, kept at original resolution). PMS (Pantone Matching System) colors ensure accuracy for branding, with RGB for digital use and CMYK for typical four-color printing.
- Contact information for obtaining logo files: Phone - 317-233-1764, Email - printing@fssa.in.gov.

**Color Information:**

- Primary color (FSSA Green): PMS 348C, CMYK: 100 0 90 20, RGB: 0 140 91, HEX: #008C4F.
- Secondary colors include Lime Green (PMS 382C, CMYK: 30 0 96 0, RGB: 190 215 56, HEX: #BED738), Yellow (PMS 109C, CMYK: 0 16 100 0, RGB: 255 209 0, HEX: #FFD100), Dark Blue (PMS 294C, CMYK: 100 86 29 22, RGB: 0 46 109, HEX: #002E6C), Medium Blue (PMS 647C, CMYK: 90 63 20 4, RGB: 38 95 146, HEX: #265F92), and FSSA Gray (Black 65%, CMYK: 0 0 0 65, RGB: 119 120 123, HEX: #77787B).
- Color usage rules: PMS and CMYK for print, RGB and HEX for digital materials. Black should primarily be used for body text and not dominate communications. White space should be included in layouts to maintain clarity and emphasis.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary fonts: Myriad Pro (headlines and subheadings) and Minion Pro (body text). These fonts offer a variety of weights and styles for versatility.
- Secondary fonts for fallback use: Trebuchet MS and Times New Roman.
- Typography guidelines: Maintain readability by avoiding distorted proportions. Ensure sufficient color contrast.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography and video services are available through the marketing department. Use professional quality images and video; avoid using low-quality equipment like cell phones. Edit media for efficiency, focusing on clarity and concise messaging. Media should align with FSSA brand guidelines, and release forms must be signed by subjects.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Clear space around the FSSA logo must be equal to the letter height of 'F' in 'FSSA' in all directions.
- White space is important for maintaining a clean layout in communications, allowing emphasis on individual design elements.

**Brand Voice:**

- The FSSA brand values consistent and professional communication to maintain a strong and unified image that resonates with its audience.
- All external communications with the FSSA logo, such as brochures, promotional items, and press releases, must be reviewed and approved by the FSSA Office of Communications prior to release.

**Notes:**

- The style guide establishes a consistent identity for Indiana Family and Social Services (FSSA) but does not cover every possible scenario. Users should consult the FSSA marketing department for guidance outside the scope of the guide.
- Approved PowerPoint templates are provided within the FSSA intranet under 'Communications Policies and Media' to ensure branding consistency across presentations.
- Email signature guidelines: Use Arial 11-point black font. Avoid personal quotes or decorative elements in email signatures. Optional confidentiality clauses may be added as needed.
- Letterhead and business cards are formatted and provided by the marketing department. Letterhead is customizable for each division and accessible on the FSSA Intranet.
