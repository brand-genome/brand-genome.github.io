## Brand Guidelines

### 2009

**Logo Information:**

- Consistent use of the Delta signature will help build visibility and recognition for the airline and will set Delta apart from competitors. Do not stretch, condense or modify the Delta signature. Master artwork must be used and must not be manipulated.
- Full color (red and blue) Delta logo is the master treatment and must appear on a white background (background color value between 0% and 30% in grayscale). Reversed signature may appear in white only (background color value between 50% and 100% in grayscale).

**Color Information:**

- Primary colors: Delta Blue (#003366, RGB: R:0 G:51 B:102), Delta Medium Blue (#006699, RGB: R:0 G:102 B:153). Secondary and tertiary colors include Delta Orange (#EA8A1A, RGB:R:234 G:138 B:26), Delta Yellow (#FFDE00, RGB: R:255 G:222 B:0), Delta Red (#C01933, RGB: R:192 G:25 B:51), Delta Green (#339933, RGB:R:51 G:153 B:51), and others.

**Typography Information:**

- Whitney typeface is used for graphical text (Light, Book, Medium, Semi-bold weights). Verdana and Tahoma are used for HTML text. Specific usages: Primary navigation, buttons for Whitney; copy-dense areas for Verdana; headings and forms for Tahoma.
- Use 'sans-serif' as backup font-family when defining in CSS. Examples provided for defining font-family in styling.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Imagery should tell stories and make human connections. Categories: people, product, destination. Visuals should be full color, sharp focus, and have a central subject with bright contrast. Avoid negative depictions (e.g., dangerous elements).
- Aircraft should not be depicted in angles that may give a negative safety impression or surrounded by dangerous elements. Images for the homepage must follow specific guidelines regarding negative space, color value, and clarity.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Messaging Component - Primary Placement: Half-screen background images are used on the homepage for visual impact (size: 710 x 250 pixels, compressed 45-50%). The layout grid requires spacing to maintain simplicity and style. Images must maintain adequate negative space for marketing messages.

**Brand Voice:**

- Copy tone: Be brief, relevant, provocative, and real. Straight-to-the-point copy guides the eye while balancing warmth with brevity. Avoid overly technical or formal language. Examples provided for concise and engaging messaging.
