## Brand Guidelines

### 2021

**Logo Information:**

- The logos incorporate Canada's Wordmark, stylized maple leaf, and credit lines for acknowledgment. The visual identity is designed to unify and ensure clear branding consistency across communications.
- The Canada Wordmark is a central element of the visual identity with strict guidelines on proportions and protection zones. It is prominently displayed, free of interference or distracting elements.
- Clear space guidelines: Minimum recommended clearance is approximately the height of the 'C' in the Canada Wordmark. Minimum size for legibility must not be smaller than 2.54 cm (1") wide.
- Alterations to the logo are prohibited. Examples of prohibited practices include: changing size, order, direction, adding drop shadows, rotating, stretching, or outlining elements.

**Color Information:**

- Color palette includes Pantone standards and equivalents for print and digital use:
- Pantone 485C (0C, 97M, 100Y, 0K), HEX: #ED2327, RGB: (238, 50, 36)
- Black PMS Process (0C, 0M, 0Y, 100K), HEX: #000000, RGB: (0, 0, 0)
- Pantone 445C (52C, 23M, 30Y, 74K), HEX: #2D4245, RGB: (45, 66, 69)
- Pantone 187C (23C, 100M, 88Y, 16K), HEX: #A91E2D, RGB: (169, 30, 45)

**Typography Information:**

- Primary typeface is Noir Pro (Medium, Regular, Semibold, Bold weights) for headlines and body text. Arial (Regular and Bold) is an alternate option for users lacking Noir Pro access.
- Noir Pro features warm geometric forms inspired by 20th-century aesthetics, balancing contemporary design. Arial is to be used sparingly when Noir Pro is unavailable.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Imagery should portray individuals with dignity, respecting informed consent and proper credits. Avoid stereotypical representation while highlighting active involvement in programs.
- Images should convey hopefulness and results focus, avoiding stereotypes. Content creators must ensure copyright adherence and respectful representation.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Protection zones are defined for logos to maintain visual effectiveness. Clearance space around the Canada Wordmark equals the height of the 'C' in the logo.
- When applying the visual identity, careful spacing around logos ensures optimal visibility. Avoid placing logos on complicated backgrounds.

**Brand Voice:**

- The tone emphasizes transparency and consistency in communication about Canada's investments in international assistance, aiming to engage diverse audiences.
- Messaging avoids complicated terms, opting for accessibility to connect with less-informed domestic audiences through the Canada Aid signature.

**Notes:**

- Canada's international assistance spans contributions in 150 countries addressing social, health, and environmental challenges.
- Partners must acknowledge Canada's contributions in communication materials ranging from newsletters to infrastructure. Safety, dignity, and program independence must be considered.
- Examples of where logos should not be used include personal goods like toothbrushes, unintended project assets, or organizations' office signage.
