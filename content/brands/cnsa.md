## Brand Guidelines

### 2009

**Logo Information:**

- The logotype is composed of an emblem and a textual component ('déroulé'). The emblem consists of a bridge and the initials CNSA, while the 'déroulé' is composed of two lines aligned beneath the emblem's 'C'. The text uses 'Info Text' font and is vectorized to prevent alterations. The emblem and text are inseparable except for specific exceptions (not detailed here).
- The logo is always displayed in 'Process Black C' (Pantone) or CMYK equivalent '100 N'.

**Color Information:**

- The logo color is specified as 'Process Black C' (Pantone) with its CMYK equivalent '100 N'.

**Typography Information:**

- The font used in the textual component ('déroulé') of the logo is 'Info Text'.

**Brand Voice:**

- The visual identity guidelines ensure CNSA's coherent communication. Standards are defined for stationery, brochures, websites, signage, and more. The document introduces accessibility requirements for all communication materials, highlighted by an 'Accessibility Alert' pictogram.

**Notes:**

- The document is organized into two main parts: one outlines the core visual identity rules, and the other provides examples of practical application.
- Three pictograms are used to indicate downloadable elements: for office personnel (documents), for professional use (e.g., printing), and to highlight accessibility considerations.
- The table of contents outlines key sections: logotype, typography, colors, design elements, imagery, and examples for print and digital use.
