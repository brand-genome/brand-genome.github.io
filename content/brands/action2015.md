## Brand Guidelines

### 2014

**Logo Information:**

- The logo includes the action/2015 wordmark and a highlight bar for movement causes. The wordmark uses a condensed sans serif typeface, characterized by a rough texture and a forward slash for motion and action.
- Logo clear space: An exclusion zone marked by a blue boundary must be maintained to ensure space is free from distractions.
- Correct usage guidelines include maintaining proportions, ensuring proper spacing and balance between the cause name and the highlight bar, and not altering the capitalization of the wordmark (action/2015 must not be written with a capital A in the wordmark).
- Incorrect logo usage examples include extended, foreshortened, compressed, overhanging designs, and improper capitalization of the wordmark.
- Minimum reproduction size for the primary logo is 25mm width. For cases with cause names, the wordmark height must be no less than 6.75mm.
- Maximum width for small spaces: The highlight bar should not exceed three times the length of the wordmark.

**Color Information:**

- Color palette includes mono, mono reversed, and color reversed options: CMYK (C0 M0 Y0 K100, C0 M0 Y100 K0, C0 M0 Y0 K80, C0 M0 Y0 K25) and RGB values (R26 G23 B27, R255 G237 B0, R88 G88 B90, R207 G208 B210) are cited.

**Typography Information:**

- The wordmark uses the Wagner Round typeface, slightly distressed for unique personality. Cause names are set in Slab Tall X Regular, available for download at: http://www.dafont.com/slab-tall-x.font.
- Typography roles include wordmark (Wagner Round) and cause names (in Slab Tall X Regular). Cause names are set within the highlight bar.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography or imagery guidelines are not explicitly provided in this document.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Offline spacing guidance includes positioning based on exclusion zones and geometry. For A4, the logo width represents about one-fifth of the format width.
- Online usage includes skyscrapers and banners that employ either full-sized or half-sized signifiers based on overall banner content.
- Partner logos should maintain an exclusion zone and ensure even distance for appropriate breathing space.

**Brand Voice:**

- Action/2015 focuses exclusively on mobilization rather than lobbying, prizes action over discussion, devolves rather than dictates, holds no bias, and is open-source.
- Causes include poverty, injustice, fairness, equality, inequality, climate change, water, children, forests, and girls. Specific places or years can aid cause contextualization (e.g., 2015, Kenya).
- Tone of voice emphasizes urgency and spontaneous action, represented visually through rough or distressed textures and highlight bars.

**Notes:**

- The guidelines primarily explain the consistent application of visual identity across communications (both online and offline).
- Highlight bars visually emphasize causes and come in various straight or irregular forms to suit typography constraints, e.g., descenders.
