## Brand Guidelines

### 2015

**Logo Information:**

- Logo clearance requires 25% additional spacing around the logo for proper distance with other graphic elements. Minimum logo size is 1 inch to ensure recognizability.
- One-color logo versions available in Pantone 350, CMYK (80/43/84/42), RGB (60/82/57) and 100% Black.
- Knockout logo version available in Pantone 134, CMYK (0/18/67/0), RGB (243/208/114), 100% White.
- Logo variants accommodating URL integration specify 20pt Bitter Bold font.
- Logo can be integrated with agency or partner logos but must remain visually distinct. Placement examples were provided, such as separating logos.
- Options for logos with agency sponsors suggest minimum sizes of 4 inches or 2.75 inches depending on context.
- List of logo usage rules includes prohibitions on rotation, stretching, separating elements, altering colors, or adjusting color combinations.
- Logos will be made available to participating agencies and partners via agency intranet sites for use in marketing, advertising, branding materials, and sales.

**Color Information:**

- Pantone 478 (CMYK 35/75/83/39, RGB 105/64/46), Pantone 134 (CMYK 0/18/67/0, RGB 243/208/114), Pantone 350 (CMYK 80/43/84/42, RGB 60/82/57), Pantone 7459 (CMYK 73/27/21/1, RGB 100/149/177). PMS substitutions permitted for offset printing; RGB colors designated for web and onscreen presentations.

**Typography Information:**

- Font families used include Bitter Bold and Franchise Bold. Fonts within logos have been converted to graphic outlines, eliminating the need for access to digital font files.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Clearance and minimum display rules where logo requires at least 25% additional spacing around it, and a minimum size of 1 inch for visibility.

**Brand Voice:**

- Every Kid in a Park is an official name and must always be properly capitalized. Creating an acronym is explicitly prohibited.

**Notes:**

- Contact details for agency leads are provided for assistance in adhering to the guidelines, such as obtaining logos or addressing usage questions.
