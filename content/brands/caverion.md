## Brand Guidelines

### 2013

**Logo Information:**

- The Caverion logo is the most important visual element of the brand identity. It includes a letter 'C' with an arrow symbolizing responsibility for the life cycle of buildings/properties, and reflects the capability to refresh, reinvent, and redesign solutions.
- Logo protection area and minimum size: The logo should be placed in a prominent position with clear and distinct boundaries. A protection area is defined around the logo where no visual elements can intrude. Minimum width for the logo is 20 mm in printed materials and 1 inch on screen.
- Three main color versions of the logo: Blue, White, and Black. Blue logo for white/light backgrounds, White logo for blue/dark backgrounds, Black logo for black-and-white materials.
- Logo usage rules: Primary colors are blue and white. Relative dimensions must not change, and logo colors should not be altered. No other elements like icons or text must be placed in direct connection with the logo.
- The logo should be positioned either at the upper left-hand corner or the lower right-hand corner in layouts.

**Color Information:**

- The primary Caverion colors are deep blue (PANTONE 295, CMYK: C:100, M:60, Y:0, K:45, HTML: #002F5F) and bright white. These colors are used in the main elements of the brand identity (logo, symbol, typography, slogan, icons).
- Caverion uses five additional bright colors to add freshness to its identity. Greys can also be used alongside these colors.

**Typography Information:**

- The primary font family is 'Sanchez'. Available weights include Extra Light, Regular, SemiBold, Bold, and Bold Italic.
- Typography usage on the web: 1. 'Sanchez' Regular is used for menus. Color: Caverion blue or white (special cases). 2. 'Sanchez' Regular is used for headlines and subtitles. Color: Caverion blue. 3. 'Arial' is used for body text and smaller text.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Caverion's imagery consists of three levels: Level I (people living and working in better buildings), Level II (Caverion employees working for better buildings), and Level III (reference images of buildings, spaces, and industrial facilities). Each level has its unique guidelines.
- Level I images showcase stories about better living and working environments, emphasizing Caverion's smart systems and services.
- Level II images focus on Caverion employees providing hands-on services, showcasing customer service in action.
- Level III images serve as straightforward references for buildings and facilities. People should not be present in these images, if possible.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Minimal spacing rules: Use protection areas around logos/symbols to maintain clear distinction. Minimum width of the logo in printed applications is 20mm; for the extracted symbol, it is 5mm.

**Brand Voice:**

- Caverion's tone of voice is described as personal, smart, respectful, and open. The focus should be on tailoring communication for the customer, projecting expertise, and clearly addressing their needs.

**Notes:**

- Master files for printed applications use file formats like PDF, EPS, AI (scalable formats). Files for digital applications include formats like PDF, PNG, JPG, noting that PNG/JPG cannot be scaled without quality loss.
