## Brand Guidelines

### 2017

**Logo Information:**

- Icons are visually aligned with the logo to represent consistency. Rounded corners, line endings, outer curves are defined for icon shaping. Main color is 'Type Blue,' but can be altered to match specific needs.
- Logo displayed in either MP Blue or White for layouts and designs.

**Color Information:**

- Corporate palette and secondary palette colors can be used for informational design such as graphs and pie charts. Contrast colors can also be added if needed.
- For layouts, logo color combinations must use MP Blue or White based on specific layers in designs.

**Typography Information:**

- Font: Paytype Bold; used for headlines and large text. Tight kerning and low leading recommended.
- Font: Paytype Regular; suited for body text, smaller sentences with proportional and tabular numbers. Proportional lined numbers are recommended in text while tabular lined numbers are suitable for tables.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Imagery emphasizes 'Exchanges with a touch of wonder,' focusing on joyful exchanges between people. Photos depict real-life situations in settings like Oslo, Helsinki, and Copenhagen. Styling is natural, cheerful, friendly, and authentic, avoiding explicit pay situations.
- General Brand Images are versatile and can be cropped to fit media. Examples provided.
- Poster Images are intended for expressive layouts, allowing typography and logos to blend effectively. Recommendation to use white typography/logo to ensure layering effect.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Tight and simple grid system emphasized in design. Layers include Top: Type, Middle: Papers, Bottom: Images/Background.
- Simple four-paragraph grid is recommended. Coated paper recommended for print production.

**Brand Voice:**

- Tone qualities include: Human (personal and honest use of language avoiding corporate jargon), Effortless (simple, easy-to-understand language highlighting unique selling points), Skillful (confident and empowering communication avoiding technical jargon), Wondrous (adds playful imagination through storytelling, slang, and puns when suitable), Social (inclusive, direct conversations emphasizing the reader).
- Value proposition 'with a touch of wonder,' emphasizing brand qualities like Wondrous in consistent voice across touchpoints.
- Recommendations for text formatting include examples of good (e.g., storytelling-driven approach engaging the reader directly) versus bad tone (generic functional descriptions). Factors of wonder and joy stressed in writing style.

**Notes:**

- Icons visually align with the logo and font 'Paytype.' Definition includes rounded shapes and equality in line thickness.
- Informational design highlights usage of Papers, Corporate colors, and secondary palette.
- Coated paper suggested for print production to ensure quality.
- Images aim to capture joy, depth, intensity, and a momentary feeling in line with brand qualities.
