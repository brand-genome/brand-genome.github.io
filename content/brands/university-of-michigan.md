### 2021

**Logo Information:**

- Primary and secondary logos for the University of Michigan: Includes primary Block M logo and secondary variations, all defined as key visual identifiers for brand consistency.
- Rules for use of logos for sponsoring organizations. Organizations MAY NOT use the Block M as part of their logo or a standalone letter and MAY NOT use it inappropriately (e.g., altering the design).
- Rules for use of University Seal. Reserved strictly for official Regental or Presidential use and cannot be employed in marketing, student materials, or websites. Specific full-color and single-color Seal guidelines provided.
- Minimum clear space and safety guidelines for both primary and secondary logos provided; emphasis on consistency when the Michigan Block M is utilized.
- Incorrect usage of logos is explicitly prohibited: Cropping, altering the color, using as a watermark, or screens are examples of what is not allowed.

**Color Information:**

- Primary colors of University of Michigan: Maize (Pantone 7406, #FFCB05, C0/M18/Y100/K0, RGB conforming to accessibility standards), and Blue (Pantone 282, #00274C, C100/M60/Y0/K60).
- Supporting palette featuring Tappan Red (#9A3324), Ross Orange (#D86018), and Rackham Roof Green (#75988D).
- Details on accessibility combination rules for the web: Color contrasts are optimized for WCAG compliance (4.5:1 for text). Examples of compliant combinations are provided.

**Typography Information:**

- Recommended typefaces for general use: IBM Plex, Montserrat, Nunito Sans, and Merriweather. Display fonts include Oswald and Playfair Display. Note: Fonts are available for free, but units must purchase any others.
- Guidelines for font legibility: Priority is given to fonts with tall x-heights and generous spacing. Avoid decorative or overly complex serif fonts for the body text.
- Accessibility best practices emphasize sans-serif body fonts and font sizes of at least 12 points for large documents or body copy.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography must reflect the University's spirit of diversity, inclusion, academic excellence, and institutional personality. Contact Michigan Photography for official assets.
- Safety procedures in photography: Ensure all research and laboratory photos adhere to safety protocols (e.g., lab coats, safety glasses). No food or drinks should appear in research images.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Guideline on clear space for logos (not detailed on the exact size). Emphasis placed on maintaining integrity, avoiding overlap with other elements, or overpowering designs near logos.
- Accessibility guidance for spacing in typography includes generous margins, padding in printed content, and minimum 20–25% line spacing for readability.

**Brand Voice:**

- Tone of voice adaptations to align with inclusivity and accessibility guidelines. Messages should avoid jargon, technical terms, and use simple active language.
- Editorial guidelines emphasize clear, engaging, audience-centered writing for external communications.

**Notes:**

- Web accessibility is mandatory for all University of Michigan digital content. Use approved WCAG-compliant tools and follow university accessibility guidelines for every project.
- University seal is strictly controlled and may not appear digitally (besides regents-related web presence). Any use requires advanced approval.
- Printed materials should always account for alternative distribution formats (e.g., contrast-adjusted versions). Designers must anticipate varying needs.
