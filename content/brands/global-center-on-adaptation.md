## Brand Guidelines

### 2020

**Logo Information:**

- Logo font is Owen S. Medium.
- Full color and white-out logo versions are explicitly showcased.
- An exclusion zone defines the minimal distance from the edges of the logo; country-specific logos can be requested.
- Logo usage rules: Do not fill the logo with a texture or image, skew/distort, use 3D versions unless creating physical signage, use in unauthorized colors, change proportions/elements, or attach taglines directly to the logo.

**Color Information:**

- Main brand colors include light grey (#CDCDCD, RGB: 205, 205, 205), mid grey (#868686, RGB: 134, 134, 134), dark grey (#0B5FBA, Pantone 300 C, RGB: 11, 95, 186).
- Secondary colors (e.g., #000000) are intended for sparing use and additional tones account for color blindness considerations like Protanopia-type and Deuteranopia-type views.
- Bright colors like #F9F871 (Pantone unavailable) are used sparingly.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary font is 'Roboto', downloadable from Google Fonts. Available weights include Thin Italic, Light Italic, Regular Italic, Medium Italic, Bold Italic, and Black Italic.
- Secondary font is 'Calibri', commonly used as a default sans-serif font designed by Luc de Groot. Weights include Light Italic, Regular Italic, and Bold Italic.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography emphasizes human elements in real situations of environmental impact rather than wildlife (e.g., polar bears) to elicit engagement and relevance.
- Portrait images of Commissioners are standardized using black-and-white treatments.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Exclusion zones around the logo were specified to maintain visual integrity.

**Brand Voice:**

- The tone of voice should be professional, responsible, intelligent, pioneering, authoritative, respected, prestigious, engaging, distinctive, and dynamic.
- Brand messaging avoids jargon, using short and accessible language wherever possible.

**Notes:**

- Photoshop guides for black-and-white image editing for consistency among Commissioner photos.
