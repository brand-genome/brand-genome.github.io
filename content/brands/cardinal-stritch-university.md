## Brand Guidelines

### 2020

**Logo Information:**

- The Cardinal Stritch University logo includes a rose window icon, representing historical and religious context from the Basilica of St. Clare in Assisi, Italy. All elements of the logo should remain consistent.
- Variations of the logo include: One-Line Logo, Stacked Logo, Icon, Stacked Wordmark, One-Line Wordmark.
- Clear space or safe zone: A 'clear area' around the logo, equal to the height of letters (x-height), must be maintained in all designs.
- Minimum size: The height of the rose window icon must not be less than 1/2 inch in print, with the centered logo no smaller than 7/8 inch tall.
- Do not manipulate or distort the logo (e.g., stretching, repositioning, separating components, adding elements or effects like drop shadows, or redesigning elements of the logo).
- Do not replace elements, include other logos/icons, add new features, or remove elements from the logo.

**Color Information:**

- Official brand colors include primary, secondary, and accent tones. Usage rules for consistent application are defined in the guidelines.
- Primary color palette: Specific values for Cardinal Stritch University's primary colors, in HEX, RGB, and CMYK if specified.
- Accent colors are used sparingly to complement the primary palette and draw attention to key elements. Strict guidelines for color combinations are provided.

**Typography Information:**

- Typography guidelines mandate the use of specific fonts to maintain brand identity in all external-facing documents.
- Serif fonts: Specific serif font families and weights are defined for traditional, formal applications.
- Sans-serif fonts: Defined sans-serif font families complement the serif fonts and provide flexibility for contemporary designs.
- Typography must ensure a balance of tradition and flexibility while maintaining coherence across all communications.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Circular text or photo elements can be used sparingly to draw attention, using only approved fonts (e.g., Barlow or Barlow Condensed) for circular graphics.
- Photography elements should focus on key highlights while adhering to the overall brand identity. Photos placed in circles are permissible for emphasis.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Clear space (safe zone) rule: An area equal to the x-height of letters must remain unobstructed around all logo variations.
- Minimum size: Logos must not scale below specified dimensions (1/2 inch for the icon, 7/8 inch for the Centered Logo).

**Brand Voice:**

- The University brand should communicate consistency and professionalism in all materials. Avoid abbreviations like 'CSU' in writing, and refer to Cardinal Stritch University as 'Stritch' or 'The University' upon second reference.
- Class years for alumni are noted in text after names, enclosed in commas (e.g., 'Lisa Miller, ’88').

**Notes:**

- All materials require strict adherence to consistency in logos and color usage. Exceptions must be cleared through Office of University Communications.
