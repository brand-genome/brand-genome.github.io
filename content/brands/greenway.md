## Brand Guidelines

### 2017

**Logo Information:**

- Guidelines specify the usage conditions for the Greenway brand mark in various contexts and backgrounds. Example usage includes standard logo on white background and reversed (white-only) on colorful backgrounds.
- Minimum size requirements are set for the Greenway brand mark. It should never appear smaller than 13mm in height to ensure visibility and integrity.
- Clear space around the logo equals the width of the 'G' in 'Greenway' denoted as 'X'. This space maintains clarity and visual prominence.
- On colored backgrounds, the logo is reversed (white-only), and the logo must never include a border.

**Color Information:**

- Primary brand colors: Green - Pantone 355 C/355 U, CMYK 85 10 100 10, RGB (0, 148, 68), HEX #009344; Grey - Pantone 430 C/430 U, CMYK 0 0 0 60, RGB (128, 130, 133), HEX #808285.

**Typography Information:**

- Amsi Pro font is used for Greenway route names. It offers variations in weights suitable for maps and communications.
- Myriad Pro is selected for its warmth and readability, offering weights for print and digital. Italic is used for trail names.
- Arial Web is specified for web usage due to its compatibility across platforms.
- Transport New Heavy font is selected for on-road signage and complies with Department of Transport, Tourism, and Sports (DTTAS) guidelines.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Moodboard imagery reflects the Greenway's recreation-focused and scenic ethos, aiming to highlight environmental enhancements and quality of life in adjacent areas.
- Illustrations developed represent wildlife associated with the Greenway, usable in formal communication and design assets.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Logo alignment based on the rule of thirds grid that overlaps circular configurations. Text aligned to bottom of 'Greenway' and subsequent text aligned to the trail graphics.
- Map legends and signage rely on alphabetical order, logo usage, and green line indications for routes.

**Brand Voice:**

- Greenway represents recreational and pedestrian corridors enhancing the environment and quality of life. Accessibility is emphasized.

**Notes:**

- Irish language policy specifies compliance with the Official Languages Act 2003, requiring bilingual signage or separate signs of equal prominence.
