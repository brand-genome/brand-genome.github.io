## Brand Guidelines

### 2019

**Logo Information:**

- Metro Transit logo minimum size specifications: 1.375 inches without tagline, 1.75 inches with tagline.
- Clear space requirements for the logo: Minimum space around logo equals 'x-height' of the lowercase letters in the logo.
- Acceptable Logo Usage: Use appropriate file format, approved artwork. Do not distort proportions, redraw, or change colors.
- Logo versions: Full Logo with contact information includes 'metrotransit.org' and phone number, using Avenir 55 Roman at 10 pt in Transit Blue.
- Circle 'T' icon for limited space applications, can be paired with URL. Examples provided.
- Reversed colors (e.g., PMS 485 red icon with white text) are acceptable for use on dark backgrounds.
- Different logo formats available: EPS, PNG, AI, PSD, and PDF for various applications. Examples given.
- Examples of incorrect usage of the logo, including distorting proportions, unapproved color changes, and alterations to symbol or typeface.

**Color Information:**

- Metro Transit primary palette: Transit Blue (PMS 287), Transit Red (PMS 485), and Transit Gold (PMS 116). Includes HEX, CMYK, and RGB codes.
- 2-color logo (PMS 287 Blue, PMS 485 Red) is the preferred logo treatment. Details provided for 2-color exceptions on dark backgrounds.
- 1-color logo options include 100% Black and reversed out (white on PMS 287 or PMS 485 backgrounds).
- METRO system colors include Blue (PMS 287), Red (PMS 485), Green (PMS 348), Orange (PMS 151), and Gray (PMS 430) with their respective HEX, CMYK, and RGB values.

**Typography Information:**

- Preferred typeface: Avenir. Specific weights include Book, Roman, Medium, Heavy, and Black with respective oblique styles.
- Typography usage standardizes consistency in branding for Metro Transit communications, maintaining a strong brand identity.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Imagery should align with brand tone and feature Metro Transit's current fleet, technology, and diversity representation.
- Photos provided by Metro Transit must not be altered to substantially change content or meaning and require attribution ('Metro Transit').

**Spacing Layout:**

- Minimum bar length for METRO line logos must maintain equal space to the left and right of the text 'METRO'.
- Guidelines for PowerPoint presentations: Avoid placing text or images in the blue bar, maintain clear space, and follow font and layout standards.

**Brand Voice:**

- Brand tone and messages: Clean, modern design emphasizing accessibility. Voice should sound intelligent, direct, caring, and of good value.

**Notes:**

- Metro Transit logo is a registered trademark of the Metropolitan Council and must not be altered or misused to cause confusion.
- Applications where branding applies include email signatures, promotional items, uniforms, badges, letterhead, and business cards.

### 2014

**Logo Information:**

- The Metro Transit logo is described as the single most visible symbol of the company. It must be preserved with correct and consistent usage, maintaining its original proportions. A list of dos and don'ts for logo usage is provided in the document.
- Do's for logo usage: Use the appropriate file format for specific applications, produce the logo from approved electronic artwork.
- Don'ts for logo usage: Do not condense, expand, distort, redraw, or alter the logo symbol; do not change the logo color to anything non-approved.

**Color Information:**

- The primary color palette includes Blue (PMS Reflex Blue), Red (PMS 485), and Yellow (PMS 116). RGB and CMYK values are provided: Blue (RGB: 0 83 160, CMYK: 100 72 0 6), Red (RGB: 237 27 46, CMYK: 0 100 91 0), Yellow (RGB: 255 210 0, CMYK: 0 16 100 0).
- Secondary palette uses gradients based on the primary colors (Red gradient, Blue gradient, Yellow gradient) and can complement the primary palette for emphasis.
- A tertiary palette includes a soft blue gradient and a light gray gradient, to be used sparingly for important information.

**Typography Information:**

- The preferred typeface for Metro Transit is Avenir, used across both online and printed documents. Avenir variants: Light, Light Oblique, Roman, Oblique, Medium, Medium Oblique, Heavy, Heavy Oblique, and Black Oblique are available for different roles.
- Gill Sans is suggested as a signage typeface and is reserved for bold headlines, used sparingly and not for long copy. Available styles include Regular, Italic, Bold, and Bold Italic.
- Arial is the secondary online typeface for all HTML live text and can be used as a substitute for graphical text when needed. Available styles: Regular, Italic, Bold.

**Notes:**

- This brand guideline document was last updated in January 2014. For additional specific style guides (e.g., signage, vehicle graphics, web), contact Creative Services at 612-349-7671.
