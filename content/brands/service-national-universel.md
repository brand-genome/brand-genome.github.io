## Brand Guidelines

### 2018

**Logo Information:**

- Logo consists of a triple contour in red, white, and blue, representing governmental communication. The central symbol is a tricolored cockade within the letter 'O' of 'national', emphasizing national representation. Text aligns with different font sizes. Minimum width is 20 mm.
- Zone of protection around the logo is defined by the size of the cockade, ensuring no graphic elements appear within this zone.
- Formats to use for the logo include: .ai (spot or CMYK) for print, .png (RGB) for Word, PowerPoint, web/email, and tablet/mobile.
- The logo can be applied against visuals with proper neutral breathing zones but must avoid visual disturbance. Logos may be fully placed on colored backgrounds or neutral zones of photos (e.g., sky, wall, table). Alignment rules are specified: 'The top of the visual must be aligned to the bottom of the word ‘service’, or the bottom must be aligned to the top of the word ‘universel’.
- Logo usage is prohibited on visuals with complex structures or on surfaces where the elements of the logo may blend into similar colors such as magenta, red, or dark orange.
- Logo monochrome version rules: Gray levels convert colors to specific black levels; for instance, red converts to black at 60%, blue to 100% black, light blue to black at 30%. Fully black or fully white versions are also acceptable, depending on specific use cases (e.g., white on black backgrounds, grayscale for black-and-white visuals).

**Color Information:**

- Standard governmental communication colors: First set - Pantone Red 032 (C0/M100/Y100/K0), Pantone Process Blue C (C100/M0/Y0/K0), Pantone 288 C (C100/M100/Y0/K0), White (C0/M0/Y0/K0). Accompanying colors - C0/M100/Y100/K0 (R: 233 G: 78 B: 36), C0/M55/Y90/K10, dark blue (R48/44/118).
- Blue and white are primary colors to ensure readability and complementing red or lighter blue which are considered supporting colors.

**Typography Information:**

- Main typefaces: Bebas Neue (condensed, modern, for titles and headlines) and Roboto (standard, for body text and subtitles, available in various weights). Arial can be substituted in digital applications if Bebas Neue or Roboto are unavailable.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Design includes a main graphic showing silhouettes against a blue horizon background representing youth and dynamism. The primary imagery is declinable for most communication materials like visuals of orange or blue used vertically based on layouts.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Spacing around logos adheres to predefined protective zones. Grids for brochures and other page layouts feature four narrow columns with uniform margins of 12 pts, and inter-column spacing (gutter) of 5 mm.
- On PowerPoint presentations, the logo placement varies from the header (aligned top left) and overlaps between visual elements as required.

**Notes:**

- Various formats for printed materials, stationery, posters, flyers, and brochures are detailed, including alignment rules, logo sizes, margins, and layout constructions. Additional guides for digital platforms include logo usage across social media graphics and website headers.
