## Brand Guidelines

### 2020

**Logo Information:**

- Our logo has two approved styles: horizontal and vertical. It can be reversed or reproduced in one- or two-color. A tone-on-tone logo is acceptable for embroidered apparel or hats; thread should be a shade or two darker for best visibility. The logo without the registration mark ® should be used for any promotional items.
- Our logo is an iconic piece of our heritage; it is important that we use it respectfully and correctly. Use the logo files provided by Boise Cascade, available in multiple formats. NOTE: use PNG logos for digital purposes; use other formats like EPS, JPG, etc. logos for print. For best results, do not mix PNG and JPG logos in the same document.
- Do not alter the logo by: (1) Changing or realigning the elements, (2) Rotating, stretching or skewing, (3) Recoloring or adding special effects, (4) Using the tree-in-a-circle symbol as a stand-alone graphic element/pattern, (5) Using the logo in a sentence. Ensure adequate space surrounding the logo (equal to ½ the height of the tree symbol).
- External usage requests must comply with our Trademark License Agreement. The ® symbol must appear with the logo on certain items.
- Updated trademark placement: The trademark symbol ® has been moved out of the tree symbol to the end of our logo, kerning has been tightened, and division/business unit names are capitalized and reduced slightly for brand hierarchy.

**Color Information:**

Secondary colors can be used sparingly for emphasis but should not be regularly used as font colors.

**Typography Information:**

- Fonts:
Arial is the typeface used in our logo; Montserrat is a more contemporary style. Use Arial for Word/PowerPoint documents; Montserrat for locked-down designs in PDFs.
Recommended Usage:
- Headings: Arial Black or Montserrat ExtraBold
- Subheads: Arial Bold or Montserrat SemiBold
- Body Copy: Arial or Montserrat in 10 pt, 11 pt, or 12 pt
- Disclaimers: Arial Narrow or Arial Italic in 8 pt
Avoid underlined text unless it’s a hyperlink. Ensure text is left-justified with ragged right.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Guidance on choosing imagery:
Reflect the brand's personality. Showcase safety practices, accurate portrayals of the organization's culture, and ensure identifiable subjects have signed photo releases.
Imagery principles: Involved (showcase people interacting), Straight Forward (capture real moments), Brave (new and experimental perspectives), Thoughtful (reflect team/customers), and Genuine (pay homage to work ethics).

**Notes:**

- Pantone spot colors are only for print vendors and should not be converted to CMYK. Use the displayed built color profiles.
- Color contrast for accessibility should be considered to ensure readability for visual impairments. Choose appropriate colors for backgrounds and text.
