## Brand Guidelines

### 2019

**Logo Information:**

- The brand features three logo formats: full brandmark (used for packaging and print collateral, small/delicate usage recommended), square brandmark (used when more appropriate, not for packaging but allowed on print materials), and icon (used for social platforms and print collateral, ideal when the larger version loses detail at small scales).
- Clear space rule: Space around the brandmark should equal the height and width of the 'N' in 'NYX'. The logo should not be placed over photographs or patterned backgrounds that interfere with readability.

**Color Information:**

- Master brand palette includes the following HEX values: #d18a3d, #040430, #d5bdb1, #f40b3d, #8d5843, #3d64f2, #d2ad37, and #a6f4c2. Separate palettes are designated for lips, eyes, and face makeup, with metallic-inspired colors paired with contemporary pops.

**Typography Information:**

- The font family used in branding is Montserrat, including font weights such as Bold and Medium Montserrat Italic.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography style guidelines emphasize clean, minimal, and provoking imagery. Priority is on the person or the makeup products, avoiding clothing details, patterns, or objects in backgrounds.

**Spacing Layout:**

- The full brandmark and logotype function within a grid system. The letters in 'NYX' can be rearranged across the grid while maintaining readability from left-to-right or top-to-bottom.

**Brand Voice:**

- The brand voice is described as youthful, clean, joyful, contemporary, expressive, sophisticated, daring, human, and beautiful. It avoids traits such as childish, sterile, ingenuine, pretentious, over-wrought, unattainable, pompous, weak, or overly traditional.

**Notes:**

- Secondary brand elements include constellation graphics, adding a decorative or practical touch. Lines between stars interact with typography to direct customer focus while providing a contemporary feel.
