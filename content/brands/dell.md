## Brand Guidelines

### 2012

**Logo Information:**

- Dell logo comes with primary stand-alone logo variations in Dell Blue and White. Either may be used for introductions to the brand. Dell Blue (C92 M34 Y0 K0, RGB 0,133,195, Hex #0085C3) and the white logo can be used as primary logos. The ring around the logo protects the name and showcases heritage.
- Clear space around the logo must be maintained. The minimum required is defined by 'X', which is equal to the height of the letter D in the wordmark.
- Minimum logo size: 0.5 inches (12.7 mm) in height for print and 30px in height for digital applications. Use beyond this size compromises integrity and legibility.
- Primary logos are Dell Blue and White. Dell Blue logos should only be used on white and Dell Light Gray backgrounds. White Dell logos can be used on any color from the supporting palette or on clear, unobstructed imagery.
- Principle usage of Dell Blue or white logo should serve as the first impression. Dell Gray variations can be used after the first usage.
- Incorrect logo usage includes: redrawing, stretching, altering proportions, rotating, creating variations, or locking up product names, colors, or partner logos. The logo must not be used as a read-through window or visually paired with unapproved elements.
- Previous logo designs have been updated to retain the canted E and bold typeface encircled with a strong, simple ring. The use of older logo designs must be discontinued.

**Color Information:**

- Primary Dell Blue color: C92 M34 Y0 K0, RGB 0,133,195, Hex #0085C3. Used for the primary logo.
- Dell Light Gray is part of the color palette and can be used as a supporting background color.
- Neutral white is applicable as a background color for Dell Blue logos or as an overlay for white logos.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Clear space around the logo must equal the height of the letter D in the wordmark ('X'). This space must remain free of text, images, or other elements.

**Notes:**

- The Dell logo is designed to maximize visual appeal and effectiveness; a custom Dell Blue color (not stock Pantone) is used to ensure uniqueness.

### 2008

**Logo Information:**

- Dell logo is used in both black and color versions. The black version is used against a colored background, and the color version is for a white background. Highlight colors in headlines must match the color version of the Dell badge. Logo files are available as PSD and vector AI formats.
- Logo badge must have a drop shadow with specified settings: Blend Mode: Multiply; Color: Black; Opacity: 50%; Angle: 120°; Distance: 7; Spread: 0; Size: 18.
- Clear space around the logo badge is equal to the width of the vertical stroke in the 'L' letterform.
- Minimum logo size is 42px × 42px or 0.5 inches. Placement rules: aligning parts of the logo with the color background plane (e.g., 'E' and 'L' alignments).

**Color Information:**

- Color palette includes Dell Blue (Pantone 300C, CMYK: C-100 M-43 Y-0 K-0, RGB: R-0 G-102 B-204), Black (Pantone Black3C, CMYK: C-0 M-0 Y-0 K-100, RGB: R-30 G-30 B-30), White (RGB: R-250 G-250 B-250), and several accent colors including Pantone 485C (Red), Pantone 1375C (Orange), Pantone 124C (Yellow), and more.
- Rules: No tints allowed for color bands or highlighted headlines, but tints are permissible in graphs and charts. All colors must print at 100%. Multiple colors can be used for longer communications.

**Typography Information:**

- Font family is Gotham, with styles including Ultra, Black, Bold, Medium, Book, and Light. Font can be purchased from Hoefler & Frere-Jones at typography.com.
- Typographical hierarchy details the use of Gotham Ultra for main headlines (uppercase only, knockout to white from black background), Gotham Bold for call-to-action text (uppercase with Dell.com), Gotham Book for body copy and quotes, and Gotham face weights for partner taglines and legal copy. Color usage varies as appropriate.
- Localized font substitutions for languages not covered by Gotham include Arial Black for Greek, Russian, Arabic, Hebrew, and others.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Dell photography emphasizes product as the hero with contrast and dramatic lighting. Lifestyle photos are candid, engaging, and realistic, avoiding cheesy stock imagery. Dell people depicted are real, diverse users.
- Lifestyle photography uses captured moments in time with interaction with products. Black-and-white images with accent colors and dramatic cropping convey emotion and relatability. Layers and high-contrast visuals maintain focus on humanity and simplicity.
- Product photography for desktops and notebooks should use dramatic lighting, surface reflections, and aim to make products as desirable as possible.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Clearance space around the logo is defined as the width of the vertical stroke in the 'L' letterform. Placement rules ensure alignment of certain parts of the logo with color planes.
- Design hierarchy prioritizes bold headlines, hero imagery, dimensional backgrounds, straightforward copy, and call-to-action elements.

**Brand Voice:**

- Tone conveys frankness and engagement expected from a friend. Dell’s messaging is bold yet performance-driven, letting facts speak for themselves.
- Call-to-action must be uppercase, short, and compelling. URLs are styled as DELL.COM/XxxxxxXxxxx. Typography baseline and spacing need to follow specified guidelines.

**Notes:**

- Segment identifiers are explicitly disallowed.
- Pending approval for co-op advertising material (noted on page).

### 2010

**Logo Information:**

- Logo assets are available from the DAM (Digital Asset Manager) tool at http://thedam.enfatico.com/. Direct link to brand assets folder: Dell Brand Assets.

**Color Information:**

- All colors in Dell's color palette may be used to create rounded corner shapes.

**Typography Information:**

- Museo for Dell and Museo Sans for Dell are specified typefaces for branding.
- Discontinued typefaces must be replaced with Museo for Dell or Museo Sans for Dell.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Lifestyle imagery demonstrates Dell's role in day-to-day life and business, focusing on people pursuing important activities to them.
- Product imagery reflects Dell's engineering and functionality, using clean and uncluttered styling with subtle shadows and props.
- Supporting imagery represents abstract concepts or reinforces primary themes, emphasizing Dell's brand personality.
- Imagery style uses natural lighting and vibrant backgrounds emphasizing depth of field and color accents.
- Cropping guidelines require emphasis on people using Dell products with aesthetic focus avoiding unattractive elements.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Rounded corners should not be smaller than 1'x1'. Incorrect uses include combining rounded and sharp corners, changing the ratio, or overusing rounded corners on a page.

**Brand Voice:**

- Voice standards will be available February 22, 2010.

**Notes:**

- Brand assets including logos, fonts, and campaign creatives are stored under the Dell Brand Assets folder on the DAM tool.

### 2012

**Logo Information:**

- Dell Blue or white logo (tagline and stand-alone) may be used as primary logos.
- Minimum digital size reduced to 30px x 30px.
- Incorrect logo lockup usage guidelines added.

**Color Information:**

- Dell Blue should be the default color as often as possible.
- Use of more than three supporting colors with Dell Blue should be avoided.

**Typography Information:**

- Added regional typeface examples.
- Arial added to discontinued typefaces.
- Expanded do's and don'ts of typographic style.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography style redefined—photojournalistic.
- Added examples of photojournalistic styles.
- How to obtain imagery that reflects Dell guidelines.
- Avoid use of overly manicured, staged, or digitally enhanced imagery.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Ensure consistent alignment and perspective of objects in imagery.
- Use subtle shadows and reflections in product images.

**Brand Voice:**

- Ensures creative alignment with brand voice through concept approvals.

**Notes:**

- Dell has accounts for sourcing imagery through Corbis, Getty Images, Veer, etc.
- Users can access logos, typefaces, and brand assets through the Digital Asset Manager (DAM).
- Illustrations should align with Dell branding and use Dell color palette, avoid gradients or drop shadows.
- Illustration application examples focus on realism and avoid decorative or overly conceptual styles.
