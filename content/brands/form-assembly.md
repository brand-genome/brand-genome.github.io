## Brand Guidelines

### 2018

**Logo Information:**

- Primary FormAssembly logo designed in full color to be used for light-colored backgrounds. Secondary uses include favicons, apparel, and accessories.
- White logo variation designed for dark backgrounds. Can also be used in favicons, apparel, icons, and thumbnails.
- Grayscale logo variation designed for neutral or specific external use cases where grayscale solutions are required, particularly for collections with other logos.
- Clear Space/Spacing rule: Surrounding content must maintain at least the height of the 'A' crossbar in FormAssembly. Exceptions exist when logo interaction is acceptable as long as it remains legible.
- Minimum size requirements for logo: 100px width for web, 1.5 inches for print. Minimum acceptable size for icons is given as 22px or 0.3 inches respectively.
- Logo usage don'ts include stretching/skewing, recoloring, rearranging elements, rotation, use of wrong backgrounds, overlaps, incorrect proximity, improper in-sentence usage, old logos, and oversizing.
- Flowline is introduced as a line of text that complements the logo but is not part of the logo. Used for campaigns to add a company slogan or message and typically appears adjacent to the logo.
- Powered By logo variation specifically for use near footer sections of sponsored forms. It must be between 100px to 200px wide.

**Color Information:**

- Primary palette contains blues for core branding: Voyage Blue (#2193C2), Slate Blue (#5D99B9), Deep Blue (#0F3751), Ocean Blue (#1F5577), Sky Blue (#4CABE0), and Form Blue (#77C7E9). Each color has specific RGB values.
- Gray palette includes Steel Gray (#A7A9AC), Midnight Gray (#343b3e), 50% Gray (#DCDDDE), Cloud White (#F2F2F2), and Titanium White (#FFFFFF), each with RGB values for usage in text and backgrounds.
- Alternate colors include functional secondary options like Red (#EE6B65), Green (#8CCE79), Gold (#FEC20F), and Orange (#FAA822) for buttons, status indicators, and accents.
- Gradient recommendations for branding transition include Ocean Blue to Titanium White or Deep Blue to Cloud White for visual overlays or backgrounds.
- Guidance to use color combinations strategically. Example schemes include proper contrasts and balance for various contexts like 'Ideal', 'Special Case', and 'Avoid' combinations.

**Typography Information:**

- Header font: Roboto Slab, used for headlines, titles, and major textual hierarchy.
- Body/Content font: Open Sans, used for subheaders, captions, and body text. Weights available: Extrabold, Bold, Semibold, Regular, Light, italics for each.
- Font hierarchy defined: Roboto Slab for H1/Title, Open Sans for headers, body text, pull quotes, and captions.
- Hyperlinks are in brand color, underlined, and must remain legible against the background.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography style is professional, clean, and stress-free. Depicts productivity and happiness. Colors in images should complement brand palettes.
- Use black and white photography with tasteful contrast when colored images are too visually busy.
- Guidelines for overlays, including Deep Blue, Ocean Blue gradient, or gray tints. Overlay colors are used to neutralize busy images and provide contrast for text.
- Photo cropping must maintain subject integrity and thoughtful composition. Avoid awkward or random cropping.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Recommended clear space surrounding logos: spacing equal to the crossbar height of the 'A' in FormAssembly.
- Avoid improper layouts: ensure text placement across images offers adequate contrast and separation from busy visuals.

**Notes:**

- Branding guide focuses on absolute adherence to rules for promotional and partner materials with prior approval. Non-compliance is the sole responsibility of creators.
- For any query about branding usage, contact the marketing team via marketing@formassembly.com.
