## Brand Guidelines

### 2019

**Logo Information:**

- Minimum size requirements for horizontal logo: 40mm. Minimum size requirements for vertical logo: 30mm.
- The logo should primarily be used on white backgrounds in specified colors. Examples indicate use cases for full color, monochromatic (black and white), and reversed (white on dark backgrounds).
- Logos are available in variations for color (colorized, monochromatic in black, white, or reversed white on dark backgrounds) and special applications (on metallic finishes like gold, silver, and textured surfaces for institutional pieces). Some logo applications require prior approval.
- Do's and don'ts for logo usage include prohibition of distortion, rotation, outlining the logotype, changing font styles, altering colors, adding transparency elements, or placing the logo with extraneous textures or visual interference.
- Safe zone rules not explicitly drawn, but multiple visual examples reveal adequate blank space encircling the logo elements on formal and printed applications.
- Incorrect logo usage examples: distorting logo, changing colors or typeface, adding backgrounds, inappropriate transparencies or overlays.

**Color Information:**

- Primary colors include: Azul (CMYK: C100 M072 Y000 K019; RGB: R000 G066 B136; HEX: #00498F), Amarelo (CMYK: C000 M019 Y100 K031; RGB: R187 G151 B005; HEX: #BB9705), and Preto (CMYK: C000 M000 Y000 K100; RGB: R000 G000 B000; HEX: #000000).
- Secondary palette includes: Azul Escuro (CMYK: C098 M100 Y035 K038; RGB: R24 G31 B73; HEX: #181F49), Cinza 75% (CMYK: C000 M000 Y000 K075; RGB: R100 G101 B103; HEX: #646567), Amarelo Escuro (CMYK: C029 M035 Y100 K030; RGB: R151 G124 B13; HEX: #977c0d), among others.
- Color usage recommends applying primary colors like Azul and Amarelo on white backgrounds or in monochromatic style for better visibility.

**Typography Information:**

- Preferred fonts for corporate communication include Circular (Book and Bold weights) and Din (Book and Bold weights). Described appropriate for formal documents, signage, and marketing materials.
- Auxiliary fonts include Garamond (Book and Bold weights) and Verdana (Book and Bold weights). These are optional and are recommended for internal documents and presentations.
- Preferred fonts for web use include Raleway (Book and Bold weights).

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photographic imagery should aim for impact and be conceptual rather than descriptive. Simple images with minimal visual clutter are recommended. Prefer closed and focused shots for emphasis.
- Guidelines recommend using wide-angle shots only when necessary, ensuring contrast in colors and lighting for aesthetic purposes. Open skies are suggested as a compositional element.
- Photographs of people should focus on central elements (portraits or related themes). Overcrowded images, clichés, or pictures with exaggerated gestures should be avoided.

**Spacing Layout:**

- No explicit grid systems or alignment rules provided, but visual examples show centered alignment with consistent spacing around logos, both horizontally and vertically.
- Formal documents (business cards, letterheads, envelopes) specify dimensions: Business Card 9x5 cm, Letterhead 21.5 x 27.9 cm, and envelopes of 22.9x11.4 cm or 36x26 cm in high-quality certified paper.

**Brand Voice:**

- Messaging should align with professionalism and sophistication, reflecting the values and identity of Crea-SP. Messaging in typography, imagery, and color must focus on credibility and authority.

**Notes:**

- Special applications include using the logo in metallic finishes, embossing (relief), or braille. Prior approval is required from the Crea-SP communication team.
- Avoid logos in a 3D format for digital displays or publications. 3D logos are reserved for physical spaces like signage and promotional installations.
- Logo animation should maintain logical sequence—symbol first, logotype follows—and avoid rotation, distortion, or transparency effects.
