## Brand Guidelines

### 2018

**Logo Information:**

- The logo is only to be used in black or white. Use of other colors is prohibited.
- The segment names are tied to their segment colors. Spacing between logo and product name should be 2x the width of 'I'.
- Alignment for product names and multi-line names: Left, right, and center alignments are allowed, with line spacing at half the height of 'I' and alignment to ascender heights.
- Improper use: Avoid using the 'Y' from the logo as a graphic element. The logo should never be written in all caps in body text.

**Color Information:**

- Corporate and segment colors are defined with specifications. Examples include: Home uses Pantone 876 C (CMYK 5-45-65-50; RGB 131-98-65); Personal uses Pantone 805 C (CMYK 0-87-64-0; RGB 255-69-79). Other colors include Car, Pro, and Custom Install. Black, white, and grey are core corporate colors.

**Typography Information:**

- Corporate typeface: Helvetica Neue LT Std with variations including 55 Roman, 56 Italic, 85 Heavy, and 86 Heavy Italic. Supports diverse weights.
- Multi-language typeface: Noto Sans (Regular and Bold), used for multilingual materials like quickstart guides. Avoids 'tofu' display of unsupported characters.
- Substitution: If Helvetica Neue LT Std is unavailable, Arial is used, especially for Microsoft Office documents to ensure compatibility.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Lifestyle images with people: Showcase real-life people that the audience can relate to and identify with.
- Lifestyle images without people: Clean, aspirational settings where products are shown in realistic use-case environments.
- Product photography: Products must be depicted iconically, simply, and authentically with specified views, including front with and without grille, angle front, back, and top.
- Speaker parts: Interior components should be photographed with as much attention and care as external components.
- Line art: Designed for simplicity, emphasizing engineering, where tweeters appear in full color and the rest in lines.
- Technical drawings: Often used in assembly instructions; they must remain simple but include relevant technical details.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Spacing for product naming: Space between logo and product name must be 2x the width of 'I'. Multi-line product names should have line spacing at half the height of 'I' and aligned to ascender heights.

**Brand Voice:**

- Tone of voice: Clear, concise, contemporary, and relatable. Friendly, impish, and informative, avoiding a patronizing tone.
- Copywriting rules: Avoid corporate jargon and irrelevant references. Use metric units unless addressing US audiences. UK English is standard (e.g., 'colour' vs. 'color').
- Approved descriptions for Dynaudio's brand story are provided in 10-, 25-, 50-, and 100-word variants.

**Notes:**

- Ads: Keep them clean and concise, allowing for creative wordplay. Example campaigns and taglines are provided.
- Packaging: Design must be clean and simple, using segment colors, while ensuring product visibility.
- Magazines can include creative editorial content alongside product information to enhance engagement.
- Manuals: Clean and informative, with an editorial touch. Front cover should feature line art in corporate colors, and the back cover should remain simple.
- Contact information for PR, offline design, online design, and social media is provided.
