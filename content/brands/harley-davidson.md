## Brand Guidelines

### 2001

**Logo Information:**

- Improper usage examples of logos on dark backgrounds: Colors of script and wheel artwork must be reversed as shown for legibility.
- Staging (clear space) rule: An area of isolation, based on the height of 'H' in H.O.G., ensures no typography or design element is placed within the safe zone.
- Federal trademark symbol display: The primary placement of the ® is centered below the right wing for legibility and may be incorporated within the logo for small or self-contained designs.
- Authorization for using Harley-Davidson logos is restricted to materials provided by the company and accessible via Dealer Ad Planner CD-Rom or approved distributors.

**Color Information:**

- Primary colors include Harley Orange (Pantone 165: C=0 M=60 Y=100 K=0), Motor Orange (Pantone 159: C=0 M=65 Y=100 K=9), H.O.G. Brown (Pantone 464: C=47 M=65 Y=100 K=0), and H.O.G. Yellow (Pantone 130: C=0 M=27 Y=100 K=0).
- Color usage rule: Harley Orange is used in the color versions of the Bar & Shield and certain logos like 'Screamin’ Eagle,' while Motor Orange is used in Genuine Motor Accessories logos.

**Typography Information:**

- Primary font options: Americana Regular, Bodoni, Helvetica (light to bold, roman or italic), Serifa, and Optima.
- 'Americana Regular' is the corporate typeface for Harley-Davidson wordmarks. Bodoni works well for headlines and body text.
- Typography rule: Minimize visual clutter by using no more than two different typefaces per document.

**Imagery Photography:**

- General photography guidelines: Motorcycles should appear stock with no aftermarket parts. Preferred profile view is from the right side.
- Riding attire for photos: Must include motorcycle boots, gloves, helmet, leather jacket, and preferably MotorClothes apparel.
- Prohibited imagery: No stunts like wheelies or burnouts. No gang-related implications. No alcohol in photos.
- Copyright rule: Photographs of Harley-Davidson products may not be reproduced without written consent.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Logos require a staging area based on the height of 'H' in H.O.G.; no elements may intrude into this space.

**Brand Voice:**

- Trademark grammar rules: Always use brand names in all capitals or initial capitals. Plural or possessive forms are not allowed.
- 'Harley-Davidson' is the proper brand name. It can only be shortened to 'Harley' or 'H-D' after it has been introduced properly.
- Use consistent hyphenation in 'Harley-Davidson,' always including the dash.

**Notes:**

- The company has strict guidelines on how dealers can use Harley-Davidson trademarks. Unauthorized reproduction or use for merchandise requires approval.
- Internet use for dealers: Guidelines include usage rights for trademarks, domain naming rules, and promotion disclaimers.
