## Brand Guidelines

### 2015

**Logo Information:**

- The Swimming Canada emblem includes colors Pantone 185C and Pantone 2995C. The preferred full-color emblem should appear on a white background. It should not be used in greyscale applications. For greyscale applications, a black emblem must be used. For colored backgrounds, a contained emblem should be used. The preferred emblem can appear on Pantone 305C or textured light greys (less than 25% black), such as for merchandise.
- Partner logos may lock up with the Swimming Canada emblem. Partner logos must be scaled to visually balance with the emblem, and all partner lock-ups require Swimming Canada's approval.

**Color Information:**

- Pantone 185C: C:0, M:100, Y:75, K:0 | RGB: R:237, G:25, B:64 | HEX: #ed1940
- Pantone 639C: C:100, M:20, Y:10, K:0 | RGB: R:0, G:148, B:200 | HEX: #0094c8
- White: C:0, M:0, Y:0, K:0 | RGB: R:255, G:255, B:255 | HEX: #ffffff
- Pantone 305C: C:54, M:0, Y:6, K:0 | RGB: R:99, G:203, B:232 | HEX: #63cbe8
- Pantone 3945C: C:8, M:2, Y:100, K:0 | RGB: R:241, G:228, B:5 | HEX: #f1e405
- Pantone 2995C: C:90, M:5, Y:9, K:0 | RGB: R:0, G:170, B:216 | HEX: #00aad8

**Typography Information:**

- Primary Typeface: Ciutadella Bold. Used for headline and display copy. All text should appear in uppercase.
- Supporting Typeface: Helvetica Neue Regular. When Helvetica Neue is unavailable, Helvetica or Arial may be used for body copy.
- All text on the feature graphic must be set in Ciutadella Bold, uppercase. The height of the text must be 60% of the lane height.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Photography should be consistent and align with themes of being iconic, athletic, emotive, proud, and representing heritage. Photos should not be blended, composited, or collaged.
- Use documentary-style photography whenever possible to convey genuine and experiential moments of athletes. Staged photography should be candid, intimate, and authentic.
- Examples of thematic photography include emotive photos (e.g., celebration shots), iconic representations (e.g., clean, high-contrast backgrounds), athletic performance, and heritage moments.

**Spacing Layout:**

- The Swimming Canada feature graphic is based on a technical drawing of an Olympic swimming pool. It should always be cropped in application, including red lane dividers. Lanes usually run horizontally, and the number of lanes shown depends on the text or application.
- Text on feature graphics must align with the lanes. The Swimming Canada emblem must always appear at the top-right corner and occupy a dedicated branding lane that is equal in height to other lanes.
- When paired with a wordmark, the emblem and the wordmark should be treated as separate elements with proper spacing between them.

**Brand Voice:**

- The tone of voice should be progressive, inclusive, and inspiring. It should create excitement and motivation while maintaining authenticity. The tone reflects Canadian heritage and a humble pride.
- Correct terminology includes usage such as 'Canadian swim team', 'national swim team,' and specific event-related descriptions (e.g., 'Canadian Olympic swim team'). Avoid vague terms like 'World Team'.

**Notes:**

- Partner logos can pair with the Swimming Canada emblem, but all lock-ups must receive prior approvals. Logos should maintain visual balance with the emblem.
- Several sanctioned events and programs include the Canadian Swimming Championships, Age Group Championships, Team Canada Trials, Speedo Eastern Open, and Swim-a-Thon.
- High Performance Centres provide environments for athletes with access to an integrated support team.
