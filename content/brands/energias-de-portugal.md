**Imagery Photography:**

- Imagery principles emphasize human-centric photos. Key rules include everyday life portrayal and a strong sense of naturalness and energy. Black and white photography is permissible under strict principles.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Details of spacing and layout rules. Includes examples of media localization and spatial alignment principles.

- Specifies tone of language, use of 'we' in headlines, formality in communication, punctuation guidelines, and speech format variations for formal and informal environments. Rules for writing numbers and units consistent, with precise formats and suggested usage.

**Notes:**

### 2013

**Typography Information:**

- EDP primarily utilizes EDP Preon as its typeface, with Calibri as a digital alternative where format restrictions apply. Specific guidelines for presenting the EDP name in headers recommend using lowercase with heavier weights (Hair and Light) to create contrast between the EDP name and other text. Headers should favor Hair for the EDP name styled in Light.
- For digital communication (e.g., email or online platforms), the typeface of choice is Calibri.

**Imagery Photography:**

- EDP's imagery is tied to the brand, focusing on visual storytelling and a dynamic approach, expressing who they are and what they stand for. Photography, while used minimally, should align with the brand's graphic focus, remain respectful to brand attributes, and project simplicity.
- EDP maintains a rich library of icons representing abstract ideas, objects, products, animals, etc., to narrate the brand story appropriately across applications.
- Illustration within the EDP brand focuses on playful, imaginative, and aspirational visual narratives that maintain simple graphic standards, resulting in whimsical yet informative and convincing brand representations.

**Brand Voice:**

- The EDP brand voice serves as an extension of the brand's visual identity and emphasizes being knowledgeable, personable, pragmatic, conscientious, clear, and open. It supports the identity by fostering educational engagements, human-centric conversations, sustainable practices, and transparent communication.

**Notes:**

- A glossary was included in the guidelines, defining key terms like 'Logomark,' 'Shadow,' 'Monogram,' and 'PMS' (Pantone Matching System). EDP Preon is mentioned as the primary typeface complemented by Calibri as a secondary digital font.
