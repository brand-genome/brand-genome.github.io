## Brand Guidelines

### 2016

**Logo Information:**

- The CMO Council logo consists of a custom typeface logotype and a red circle bug with white letterforms. It should be used consistently across printed and on-screen applications without alteration or recreation. Proportions and positions should not be changed.
- The clear space around the logo is determined by the height of the 'C' and the spacing between the two 'Cs'. No other graphics or text should violate this clear space.
- The tagline should be used with the CMO Council signature whenever possible. Avenir Book font is used for the tagline, and it is always positioned under the logotype and aligned left. When the logo size is 3/4 inch or smaller, use the logo without the tagline for readability.
- Color variations of the logo include full color (Pantone 186), 4-color CMYK, RGB for electronic media, 1-color positive and negative for limited-color jobs, and black and white. Negative colors should only be used sparingly.
- Background control rules specify that the preferred background is white. Tints of 10-30% for other colors are acceptable, and for dark backgrounds above 70%, a negative logo should be used. The logo should provide sufficient contrast against photographic or solid color backgrounds.
- Incorrect logo usages include altering colors, converting to grayscale, distorting proportions, repositioning elements, enlarging/reducing portions, using only parts of the logo, or separating the bug from the logotype.

**Color Information:**

- Main colors include CMOC Red (Pantone 186 C, Hex #e51a2e, RGB 229,26,46; CMYK 4,100,91,1), CMOC Black (Hex #000000, RGB 0,0,0), CMOC Dark Grey (Pantone 447 C, Hex #333333), Medium Grey, and Light Grey.
- Regional colors are defined for different CMO Council regions: Africa (Hex #7f4471), Europe (Hex #142571), Latin America (Hex #ffa828), Asia Pacific (Hex #ff4b32), India (Hex #f9651b), and Middle East (Hex #00c773).

**Typography Information:**

- Gotham is the primary font family for brand collateral and print materials. Gotham Bold is used for main headlines, Gotham Thin for body text, and Gotham Medium for secondary headings.
- Open Sans is the primary font for the CMO Council website. Open Sans Semibold is used for headlines and buttons, Regular for subheads, and Light for body copy and captions.
- Calibri is recommended as a replacement font for office documents like Microsoft Word and PowerPoint, enabling editing accessibility across systems.

**Imagery Photography:**

- Imagery should be bold, clever, practical (e.g., enhancing contrast for readability), and modern to reflect the marketing industry.
- Photography style should be clean, in-focus, modern, and depict business leaders collaborating in diverse global settings. Composition and effects such as shading for text readability may be applied.

**Spacing Layout:**

- Grid specifications, clear space rules, and alignment of elements including the logo adhere to layout standards. Clear space for the logo is based on the height of the 'C' (see logo clear space details).

**Brand Voice:**

- The CMO Council's tone of voice emphasizes authority, credibility, strategic knowledge sharing, and senior leadership perspectives within marketing.

**Notes:**

- The CMO Council bug as a graphic element is uniquely used on official stationery. Its separate use outside of these predefined contexts is not allowed.
- Icons used on the website and in leadership content should maintain consistent proportions, stroke weights, and balance between positive and negative space.
- PowerPoint templates utilize Calibri fonts and brand color schemes, limiting mixing of photography and flat illustrations within the same presentation.
